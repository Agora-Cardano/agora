## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to maintain Oura, an open-source Cardano developer and infrastructure tool used for processing blockchain events, indexing, monitoring, analytics, and real-time event routing. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this maps most directly to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, specifically A.3 Developer Experience, which includes incentivizing maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure in line with open-source best practices.

Although the proposal has technical infrastructure characteristics, it does not directly target Pillar 1 focus areas such as L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or protocol-level threat recovery. Its main strategic function is reducing developer friction and sustaining open-source tooling that builders depend on, which is explicitly covered by Pillar 2’s Developer Experience area.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Oura is “a Rust-native pipeline tool that connects to Cardano nodes, monitors blockchain events, and routes matching patterns to pluggable outputs for processing.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the maintenance work includes “dependency updates, Cardano protocol compatibility, performance improvements, bug fixing and documentation,” along with issue triage, pull request review, and community-driven enhancements.

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly aligns itself to Pillar 2 through “A.3 (Developer Experience), specifically the open-source incentives area,” citing maintenance of “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure.”

### Evidence 4

The proposal argues that Oura lets “developers and infrastructure operators focus on their application logic rather than building custom blockchain data pipelines.”

### Evidence 5

The proposal frames its expected value as maintaining “real-time event infrastructure” used by “indexers, monitoring systems, analytics platforms, and alerting infrastructure” to process Cardano blockchain events.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is the strongest fit because the Cardano Vision 2030 framework places open-source maintenance of Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure under A.3 Developer Experience within Pillar 2. The proposal’s primary deliverable is not a new L1 protocol feature, scalability upgrade, security mechanism, governance tool, treasury mechanism, or community growth program. It is sustained maintenance of an open-source developer tool that reduces friction for builders and downstream infrastructure operators.

Infrastructure & Research could appear plausible because Oura is technical infrastructure. However, the Vision 2030 Pillar 1 definition is narrower and focused on protocol scalability, interoperability, L2 integration, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and resilience mechanisms. Oura maintenance does not directly deliver those outcomes. Therefore, Pillar 1 should not be assigned as Primary or Secondary under the provided rules.

### Additional Notes

No Secondary Pillar is assigned. The proposal has incidental benefits for ecosystem reliability and developer community collaboration, but these are not substantial enough to support a second Vision 2030 pillar under the classification rules.
