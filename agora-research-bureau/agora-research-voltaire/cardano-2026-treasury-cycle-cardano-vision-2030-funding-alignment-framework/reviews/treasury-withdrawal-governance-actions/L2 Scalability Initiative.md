## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to fund Cardano Layer 2 infrastructure: shared L2-agnostic data availability work, Hydra production hardening, and Midgard’s optimistic rollup path to mainnet. This maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on making “L2 solutions first-class so high-volume activity can move off L1 and settle back,” producing “high-frequency, low-latency transactions with L1 security.” 

The proposal also has substantial direct alignment with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, because it explicitly targets DeFi, AI agent micropayments, gaming, and consumer payments, and includes Hydra DeFi reference implementations and support for live adopters. However, those adoption outcomes depend on the funded technical infrastructure being delivered first. The proposal exists primarily to build and harden scaling infrastructure, not to run a market adoption, UX, enterprise procurement, or user acquisition program.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “delivers three complementary workstreams: shared L2-agnostic infrastructure,” “production hardening of Hydra,” and “the mainnet launch of Midgard,” identifying the funded work as Cardano L2 infrastructure delivery. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal’s roadmap is organized around “Workstream 1 L2-Agnostic Infrastructure,” “Workstream 2 Hydra: Production Hardening + Turnkey Solutions,” and “Workstream 3 Midgard: Optimistic Rollup to Mainnet.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the L2-agnostic data availability solution “benefits all current and future Cardano L2s” and is intended to prevent fragmentation by solving “technical bottlenecks once for the whole ecosystem rather than once per project.” 

### Evidence 4

The proposal directly connects the work to scaling requirements, stating that Hydra targets “sub-1-second finality” and near-zero fees, while Midgard targets “10,000+ TPS per instance with 1 to 2 second finality.” 

### Evidence 5

The secondary Adoption & Utility alignment is supported by the proposal’s claim that high-performance verticals such as “DeFi, AI agent micropayments, gaming, and consumer payments” currently exclude Cardano due to finality, fee, developer burden, and throughput constraints, and that L2s would remove these disqualifiers.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s funded deliverables are primarily technical infrastructure: data availability architecture, Hydra performance and operational improvements, developer tooling, reference implementations, and Midgard mainnet readiness.

This directly matches the Cardano Vision 2030 description of Pillar 1, which includes scalability, interoperability, L2 integration, and infrastructure that enables high-volume activity to move off L1 while settling back to Cardano. 

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal directly targets use cases listed or closely related to Vision 2030 adoption priorities, including DeFi, payments, and high-volume application categories. The proposal also includes support for specific application builders and DeFi reference implementations. However, these are downstream or enabled outcomes of the infrastructure work, not the core funded purpose.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as the Secondary Pillar because, although the proposal discusses L1 settlement fees, sequencer revenue, treasury sustainability, and distributed stewardship, those elements are not the dominant funded work package. The proposal’s direct deliverables are not primarily treasury management, SPO incentives, tokenomics design, or long-term financial stewardship mechanisms as described in Pillar 5.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably be argued across multiple pillars. It contains strong Primary alignment with Infrastructure & Research, substantial Secondary alignment with Adoption & Utility, and some explicit but less central claims related to Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience. The proposal also contains multiple major technical workstreams, even though they are unified by a common L2 scaling purpose.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own pillar table marks Pillars 1, 2, 4, and 5 as “Yes: Fully.” For classification purposes, that self-assessment should not control the outcome. The funded substance is primarily L2 infrastructure and scalability delivery; adoption and sustainability are important intended effects, but they do not replace the infrastructure-first nature of the proposal.
