## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to create a long-term open-source maintenance and sustainment architecture for Cardano’s SDKs, tooling, protocol libraries, indexers, shared infrastructure, and maintainer ecosystem. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the **Developer Experience** focus area, which includes open-source incentives for maintaining “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure” in line with open-source best practices.

A substantial secondary alignment exists with **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, because WP3 and WP5 create contributor pipelines, mentorship structures, Summer of Code programs, hackathons, and pathways for new contributors to become maintainers. These are direct ecosystem talent-development activities, but they serve the broader open-source maintenance architecture rather than defining the proposal’s primary purpose. 

Pillar 1 and Pillar 5 are plausible but weaker primary classifications. The proposal uses infrastructure, resilience, and sustainability language, but its concrete deliverables are not primarily L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, ZK, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, managed treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention, or SPO incentive reform.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “establishes a community-governed, data-driven open source sustainment architecture,” including a Maintenance Fund, Maintainer Development program, CodeForUs bounty program, and Ecosystem Activation Reserve. This supports the conclusion that the main purpose is open-source sustainment and developer infrastructure maintenance. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the d(OSPO) and OMF are intended to provide “a neutral operational layer responsible for coordinating, sustaining, and stewarding Cardano’s open source infrastructure as a long term public good.” This directly supports classification under the Vision 2030 Developer Experience / open-source maintenance focus area. 

### Evidence 3

WP2 is described as the “core sustainment program,” deploying 6,000,000 ADA to provide long-term funding for “Cardano's highest-risk open source infrastructure: protocol libraries, wallet SDKs, developer tooling, indexers, and shared CI infrastructure.” This is the strongest evidence for Primary Pillar 2 because Vision 2030 explicitly places maintenance of SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure under Adoption & Utility’s Developer Experience section. 

### Evidence 4

WP3 establishes “structured mentor–mentee pipelines within the Cardano ecosystem,” compensates experienced maintainers as mentors, and builds “the next generation of contributors.” This supports Secondary Pillar 4 because it develops ecosystem talent and practical contributor capacity. 

### Evidence 5

WP5 funds Cardano Summer of Code, targeted hackathons, and developer activation events that recruit new contributors and direct them toward Cardano infrastructure needs. This reinforces the secondary alignment with Community & Ecosystem Growth, but remains subordinate to the proposal’s open-source sustainment purpose.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the Cardano Vision 2030 framework places **Developer Experience** inside Pillar 2, including the maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure. The proposal’s largest and most central workstream, WP2, is explicitly about funding and sustaining high-risk open-source dependencies, developer tooling, wallet SDKs, indexers, protocol libraries, and shared CI infrastructure.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was selected as the Secondary Pillar because WP3 and WP5 contain substantial contributor-development objectives, including mentorship, contributor ladders, on-chain attestations, Summer of Code, hackathons, and activation programs. These are direct talent pipeline and ecosystem growth mechanisms.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily deliver L1 protocol improvements, throughput increases, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, full-node client diversity, or protocol-level threat recovery systems. Its infrastructure impact is mainly through open-source maintenance and developer tooling sustainment.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as Primary or Secondary because the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus areas are specifically centered on treasury/tokenomics, multi-asset treasury, managed treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO role diversification, and decentralization targets. The proposal’s “sustainability” language refers mainly to open-source maintainer continuity, dependency health, bus-factor reduction, and contributor pipelines. That is real sustainability, but not the specific Pillar 5 sustainability defined in the reference framework. 

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes: open-source maintenance funding, operational governance infrastructure, maintainer development, bounty-based technical delivery, and contributor activation. It also self-identifies with Pillars 1, 4, and 5, while the Vision 2030 reference framework places the strongest matching activity under Pillar 2 Developer Experience. This creates a reasonable classification ambiguity even though Pillar 2 remains the strongest formal fit.

### Additional Notes

The proposal should not be classified based on its own stated pillar labels alone. Under the provided Cardano Vision 2030 framework, the most exact match is the Pillar 2 Developer Experience focus area on open-source incentives and maintenance of Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure.
