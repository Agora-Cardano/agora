## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to upgrade Cardano’s presence at TOKEN2049 Singapore from a baseline sponsorship to a higher-visibility “Title” sponsorship. Its main deliverables are event visibility, a larger booth, a mainstage keynote, media introductions, branding, builder showcase space, and ecosystem project exposure. That maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on global engagement, localized adoption, and proactively demonstrating ecosystem value. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal includes explicit targets for lead generation, wallet sign-ups, partnership initiations, media coverage, and positioning Cardano’s DeFi, RWA, payments, and enterprise applications before institutional and builder audiences. However, Adoption & Utility is not the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not directly build, deploy, or improve high-value vertical applications, UX, compliance patterns, or developer tooling. It funds premium exposure intended to stimulate adoption downstream.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the top-up unlocks “the ‘Title’ Sponsorship tier,” upgrading Cardano’s presence “from a 108 sqm booth to a massive 144 sqm footprint” and securing a “Mainstage Keynote.” This supports Pillar 4 because the funded work is primarily public presence, visibility, and global engagement. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the upgrade provides “a 15-minute mainstage keynote, premium media access,” “expanded branding via lanyards,” “a high-traffic engagement point,” and “a larger booth footprint,” all intended to “enhance Cardano’s visibility and reach across the event.” This directly matches the Vision 2030 Pillar 4 emphasis on demonstrating ecosystem value and global market engagement.  

### Evidence 3

The proposal’s stated motivation is to give Cardano builders “a professional, high-traffic platform to demonstrate their innovations,” including event tickets and dedicated showcase space. This aligns with Pillar 4’s focus on ecosystem growth, talent/community development, and practical exposure for builders. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal states that the primary objective is to convert “premium visibility, specifically the mainstage keynote and media introductions, into measurable ecosystem growth, strategic partnerships, and institutional relationships.” This supports Pillar 4 as the primary classification, while also supporting Adoption & Utility as a secondary pillar. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes KPI targets such as 2,530 booth contacts, 26 new strategic collaborations, 260 Cardano wallet sign-ups, 260 Cardano Academy subscribers, 26 major media outlets, and 5 facilitated media introductions. These are adoption-oriented outcomes, but they remain downstream from an event marketing and ecosystem visibility activation.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** is selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to increase Cardano’s visibility, builder exposure, media presence, and market engagement at a major global event. The Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 4 around “Driving global engagement through a market-centric approach,” “Proactively Demonstrate Ecosystem Value,” and “Localized adoption,” including regional engagement in areas such as East Asia. The Singapore event activation fits that pillar more directly than any other.

**Adoption & Utility** is selected only as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal contains measurable adoption-oriented targets: wallet sign-ups, strategic collaborations, lead generation, and exposure for high-value verticals such as DeFi, RWA, payments, and enterprise applications. However, those outcomes are mediated through marketing exposure and networking rather than direct delivery of utility, applications, infrastructure, or user experience improvements.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected because the proposal does not fund protocol work, L1/L2 integration, cryptography, client diversity, security infrastructure, or technical research.

**Governance** is not selected because the governance and treasury administration sections describe compliance and fund-handling mechanics, not the strategic purpose of the proposal.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not selected because the proposal does not directly address treasury management, tokenomics, SPO incentives, L2-to-L1 value retention, or long-term operational integrity of network infrastructure.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably be argued under both **Community & Ecosystem** and **Adoption & Utility**. The dominant funded activity is global ecosystem visibility and builder showcase exposure, which supports Pillar 4. However, the proposal includes explicit adoption KPIs and institutional partnership objectives that create a meaningful secondary alignment with Pillar 2.

### Additional Notes

The top-up nature of the proposal matters for interpreting scope: this is not a standalone ecosystem strategy, but an incremental upgrade to an underlying “Platinum” sponsorship proposal. Its classification is therefore based on the incremental benefits funded by this proposal: mainstage keynote, larger booth, media access, event-wide branding, and related engagement assets.
