## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund continuous maintenance of Cardano’s core technical platform, including bug fixing, security reviews, CI/CD operations, disaster recovery, monitoring, performance optimization, QA, node release management, incident response, and component maintenance across major protocol and developer-facing infrastructure.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, which focuses on keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” and includes protocol improvements, security, resilience, client diversity, observability, and recovery. The proposal exists primarily to preserve and improve the technical foundation of Cardano.

A secondary alignment with **Pillar 5: Sustainability & Resilience** is present because the proposal directly supports long-term operational integrity, uptime, disaster recovery, and reliable infrastructure health. However, Pillar 5 is not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily focus on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or network economics. Its dominant activity is core infrastructure maintenance.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it funds “continuous core maintenance and operational support from Q3 2026 through Q1 2027,” describing maintenance as the foundation underneath every Cardano feature, upgrade, and innovation. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal lists nine functional areas including “bug fixing and security reviews, CI/CD and infrastructure operations,” “mainnet and mempool monitoring,” “systematic performance analysis and optimization,” “quality assurance and release sign-off,” and “full node release management with L1/L2/L3 incident response.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the work ensures “network uptime, swift incident resolution, and proactive security hardening,” while also delivering the Cardano Blueprint and performance optimization to support alternative node implementations and release quality. 

### Evidence 4

The expected outcomes include “Continuous Network Availability,” “Reliably High Transaction Throughput,” “Predictable Release Cycles,” “Hardened Security Posture,” and “Accelerated Incident Resolution,” all of which directly support Cardano’s infrastructure reliability and resilience. 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 1 as keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” with focus areas including L1 protocol improvements, security and resilience, client diversity, threat detection, recovery, and observability.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the strongest Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main deliverables are technical maintenance, release engineering, performance work, security review, QA, infrastructure operations, node/component maintenance, and documentation for implementation-independent specifications.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal directly supports operational integrity, uptime, incident response, disaster recovery, and long-term infrastructure health. However, it is secondary because the proposal’s concrete funded activities are infrastructure maintenance activities rather than treasury, tokenomics, SPO incentive, or economic sustainability mechanisms.

**Adoption & Utility** is not selected as Primary or Secondary. The proposal may indirectly enable adoption by improving reliability, performance, UX, and developer confidence, but these are downstream effects of maintaining infrastructure. It does not primarily fund user acquisition, DeFi, RWA, payments, supply chain use cases, enterprise adoption, or direct application-layer utility.

**Governance** is not selected as Primary or Secondary. The proposal mentions guardrails scripts, CC identity scripts, CLI/API support, and technical foundations for DRep participation, but governance is not the main strategic purpose. Those elements are components of broader technical maintenance.

**Community & Ecosystem** is not selected because the proposal does not primarily fund talent acquisition, education, localized engagement, marketing, ecosystem storytelling, or community growth programs.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal has a clearly dominant Infrastructure & Research purpose, but it also contains substantial direct evidence for Sustainability & Resilience through uptime, disaster recovery, incident response, operational integrity, and long-term infrastructure health. The proposal could therefore reasonably be considered across more than one pillar, even though Infrastructure & Research is the strongest primary classification.

### Additional Notes

The proposal itself describes Pillar 1 as “Yes, Fully” and Pillar 5 as “Yes, Fully.” For this classification, Pillar 1 is treated as primary because it best explains the funded work itself, while Pillar 5 describes a major strategic outcome of that work.
