## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund twelve months of engineering work to bring Dingo, a Go implementation of a Cardano node, to mainnet block-production readiness. Its main deliverables include Ouroboros Praos consensus completion, Dijkstra hard fork support, CIP-0164 Linear Leios implementation, operational hardening, storage scalability, conformance testing, security audit completion, and mainnet readiness.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, especially the Vision 2030 focus areas around **client diversity**, **security and resilience**, **L1 protocol improvements**, and **scalability**. The Vision framework explicitly identifies alternative full-node clients as a target for reducing single-client risk.

Although the proposal mentions developer accessibility through Go and open-source availability, those benefits are not the main strategic purpose. The funded work is not primarily an adoption campaign, user-facing application, education program, or market-expansion initiative. The proposal exists chiefly to strengthen Cardano’s core infrastructure and improve network resilience through an alternative block-producing node implementation.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Blink Labs is requesting funding for “twelve months of full-time engineering on Dingo, our Go Cardano node” and that the funding would get Dingo “the rest of the way to mainnet block-production readiness,” including consensus completion, Dijkstra hard fork support, Leios work, a security audit, and operational hardening. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal directly frames the work around node diversity: “Cardano runs on a single node implementation for block production,” and “Node diversity changes that,” with Dingo intended to bring a Go implementation alongside other clients. 

### Evidence 3

The scope of work includes “Full Ouroboros Praos consensus,” “Hard fork combinator,” “Genesis bootstrap,” “Stable ChainSync from genesis to tip,” and “CIP-0164 Linear Leios prototype,” all of which are core infrastructure and protocol implementation deliverables. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s final expected outcomes include: “Dingo produces blocks on mainnet,” “SPOs have a real alternative block producer,” “Dijkstra works from day one,” “Leios exists in Go alongside the Haskell reference,” and “The audit report is public.” 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places “alternative full node clients” under operational resilience and states that having “≥ 2 live, spec-conformant” clients reduces “single-client risk.” It also places client diversity under Pillar 1’s Security & Resilience focus area.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the strongest classification because the proposal is fundamentally about building, hardening, auditing, and preparing an alternative Cardano node implementation for production block generation.

Other pillars are less suitable:

**Adoption & Utility** is not the primary fit. The proposal may indirectly support adoption by improving scalability through Leios and making Cardano infrastructure more accessible to Go developers, but its primary deliverables are not focused on DeFi, RWA, payments, supply chain, consumer UX, enterprise adoption, or direct on-chain demand generation.

**Governance** is not the primary fit. The proposal mentions governance transactions and treasury reporting mechanisms, but those are implementation or accountability details, not the proposal’s strategic purpose.

**Community & Ecosystem** is not the primary fit. Open-source availability and developer accessibility may benefit the ecosystem, but the proposal is not mainly an education, talent acquisition, regional engagement, or ecosystem marketing initiative.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not the primary fit under this framework because Pillar 5 focuses on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, and long-term financial/network economics. While Dingo may improve technical resilience, the Vision 2030 document places client diversity and alternative full-node implementations under Infrastructure & Research, not Pillar 5.

### Additional Notes

No Secondary Pillar is assigned because the proposal’s other benefits are either indirect or subordinate to the main infrastructure objective. The proposal’s existence is best justified by Cardano’s need for a production-ready alternative node implementation that improves client diversity, protocol resilience, scalability implementation, and operational robustness.
