## AI Rationale

Gerolamo’s core purpose is to deliver a production-ready, fully-validating Cardano light node that runs in the browser. That maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, especially the framework’s focus on security, resilience, client diversity, light-client implementations, and reduced single-client risk.

A secondary alignment with **Adoption & Utility** is present because the proposal includes dApp and wallet integrations, developer-facing APIs, browser extension distribution, and adoption targets for wallets/dApps. However, those adoption outcomes depend on the successful delivery of the underlying node infrastructure. The proposal exists primarily to build technical infrastructure, not to directly drive a specific adoption vertical such as DeFi, RWA, supply chain, or payments.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “funds Gerolamo, the first production-ready Cardano node that runs in the browser,” describing it as “a real, fully-validating node” rather than a research demo or stripped-down SPV client. 

### Evidence 2

The key delivery is defined as “a production-ready light node that runs in the browser,” with the proposal scoped around Gerolamo as a browser-based Cardano light node. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly aligns Gerolamo with the Cardano 2030 KPI for “Alternative full node clients,” stating that Gerolamo contributes as a second spec-conformant client implementation. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal maps itself to “Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research Excellence,” specifically “I.2 Security & Resilience → Client Diversity,” by supporting additional full-node and light-client implementations to improve decentralization and reduce single-client risk. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal also includes adoption-facing deliverables, including browser-based node integrations with at least three wallets/dApps, a public browser extension, a messaging API, demo dApp pages, and integration documentation for wallet and dApp developers.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main deliverable is a Cardano node/client implementation, not an application, user acquisition program, commercial adoption campaign, governance process, community initiative, or treasury-management mechanism.

The strongest Vision 2030 mapping is to Pillar 1’s security and resilience focus, particularly client diversity and light-client/full-node implementation support. The work also has research and infrastructure implications for compact validation, trustless bridges, L2 verifiers, dApp-side verification, and resilient client architecture.

**Adoption & Utility** was assigned as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes direct developer and application-facing outcomes: dApp integration, wallet integration, browser extension distribution, APIs, and documentation. However, these are downstream uses of the technical infrastructure. They do not override the fact that the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to build foundational Cardano infrastructure.

### Additional Notes

The classification has some formal ambiguity because the Cardano Vision 2030 framework places developer experience, open-source tooling, SDKs, frameworks, and compatibility under Pillar 2. However, in this case, the proposal’s dominant object is a node/client implementation and client-diversity infrastructure, so Pillar 1 remains the strongest primary classification.
