## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to strengthen Cardano’s smart-contract infrastructure through Plutus language improvements, formal specification, conformance testing, security review, compiler work, and tooling upgrades. That maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, which the Cardano 2030 framework defines around keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” and includes protocol improvements, L2 integration, core ZK capabilities, client diversity, and threat detection/recovery. 

The proposal also has substantial direct alignment with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the Developer Experience area, because one of its three workstreams is explicitly focused on improving compiler usability, setup friction, error reporting, and developer onboarding. The Vision 2030 framework places maintenance of SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure, education, migration, and compatibility under Developer Experience. 

Still, the proposal is not primarily an adoption campaign, market-entry initiative, enterprise use-case deployment, or user-acquisition program. The main deliverables are technical infrastructure: Plutus primitives, UPLC changes, conformance testing, Agda formalization, security audits, compiler architecture, and hard-fork-ready code.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “strengthens Cardano’s smart contract platform across three critical and closely connected areas: language capabilities, formal correctness, and developer experience.” This directly frames the work as improvement of Cardano’s technical smart-contract infrastructure. 

### Evidence 2

Workstream 1 expands Plutus capabilities and primitives by extending the UPLC execution model, adding built-in casing on Data, implementing CIP-0156 multiIndexArray, implementing additional BuiltinValue functions, and exploring laziness and memoization in UPLC. These are core protocol/platform engineering deliverables, not downstream adoption activities. 

### Evidence 3

Workstream 2 delivers “a property-based conformance testing framework,” “systematic security and correctness audit,” and “formalization of programmatic built-in types and functions in the Plutus metatheory,” supporting correctness, node diversity, and security. This strongly supports Pillar 1’s security, resilience, and client-diversity objectives. 

### Evidence 4

Workstream 3 improves developer experience through “a new compiler and optimizer architecture,” clearer source-level error messages, reduced boilerplate, and simpler setup without Nix or manual dependency installation. This directly supports Pillar 2’s Developer Experience component. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s budget allocates 86% of funding to Development, reinforcing that the core funded activity is technical delivery rather than ecosystem marketing, governance, community growth, or treasury management.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s strongest and most concrete deliverables are technical improvements to Cardano’s smart-contract platform: Plutus execution efficiency, UPLC primitives, compiler architecture, conformance testing, Agda formalization, security review, and production-ready code.

**Adoption & Utility** is relevant as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal contains a substantial developer-experience workstream, and the Cardano 2030 framework explicitly includes Developer Experience under Pillar 2. However, the adoption effect is mainly enabled through better infrastructure and tooling rather than direct deployment of high-value verticals, user-facing applications, enterprise integrations, payments, RWA, supply-chain adoption, or market expansion.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily focus on treasury management, multi-asset treasury strategy, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or decentralization targets. Its resilience contributions are mostly technical security and correctness improvements, which are better captured under Pillar 1.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily focus on youth engagement, talent acquisition, global engagement, market adoption, localized partnerships, or public narrative-building. Any ecosystem growth effect is downstream of better infrastructure and developer tooling.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily fund DRep incentives, voting tools, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, or treasury season mechanisms.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic emphases. Workstreams 1 and 2 are strongly infrastructure/research-oriented, while Workstream 3 directly supports Developer Experience under Adoption & Utility. The Primary Pillar remains Infrastructure & Research, but the proposal has enough direct secondary alignment to justify review rather than treating the classification as mechanically obvious.

### Additional Notes

The Secondary Pillar is assigned because Developer Experience is not merely an indirect benefit here. It is a named workstream with concrete deliverables, including compiler improvements, source-level error messages, reduced boilerplate, broader GHC support, and simpler setup.
