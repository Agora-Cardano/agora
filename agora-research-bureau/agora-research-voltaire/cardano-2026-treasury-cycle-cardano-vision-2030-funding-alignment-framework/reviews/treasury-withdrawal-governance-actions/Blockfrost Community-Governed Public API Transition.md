## AI Rationale

The proposal’s strongest primary alignment is with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the **Developer Experience** section of the Cardano 2030 Strategic Framework. The proposal is centered on keeping Blockfrost available as a free, reliable, hosted API that lets developers interact with Cardano without running their own node or infrastructure. The Vision 2030 framework places maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure under Developer Experience, with the expected enhancement of a “sustainable builder ecosystem.” That is the cleanest fit here, because the proposal’s stated purpose is to reduce developer friction, preserve the free tier, and keep applications and wallets able to build on Cardano. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is also justified because the proposal directly funds operational continuity, public-good stewardship, infrastructure cost transparency, long-term sustainability planning, and decentralized operation through Icebreakers or vendor-backed infrastructure. This is more than a generic positive externality: the proposal includes an 18-month transition, ongoing operations, a not-for-profit ownership structure, public dashboards, quarterly reporting, a 99% monthly uptime SLA, and a sustainability consultation. However, Pillar 5 is not selected as Primary because the proposal’s main strategic function remains preserving developer access and application usability rather than changing treasury management, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO economics, or base network tokenomics.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Blockfrost “lets developers read from and interact with the Cardano blockchain through a simple, hosted REST interface — without having to run and maintain their own node,” and that it “removes the operational complexity of building on Cardano, so teams can focus on their applications instead of their infrastructure.” This directly supports classification under Developer Experience within Pillar 2. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says Blockfrost is the “number-one hosted platform developers use in their projects,” with reported adoption rising from 39.3% in 2022 to 71.5% in 2025. This supports the view that the funded work primarily preserves a widely used developer access layer, rather than being mainly a governance, education, or protocol research initiative. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal states that “90% of all Cardano traffic served through the Blockfrost API comes from the free tier” and that a fully commercial path would “harm adoption” by forcing price increases or removal of the free tier. This supports Pillar 2 because the proposal’s central adoption mechanism is lowering infrastructure cost and friction for builders. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal commits to transitioning Blockfrost into a “fully not-for-profit, community-governed public API of Cardano,” with source code, trademarks, domains, and associated assets transferred to a governing not-for-profit entity. This supports the Secondary Pillar because it directly addresses long-term stewardship and operational integrity of ecosystem infrastructure. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes sustained operations with “a minimum 99% monthly uptime SLA,” public dashboard verification, quarterly reports, infrastructure cost transparency, and a sustainability consultation to define the post-18-month model. This provides direct evidence for secondary alignment with Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s core purpose is to maintain a free, reliable access layer that developers, wallets, and applications use to interact with Cardano. The Cardano 2030 framework explicitly includes Developer Experience under Pillar 2, including maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure to support a sustainable builder ecosystem. Blockfrost’s role as a hosted API for developers maps more directly to that Developer Experience category than to protocol infrastructure or treasury economics.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected as Primary because the Vision 2030 examples under Pillar 1 focus on L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and threat detection/recovery. The proposal supports access-layer reliability, but it does not primarily deliver protocol-level scalability, interoperability, cryptographic research, full-node diversity, or base-layer security improvements.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is selected as Secondary because the proposal materially funds long-term operational continuity and governance of a critical public API, including a not-for-profit structure, public dashboards, quarterly reporting, possible revenue models, and decentralized operator participation. It is not selected as Primary because these mechanisms serve the broader purpose of keeping developer access free and reliable.

**Governance** is not selected because the board election and not-for-profit governance are governance mechanisms for Blockfrost itself, not direct improvements to Cardano’s DRep, SPO, Constitutional Committee, voting, delegation, or treasury-season governance systems.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** is not selected because the proposal may help builders indirectly, but it does not primarily fund education, youth engagement, hands-on training programs, localized adoption, market strategy, or public narrative work as described in Pillar 4.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple Primary Pillars. It combines developer access, public-good infrastructure maintenance, operational reliability, decentralized operation, and long-term sustainability planning. The strongest single fit is Pillar 2, but Pillar 5 is substantial enough to require explicit review.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own pillar table labels Pillar 1, Pillar 2, and Pillar 5 as aligned. For CVTFAF classification, however, the decisive question is not whether several pillars benefit, but which pillar best justifies the proposal’s existence. On that basis, Pillar 2 is the strongest primary classification, with Pillar 5 as a direct and substantial secondary alignment.
