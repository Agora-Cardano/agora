## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to improve Cardano’s developer experience by reducing onboarding friction, consolidating documentation, improving tooling, creating reusable smart contract libraries, and helping developers move from interest to working projects faster. The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places “Developer Experience” directly under **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, including open-source incentives, education and migration, and compatibility with common tooling. That makes Adoption & Utility the strongest primary classification

A secondary alignment with **Community & Ecosystem** is justified because the proposal also includes developer outreach, hackathons, community collaboration, learning paths, and efforts to expand the skilled developer base. However, those activities serve the main DevX and adoption objective rather than replacing it as the proposal’s core strategic purpose. 

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “funds a focused six-month program to streamline Cardano's developer tooling, documentation, and onboarding experience,” with a target of “a 30%+ improvement in developer growth rate.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposed solution includes “creating bounties” for DevX tooling and libraries, “creating a starter CLI,” compiling an “OpenZeppelin-like library of ready-to-use smart contracts,” and “unifying and improving the documentation and educational resources” under the Developer Portal. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly frames the expected effect as reducing developer friction so that more builders can create DApps, which then drives users, transactions, and protocol revenue. It states that “more builders lead to more DApps, which attract more users and generate more protocol revenue.” 

### Evidence 4

The Vision 2030 framework places Developer Experience within Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, including “open-source incentives,” “education & migration,” and “compatibility” as focus areas. This directly matches the proposal’s tooling, documentation, onboarding, migration, and compatibility objectives. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal also claims Pillar 4 alignment by stating that it “cultivates a skilled developer base through structured learning paths, hands-on hackathons, and continuous community engagement,” supporting the Secondary Pillar classification.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s core work is developer experience: tooling, documentation, onboarding, reusable contracts, developer migration, and compatibility. These are not merely adjacent to Pillar 2; they map directly to the Developer Experience section of the Vision 2030 framework.

Community & Ecosystem is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes substantial developer community-building elements, including outreach, hackathons, collaboration, and structured learning. However, those elements primarily support the broader DevX adoption pathway. They are not the main reason the proposal exists.

Infrastructure & Research was not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily focus on L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat detection/recovery. Some deliverables involve technical tools and libraries, but under the Vision 2030 taxonomy, developer tooling and open-source SDK/framework support are classified under Adoption & Utility’s Developer Experience category.

### Manual Review Reason

Not required.

### Additional Notes

The uploaded proposal text does not provide a separate formal title. “Cardano Developer Experience / DevX Initiative” is used as a descriptive proposal name based on the proposal’s stated objectives and repeated DevX framing.
