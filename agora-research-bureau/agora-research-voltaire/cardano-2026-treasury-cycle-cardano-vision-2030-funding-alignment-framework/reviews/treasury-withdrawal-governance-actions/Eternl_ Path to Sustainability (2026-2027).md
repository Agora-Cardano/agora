## AI Rationale

The proposal aligns primarily with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** because its central purpose is to maintain and improve Eternl as a user-facing gateway through which Cardano users conduct payments, staking, governance participation, and DApp interactions. Its principal deliverables include cross-platform wallet development, backend infrastructure, transaction construction, hardware-wallet integration, DApp interoperability, interface improvements, support for emerging Cardano standards, and continued availability of the wallet across web, browser, Android, and iOS platforms. 

This purpose maps most directly to the **Experience** component of Pillar 2, which seeks to reduce adoption friction and provide accessible, reliable user experiences. It also supports Cardano utility by enabling protocol and smart-contract interactions, payments, DeFi access, multisignature transactions, and other wallet-based activities. The Cardano Vision 2030 framework specifically associates Adoption & Utility with superior user experience, usable Cardano-native applications, payments, DeFi, and reducing friction for users and enterprises. 

**Governance** qualifies as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal contains specific governance-related deliverables rather than merely claiming a general governance benefit. These include DRep dashboards, proposal browsers, enhanced in-wallet voting, visibility into DRep voting behavior, and planned functionality for creating governance proposals directly through Eternl. These activities directly correspond to Pillar 3’s governance-accessibility-tools focus area. However, governance functionality remains one component within a broader wallet maintenance and development program and does not define the proposal’s overall strategic purpose.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Eternl is a non-custodial Cardano light wallet used for “payments, staking, governance, and DApp interaction” and that the funding would support 12 months of operations, maintenance, and improvements across web, browser extension, Android, and iOS. This establishes the proposal as primarily a user-facing Cardano access and utility initiative. 

### Evidence 2

The proposed frontend work includes cross-platform releases, transaction-building improvements, multisignature wallet functionality, hardware-wallet support, Bluetooth connectivity, new Cardano Improvement Proposal implementations, and “dApp interoperability across the broader Cardano dApp ecosystem.” These deliverables directly enable users to access and use Cardano applications and protocol functionality. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal describes Eternl Core as a substantial rewrite intended to provide faster transaction building, greater flexibility for future features, a more responsive interface, and improved loading times. These are direct improvements to the wallet user experience and therefore align with Pillar 2’s objective of lowering adoption friction through superior business and consumer experiences. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal includes a dedicated governance-tooling workstream covering “DRep dashboards and proposal browsers,” enhanced in-wallet voting, and the planned ability to create governance proposals directly in Eternl. These deliverables directly support the Pillar 3 objective of maintaining tools through which users can delegate, vote, and submit or view governance actions. 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 2 as driving non-speculative utility through high-value applications and superior user experience, while its Experience focus area seeks to hide technical complexity and lower adoption friction. Eternl’s continued wallet availability, interface development, device compatibility, DApp connectivity, and transaction functionality are more directly connected to this purpose than to protocol-level infrastructure or ecosystem-wide financial resilience.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected over **Infrastructure & Research** because the proposal is principally concerned with maintaining and developing an application-level wallet and its supporting services, rather than improving Cardano’s consensus, ledger, networking, Layer 1 throughput, interoperability infrastructure, cryptographic foundations, node-client diversity, or protocol-level resilience.

Although the proposal operates backend servers, Cardano nodes, indexers, databases, application servers, monitoring systems, and transaction-submission endpoints, these components exist primarily to operate Eternl and provide wallet functionality to its users. They are supporting infrastructure for a consumer-facing application rather than shared base-layer infrastructure or independent protocol research.

**Governance** was not selected as the Primary Pillar because governance functionality represents only one subset of the broader development scope. Most funded activities concern general wallet operations, frontend and backend maintenance, DApp interaction, transaction construction, hardware-wallet support, cross-platform distribution, user support, and continued access to Cardano.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily focus on education programs, talent acquisition, student experience, localized market engagement, public ecosystem narratives, or structured contributor-funding routes. Developer coordination and user support are operational elements of the wallet service rather than the proposal’s main strategic objective.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the Vision 2030 pillar is specifically structured around multi-asset treasury management, treasury investment returns, Layer 2-to-Layer 1 value retention, SPO role diversification, and block-production decentralization. The proposal’s paid-plan strategy and potential treasury repayment concern the financial sustainability of Eternl itself, not the network-level financial stewardship and tokenomics mechanisms defined under Pillar 5. 

### Additional Notes

Confidence is assessed as Medium because the proposal contains a direct and meaningful governance-tooling component that supports a second Vision 2030 pillar. Nevertheless, the wallet’s overall operations, maintenance, user experience, transaction functionality, DApp access, and cross-platform availability provide a sufficiently dominant basis for selecting Adoption & Utility as the Primary Pillar.
