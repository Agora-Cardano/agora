## AI Rationale

The proposal is best classified under **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research** because its strongest single strategic justification is the development of **Gerolamo**, a production-ready TypeScript Cardano light node designed to improve client diversity, trust-minimized access, relay diversity, and network resilience. This maps directly to the Vision 2030 focus on additional full-node and light-client implementations, reducing single-client risk, and improving decentralization.  

However, the classification is ambiguous because the proposal also contains substantial **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** work through **Pebble**, developer tooling, TypeScript libraries, SDK/framework maintenance, and developer onboarding. The Vision 2030 framework explicitly places core SDK/framework maintenance, developer migration, and compatibility under Adoption & Utility.

Because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes, **Manual Review Required = Yes**.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal identifies three key deliveries: “maintenance for an upcoming hard fork,” “a production-ready light node (Gerolamo),” and “a production-ready, imperative and efficient, programming language for smart contracts (pebble).”

### Evidence 2

Gerolamo is described as enabling dApps to run lightweight nodes directly in the browser, giving them “direct, trustless access to the Cardano ledger” and reducing reliance on centralized indexers or third-party APIs.

### Evidence 3

The proposal states that SPOs can use Gerolamo as an additional relay node and that “a diverse node implementation landscape strengthens the network's resilience” by reducing the risk of bugs in a single implementation.

### Evidence 4

The proposal explicitly maps Gerolamo to the Cardano 2030 goal of “supporting additional full-node and light-client implementations” to achieve “better decentralization” and “reduce single-client risk.”

### Evidence 5

The proposal also states that Pebble supports developer migration by offering familiar TypeScript/JavaScript-style syntax and that ongoing TypeScript tooling maintenance supports core Cardano libraries used across the developer ecosystem.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because Gerolamo directly advances a core Vision 2030 infrastructure objective: additional node/light-client implementations and reduced single-client risk. The work also includes hard-fork maintenance and protocol-adjacent TypeScript infrastructure, reinforcing the infrastructure classification.

**Adoption & Utility** is assigned as the Secondary Pillar because Pebble and the TypeScript tooling maintenance directly support Vision 2030’s Developer Experience section, especially open-source incentives, developer migration, and compatibility. This is not merely an indirect adoption benefit; it is a substantial and separately scoped part of the proposal.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as Primary or Secondary. Although the proposal uses language about long-term sustainability and resilience, Vision 2030 Pillar 5 is focused on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, and network economic resilience. The proposal does not directly fund treasury management, tokenomics reform, or SPO incentive structures.

**Governance** was not selected. The escrow, oversight board, delegation policy, and milestone controls are administrative and accountability mechanisms for this funding request, not the strategic purpose of the proposal.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected. Developer onboarding is present, but under the Vision 2030 taxonomy this is more directly covered by Pillar 2’s Developer Experience section rather than Pillar 4’s talent, education, market engagement, or localized ecosystem growth focus areas.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes: Gerolamo aligns strongly with Infrastructure & Research, while Pebble and TypeScript tooling maintenance align strongly with Adoption & Utility through Developer Experience. The two pillars could reasonably compete for Primary classification depending on whether the reviewer prioritizes the largest technical infrastructure deliverable or the broader developer tooling/developer experience substance.

### Additional Notes

The Primary Pillar classification should be treated as a strategic judgment rather than a purely mechanical keyword match. The proposal itself explicitly claims alignment with both Pillar 1 and Pillar 2, and the Vision 2030 framework supports both readings.
