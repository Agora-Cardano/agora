## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to improve Cardano’s application delivery capacity by giving builders a unified platform to build, verify, launch, operate, and scale applications. Its intended users are Cardano builders, new JVM developers, and teams building mission-critical applications. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, especially **A.3 Developer Experience**, because the proposal focuses on open-source tooling, developer migration, compatibility, reusable SDK/platform infrastructure, and reducing builder friction.

A substantial secondary alignment exists with **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, because the proposal includes explicit technical infrastructure deliverables: an application-focused L1 node, native L2 integration, formal verification, ledger conformance testing, production hardening, observability, and client-diversity-related work. However, these infrastructure components serve the broader application-platform objective rather than standing alone as base-layer protocol research or core network infrastructure. Therefore, Pillar 1 is secondary, not primary.

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes and could reasonably be classified either as Developer Experience under Adoption & Utility or as technical infrastructure under Infrastructure & Research.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Scalus extends from a development environment into “a full application platform: one coherent stack to build, verify, launch, and scale Cardano applications, with its own L1 node, native L2 integration, formal verification, and production-grade runtime capabilities.” This supports Adoption & Utility as the primary pillar because the core purpose is application delivery and builder enablement, while also supporting Infrastructure & Research as secondary due to L1 node, L2, and verification components. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies the core ecosystem bottleneck as application delivery: teams struggle to ship non-trivial applications because they must assemble smart contracts, formal verification, chain access, operations, and scaling themselves. This directly supports Pillar 2’s Developer Experience focus: reducing friction so more applications can reach production. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly maps Scalus to Pillar 2 Developer Experience through open-source incentives, education and migration, and compatibility: reusable tooling, starter kits, blueprints, ecosystem integrations, documentation, tutorials, training materials, and alignment with common Cardano off-chain tooling. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal also explicitly maps to Pillar 1 through L2 integration and client diversity: Hydrozoa state-channel integration, embedded L1 access, and further development of an application-focused Scala-based Cardano node implementation with conformance testing. 

### Evidence 5

The Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 2 Developer Experience as including open-source incentives for “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure,” education and migration for developers moving to Cardano/UTxO, and compatibility with common multi-chain tooling. It defines Pillar 1 as including L2 integration and client diversity, which explains the secondary alignment.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal is fundamentally about enabling more Cardano applications to be built and operated by reducing developer complexity. Its main claimed ecosystem impact is more serious teams shipping, more production applications, and downstream growth in transactions, users, and TVL.

Although the proposal includes substantial infrastructure components, those components are framed as part of an integrated application platform for builders. The L1 node, L2 integration, runtime, formal verification, and tooling are not presented primarily as protocol-level research or base-layer upgrades; they are presented as the infrastructure needed for application teams to build and operate serious products.

**Infrastructure & Research** was assigned as Secondary Pillar because several deliverables directly match Pillar 1 focus areas: L2 integration, client diversity, ledger conformance, application-focused node capabilities, observability, resilience, and formal verification. This is not merely an indirect benefit; it is a substantial part of the proposal scope.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as secondary because the training, documentation, outreach, Scalus Club, and mentorship elements support adoption of the platform, but they are not the main strategic purpose and do not outweigh the direct Developer Experience and Infrastructure workstreams. 

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily develop governance tooling, DRep participation mechanisms, voting safeguards, or treasury seasons.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal does not directly address multi-asset treasury design, managed treasury strategy, L2-to-L1 tokenomics, SPO incentive diversification, or decentralization targets under Pillar 5. References to public goods, reusable infrastructure, or future maintenance are not enough for Pillar 5 alignment under the provided framework.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes, including developer experience, L1 node infrastructure, L2 scaling integration, formal verification, documentation, training, and ecosystem outreach. The proposal could reasonably be classified as either Adoption & Utility or Infrastructure & Research depending on whether the reviewer prioritizes the official Vision 2030 Developer Experience taxonomy or the economic substance of the technical infrastructure work.

### Additional Notes

The classification is Medium confidence because Adoption & Utility is the strongest fit under the official Vision 2030 taxonomy, but the proposal has unusually strong Infrastructure & Research content for a developer-platform proposal.
