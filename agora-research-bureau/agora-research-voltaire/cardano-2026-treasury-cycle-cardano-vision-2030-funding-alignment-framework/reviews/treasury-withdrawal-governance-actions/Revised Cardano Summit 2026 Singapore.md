## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core funded activity is the delivery of a flagship Cardano Summit in Singapore. Its main strategic purpose is to expand Cardano’s reach, strengthen its presence in Asia, bring together builders, community members, developers, enterprises, institutions, regulators, and media, and demonstrate ecosystem value through a major public-facing event. That maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on global engagement, market-centric ecosystem growth, proactive demonstration of Cardano’s value, and localized adoption in high-impact regions such as East Asia. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal includes direct adoption-oriented objectives and KPIs: enterprise MQLs, strategic meetings, live demos, technical workshops, developer participation, hackathon submissions, and targeted engagement with enterprise, DeFi, stablecoin, RWA, AI, identity, and regulatory audiences. However, Adoption & Utility is not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not directly build, deploy, or maintain adoption infrastructure or real-world applications. It primarily funds an event designed to create visibility, relationships, and adoption pathways.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes the initiative as “a strategic high-impact Cardano Summit 2026” in Singapore and states that the Singapore edition is designed to “expand Cardano’s reach beyond its existing ecosystem” and “strengthen its position in Asia.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that the event will bring together “builders, enterprises, institutions and regulators” to “meaningfully meet, connect, build lasting partnerships,” which supports ecosystem growth and global engagement as the primary purpose. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the objective is not simply attendance, but converting audience concentration into “qualified relationships, enterprise conversations, ecosystem visibility, and measurable post-event outcomes.” This supports Pillar 4 as the event’s main purpose while also supporting Pillar 2 as a secondary adoption pathway. 

### Evidence 4

The KPI framework includes adoption and impact targets: “250 enterprise MQLs, 50 strategic meetings within 45 days post-event, 10 live demos/technical workshops, 200 developer participants, and 30 hackathon submissions/applications.” 

### Evidence 5

The proposed program separates **Ecosystem Day**, focused on community, builders, DReps, developers, workshops, hackathon finals, and ecosystem deep dives, from **Industry Day**, focused on enterprise, institutional, regulatory, tokenisation, stablecoins, interoperability, AI, digital identity, and targeted meetings.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** is selected as the Primary Pillar because the funded deliverable is a global ecosystem event, not a protocol upgrade, infrastructure project, governance mechanism, treasury-management system, or direct commercial adoption product. The Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 4 around global engagement, market-centric ecosystem growth, demonstrating ecosystem value, and localized adoption. The proposal’s Singapore location strategy, TOKEN2049 timing, media targets, regional positioning, ecosystem programming, builder activities, and institutional visibility objectives fit that pillar most directly. 

**Adoption & Utility** is a substantial Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes concrete adoption-oriented targets and targets high-value verticals such as DeFi, RWA/tokenisation, stablecoins, identity, enterprise deployment, and financial institutions. This aligns with Pillar 2’s focus on non-speculative utility and high-value verticals. 

**Governance** is not selected as a Secondary Pillar. The proposal includes governance sessions and DRep/community participation, but it does not directly fund governance tooling, DRep incentives, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, or treasury season mechanisms.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected. The proposal discusses infrastructure topics and showcases integrations, but it does not itself deliver L1 improvements, L2 integrations, bridges, ZK capabilities, client diversity, or threat recovery systems.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not selected. The proposal includes revenue targets and ring-fencing revenue for future Summit planning, but that is event financing continuity, not the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus on treasury tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or network infrastructure resilience.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal could reasonably be viewed as fitting multiple Primary Pillars. Its event format, regional market positioning, builder/community programming, and visibility goals point most strongly to **Community & Ecosystem**, while its enterprise lead-generation targets and adoption-focused agenda create substantial but secondary alignment with **Adoption & Utility**.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains multiple program components with different strategic emphases: ecosystem/community programming, developer and builder engagement, governance discussions, institutional adoption targeting, media/brand impact, and enterprise follow-up. This reinforces the need for manual review, even though the dominant classification remains Pillar 4.
