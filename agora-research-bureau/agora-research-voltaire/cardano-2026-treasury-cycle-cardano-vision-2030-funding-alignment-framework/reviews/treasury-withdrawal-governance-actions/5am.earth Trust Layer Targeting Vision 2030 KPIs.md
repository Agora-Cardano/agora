## AI Rationale

The proposal aligns primarily with **Adoption & Utility** because its core purpose is to deploy Cardano in concrete agricultural use cases at substantial real-world scale. It combines decentralized identity, verified farm and crop information, supply-chain traceability, compliance workflows, stablecoin payments, credit scoring, and lending infrastructure for farmers across India, Cambodia, and Kenya. 

This directly corresponds to the Vision 2030 focus on non-speculative utility through high-value verticals, particularly supply-chain provenance, payments, real-world assets, decentralized identity, and enterprise-ready trust infrastructure. The framework specifically identifies native assets and metadata for traceability, low-cost payments, and embedding decentralized identity into transactions and contracts as Pillar 2 strategies. 

The proposal includes measurable adoption outcomes rather than merely developing a general technical component: 500,000 registered farmers, 2 million funded-period on-chain transactions, $2 million in lending through Cardano stablecoin rails, and three operating application paths. These intended outcomes make real-world adoption and recurring on-chain utility the clearest justification for the proposal’s existence.

No Secondary Pillar is assigned. Although the proposal describes a reusable trust layer, foundation governance, ecosystem integrations, and a future self-sustaining revenue model, these elements either support the primary adoption programme or do not directly match the specific strategic mechanisms defined under the other Vision 2030 pillars.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal seeks to build an “open, Cardano-anchored trust layer for global agricultural supply chains,” combining self-sovereign identity with an on-chain satellite oracle providing verified farm and crop information. 

### Evidence 2

The funded programme targets “500,000 registered farmers” across India, Cambodia, and Kenya and operates three concrete application paths: AE certification, traceability and compliance, and finance and credit. 

### Evidence 3

The traceability work includes “EUDR-compliant supply chain workflows, schema-validated compliance records, and anchor-brand pilots” using verified farm-origin information from the trust layer. This directly matches the Vision 2030 supply-chain and provenance strategy of using metadata and native assets for traceability.  

### Evidence 4

The finance work delivers credit scoring based on verified agricultural data, Cardano-native stablecoin rails for loan disbursement, on-chain repayment monitoring, and general farmer payments and settlement. These are direct utility outcomes corresponding to the framework’s DeFi and payments focus areas. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal establishes funded-period targets of 500,000 registered farmers, 2 million on-chain transactions, and $2 million in lending disbursed through Cardano stablecoin rails. These targets directly contribute to the Vision 2030 adoption KPIs concerning monthly users, transaction activity, TVL, and protocol revenue.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected because the proposal’s main deliverables are operational applications for agricultural identity, supply-chain provenance, compliance, lending, and payments. The target users are farmers, Agri-Entrepreneurs, lenders, buyers, certifiers, and institutional partners, rather than protocol developers, governance participants, SPOs, or treasury managers.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the trust layer operates above Cardano’s base protocol and primarily integrates existing components. The proposal explicitly states that the relevant Cardano components are already operational and that “the work is integration and scaling, not greenfield development.” It does not primarily improve consensus, networking, L1 throughput, L2 integration, cross-chain standards, ZK infrastructure, client diversity, or protocol security.

**Governance** was not selected because the governance deliverables concern the internal operation, auditing, administration, and legal structure of the proposed Foundation. They do not directly advance DRep incentives, governance accessibility, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, or treasury seasons.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not assigned as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal’s references to enabling future Cardano applications are broad ecosystem effects arising from the trust layer. The funded deliverables do not substantially focus on talent acquisition, education, hands-on developer training, structured funding routes, public narrative development, or localized communication programmes.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal’s intention to become financially self-sustaining refers to the Foundation’s own future operating model. It does not directly implement the Vision 2030 mechanisms for a multi-asset treasury, active treasury management, L2-to-L1 value retention, diversified SPO roles, or stake-pool decentralization.

### Additional Notes

The proposal states that it contributes across all five Vision 2030 pillars. However, classification is based on the specific definitions contained in the reference framework rather than the proposal’s self-classification. Several claimed alignments concern project administration, indirect ecosystem effects, or organizational sustainability rather than direct delivery against the relevant pillar strategies.
