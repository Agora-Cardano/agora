## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to establish the legal, administrative, custody, and smart contract infrastructure needed to manage treasury-funded liquidity operations. That maps most directly to **Pillar 5: Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on financial stewardship, managed treasury activity, operational integrity, and strategic capital deployment. The framework defines this pillar around the long-term financial health and operational integrity of the network and specifically includes evolving the treasury beyond a passive ADA pool into a managed portfolio that strategically deploys capital into growth-focused investments. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal is explicitly framed as infrastructure required for the **Stablecoin DeFi Liquidity Budget**, and Vision 2030 identifies DeFi, secure institutional-grade liquidity onramps, and more usable Cardano-native DeFi as part of Pillar 2. However, this withdrawal does not itself deploy liquidity or directly onboard users. It funds the legal entity, fund-management structure, smart contract administration, and audit. So Adoption & Utility is substantial, but secondary.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the withdrawal “requests 500,000 ADA from the Cardano Treasury to establish the legal framework and smart contract infrastructure required for the Stablecoin DeFi Liquidity Budget.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal funds “establishment of a Cayman Islands Foundation Company as the legal vehicle for fund management,” which directly supports treasury-backed fund administration rather than a consumer-facing DeFi product.

### Evidence 3

The proposed Foundation Company would have “separate legal personality enabling the FC to hold assets, enter contracts, and act as legal counterparty for off-chain transactions,” reinforcing the financial stewardship and operational integrity function.

### Evidence 4

The Amaru contract is described as a mechanism to “receive the 500,000 ADA from this withdrawal and manage disbursements to service providers,” providing “transparent on-chain tracking of all payments” and “protection against single point of failure.”

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that “all liquidity tokens” will be held by a smart contract and that the structure includes tDAO oversight, committee multisig controls, audit provisions, monthly reporting, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the funded work is mainly about treasury fund management, legal structuring, asset custody, operational controls, auditability, and secure capital administration. These are closer to Pillar 5’s financial stewardship and managed treasury logic than to direct user adoption.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the funded infrastructure exists to enable the Stablecoin DeFi Liquidity Budget, and Vision 2030 explicitly includes DeFi liquidity under Adoption & Utility. Still, the immediate deliverables are not liquidity deployment, user acquisition, payments, RWA, or direct transaction growth. They are setup and control mechanisms for a treasury-funded DeFi liquidity program.

**Governance** was not selected as Primary because the proposal includes governance mechanisms for this specific fund, but it does not primarily develop Cardano-wide governance tooling, DRep incentives, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, or treasury season infrastructure as described under Pillar 3.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit more than one primary pillar. The strongest ambiguity is between **Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience**, due to treasury fund management and strategic capital deployment infrastructure, and **Adoption & Utility**, due to the proposal’s direct connection to stablecoin DeFi liquidity.

### Additional Notes

This classification treats the current withdrawal as a setup and administration proposal, not as the full liquidity deployment itself. If a later withdrawal directly deploys treasury funds into DeFi liquidity positions, that later action may warrant a stronger or primary classification under Adoption & Utility, depending on its specific mechanics and stated outcomes.
