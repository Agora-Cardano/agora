## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to improve Cardano’s developer experience by funding a production-ready smart-contract language, Pebble, and maintaining HLabs’ TypeScript tooling stack across protocol upgrades. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, these activities map most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the **A.3 Developer Experience** focus area.

The strongest alignment is not direct end-user adoption, DeFi usage, RWA, payments, or enterprise deployment. It is developer enablement: SDK/framework maintenance, developer onboarding, compatibility, tooling, documentation, and migration support. Pillar 2 includes open-source incentives for core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure, education and migration for EVM/account-based developers, and compatibility with common multi-chain tooling. 

Infrastructure & Research is plausible at a surface level because the proposal funds foundational tooling and hard-fork maintenance. However, the Vision 2030 framework places this type of developer tooling and open-source SDK/framework maintenance under **Adoption & Utility / Developer Experience**, not under Pillar 1’s protocol-level infrastructure categories such as L1 improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat recovery.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it funds “two complementary engineering tracks”: **Pebble**, described as “the first production-ready imperative smart-contract language on Cardano,” and **tooling maintenance**, described as keeping HLabs’ TypeScript stack synchronized with protocol upgrades. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal directly frames Pebble as a developer onboarding tool, stating that its TypeScript-shaped syntax gives Cardano access to a large developer audience and creates “a direct on-ramp for Solidity developers.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the tooling maintenance work supports Cardano libraries used directly or transitively by Mesh, Lucid Evolution, Midgard, wallets, indexers, and dApp backends, emphasizing continuity for the TypeScript ecosystem through protocol upgrades. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s own Cardano 2030 alignment section identifies **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically **A.3 Developer Experience**, citing open-source incentives and education/migration as the relevant framework areas. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal defines adoption-related developer metrics such as developer onboarding, documentation completeness, and tutorial completion, which are leading indicators for developer experience rather than direct protocol infrastructure or treasury sustainability.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected because the proposal most directly supports **Developer Experience**, which the Cardano Vision 2030 framework places inside Pillar 2. The proposal funds a smart-contract language, developer tooling, documentation, tutorials, migration support, IDE/LSP features, CLI improvements, and maintenance of TypeScript libraries used by application developers.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily fund L1 protocol improvements, consensus, ledger/networking upgrades, L2 interoperability, cross-chain standards, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat recovery. Its “infrastructure” is developer-facing tooling infrastructure, which the Vision 2030 framework explicitly classifies under Developer Experience in Pillar 2.

### Additional Notes

No Secondary Pillar is assigned. The hard-fork maintenance work is substantial, but in the provided framework it is best interpreted as part of open-source SDK/framework/tooling maintenance under **A.3 Developer Experience**, rather than as a separate direct alignment with Pillar 1.
