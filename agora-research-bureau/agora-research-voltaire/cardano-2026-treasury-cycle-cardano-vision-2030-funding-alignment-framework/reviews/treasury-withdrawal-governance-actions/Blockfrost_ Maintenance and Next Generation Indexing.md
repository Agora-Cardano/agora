## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to fund technical data infrastructure for Cardano: decentralized slice indexing, a Mandoline indexer, a DSL, a provider API layer, Bitcoin/Cardano multi-chain indexing support, security audits, and continued operation of Blockfrost’s free-tier API infrastructure.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, which focuses on keeping Cardano secure, fast, interoperable, scalable, and technically capable of hosting more economic activity. The proposal is not primarily funding end-user adoption, a specific application vertical, governance participation, marketing, education, or community engagement. Its core purpose is infrastructure capacity, decentralization of data-serving operations, reduced indexing cost, API access, and continuity of critical developer-facing infrastructure.

A **Secondary Pillar: Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is justified on a narrower item-level basis through Pillar 5’s focus on SPO role diversification. The proposal creates a structure for SPOs and node operators to participate in decentralized data-serving infrastructure by indexing and serving selected slices of chain data, rather than requiring every operator to maintain a full-chain index. This aligns with the Pillar 5 objective of expanding SPO roles beyond L1 block production into broader infrastructure functions that strengthen ecosystem resilience. However, this remains secondary because the proposal’s dominant purpose is still the design, development, and operation of technical indexing infrastructure under Pillar 1.

However, manual review is required because the proposal itself claims full alignment with multiple pillars, including Adoption & Utility and Sustainability & Resilience, while also bundling two major components: a new decentralized indexing architecture and an operational subsidy for an existing free-tier service. That creates classification ambiguity.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that “Project Cayley introduces decentralized slice indexing — allowing SPOs and node operators to choose which portions of the chain they want to index and serve,” and says this “lowers the barrier to participation, reduces infrastructure costs across the ecosystem, and keeps data-serving infrastructure decentralized as the chain scales.” This directly supports classification under Infrastructure & Research.

### Evidence 2

The proposal funds “Project Cayley ($1,000,000) to build the next-generation indexing architecture” and an “operational subsidy ($900,000) to maintain the free-tier infrastructure.” The split confirms that the core funded work is infrastructure construction and infrastructure operation, not direct adoption programming.

### Evidence 3

The deliverables are technical infrastructure milestones: “Indexer first release,” “Implement DSL 0.1 and the Mandoline indexer with initial Cardano support,” “Provider API Layer,” “Expose indexed Cardano data via a GraphQL API,” and “Bitcoin Indexer.” These deliverables align most directly with technical infrastructure, scalability support, and interoperability.

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s budget allocation assigns 42.1% to “Product, Engineering and R&D,” 10.5% to “Security Audits,” and 47.4% to “Ops & Infrastructure.” This reinforces that the primary economic substance of the proposal is infrastructure development, security, and operational service continuity.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal’s main funded outputs are decentralized indexing infrastructure, API infrastructure, multi-chain data access architecture, security audits, and continued operation of data-serving infrastructure. These directly support Cardano’s technical capacity to scale, remain interoperable, and support economic activity.

The Cardano 2030 Strategic Framework defines Pillar 1 as focused on keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable so it can host more economic activity,” including scalability, interoperability, security, and resilience. Project Cayley’s slice indexing, provider API layer, multi-chain architecture, and operational reliability commitments fit this pillar more directly than any other.

**Adoption & Utility** is not selected as the Primary Pillar. The proposal does mention developers, applications, free access, reduced integration time, and support for active users. However, these are downstream effects of infrastructure availability. The proposal does not primarily fund a high-value vertical such as DeFi, RWA, supply chain, or payments, nor does it primarily fund consumer UX or enterprise adoption programs. Its adoption impact depends on builders using the infrastructure later, which makes adoption relevant but not dominant.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** is also not selected as the Primary Pillar. The proposal may support SPOs, node operators, developers, and ecosystem projects, but it is not mainly an education, talent acquisition, outreach, market adoption, or community engagement initiative.

**Governance** is not selected because the proposal does not directly target DRep participation, voting systems, governance accessibility tools, treasury seasons, constitutional processes, or governance safeguards.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is selected as a Secondary Pillar on a narrower item-level basis, specifically through Pillar 5’s focus on diversified SPO roles. The proposal creates a structure for SPOs and node operators to participate in decentralized data-serving infrastructure by indexing and serving selected portions of chain data, reducing the need for every operator to maintain a full-chain index. This supports broader and more resilient infrastructure by expanding the role of SPOs beyond L1 block production into additional infrastructure services. However, this alignment remains secondary because the proposal’s dominant purpose is still the design, development, and operation of technical indexing infrastructure under Pillar 1.


### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple pillars and contains multiple major workstreams with related but distinct strategic purposes:

1. Project Cayley: new decentralized indexing architecture, most directly aligned with Infrastructure & Research.
2. Blockfrost free-tier subsidy: operational continuity and reliability of existing ecosystem-critical infrastructure, supporting Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience.

The proposal also self-identifies alignment with Pillars 1, 2, 4, and 5, which increases classification ambiguity. Under the classification rules, however, Pillar 1 remains the best primary classification because it most directly explains why the proposal exists.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s Adoption & Utility claims should be treated as secondary effects rather than a separate secondary pillar under this framework. While free API access and lower infrastructure costs may help developers and applications, the proposal’s funded work is not direct adoption activity. It is technical enablement.
