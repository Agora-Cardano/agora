## AI Rationale

The proposal is best classified primarily under **Adoption & Utility** because its strongest recurring strategic purpose is to reduce user and developer friction, make Cardano-native DeFi cheaper and easier to use, enable fee abstraction through Babel Fees, and remove the requirement for users to hold ADA before transacting. These objectives map directly to Pillar 2’s focus on DeFi, payments, lower adoption friction, and improved user experience. The Vision 2030 framework defines Adoption & Utility around widespread non-speculative utility, high-value verticals such as DeFi and payments, and reducing friction through improved user experience. 

A substantial secondary alignment exists with **Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** because Workstream 2 directly targets Multi-Asset Treasury design, including stable-value treasury holdings, treasury diversification, and improved funding resilience. This maps directly to Pillar 5’s focus on financial stewardship, tokenomics, and multi-asset treasury design. 

However, the classification requires manual review because the proposal bundles multiple major workstreams with distinct strategic purposes: ledger/account infrastructure, treasury diversification, and adoption/fee abstraction.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it delivers “three coordinated initiatives” intended to “expand Cardano's economic models, strengthen long-term Treasury resilience, and remove one of the biggest barriers to mainstream adoption, the requirement to hold ADA before you can transact.” 

### Evidence 2

For Babel Fees, the proposal says users will be able to “pay transaction fees in any Cardano native asset,” eliminating the need to hold ADA and allowing users to transact “without first acquiring ADA.” This directly supports lower onboarding friction and broader user adoption. 

### Evidence 3

For CIP-159, the proposal says account address enhancement enables wallet micro-fees, makes DeFi applications cheaper for end users, reduces batcher and aggregator costs, and creates patterns needed for L2 reserves. These are primarily utility, DeFi usability, and ecosystem application-enablement outcomes. 

### Evidence 4

For Multi-Asset Treasury, the proposal says enabling the Treasury to hold stablecoins and other native assets would improve budget predictability, protect the funding process from ADA price volatility, and strengthen the Treasury’s long-term financial position. This directly supports Pillar 5. 

### Evidence 5

The deliverables are split across three workstreams: CIP-159 Account Address Enhancement, CPS-23 Cardano Multi-Asset Treasury, and Babel Fees. This confirms that the proposal contains multiple major strategic purposes rather than a single clean alignment category.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main ecosystem-facing justification is to make Cardano easier and cheaper to use, especially for DeFi users, wallet operators, DApp developers, bridged BTC users, and stablecoin users. Babel Fees directly removes the ADA acquisition requirement, while CIP-159 supports cheaper DeFi interactions and wallet revenue models. These outcomes most directly match Pillar 2’s focus on DeFi, payments, user experience, and practical utility.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was selected as the Secondary Pillar because the Multi-Asset Treasury workstream is direct, substantial, and explicitly tied to treasury diversification and stable-value funding. This is not merely an indirect benefit; it is a named workstream with dedicated deliverables.

**Infrastructure & Research** is also plausible because CIP-159 involves ledger-level account address enhancements and support for L2 reserve patterns. However, it was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal frames those technical changes mainly as enablers for adoption, DeFi usability, wallet economics, L2 usability, and treasury functionality, rather than as standalone protocol scalability, security, client diversity, or research objectives.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes and could reasonably fit more than one Primary Pillar. Adoption & Utility, Infrastructure & Research, and Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience all have direct support in the proposal text. The selected Primary Pillar reflects the strongest overall strategic justification, but the classification is not clean.

### Additional Notes

The provided proposal text does not present a separate formal title before the abstract. The proposal name used above is derived from the three named workstreams in the proposal text.
