## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is core Cardano infrastructure delivery. Its main work is the mainnet deployment of Peras, including production cryptography, KillSwitch, mainnet readiness, support and maintenance, plus conformance testing for Peras and Leios. This maps directly to Pillar 1 because the Vision 2030 framework defines Infrastructure & Research around keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” including L1 protocol improvements that improve consensus, ledger, networking, throughput, and finality.

A Secondary Pillar of Sustainability & Resilience is justified, but only as secondary, because History Expiry is one of the three major work packages and is explicitly aimed at reducing SPO storage costs and avoiding economically prohibitive full-history requirements that could threaten decentralization. That is a direct operational-resilience objective, not merely a vague ecosystem afterglow.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal requests funding for “Tweag by Modus Create's delivery of 3 work packages across core infrastructure areas for the Cardano ecosystem over 2026–2027.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that its “primary focus is the mainnet deployment of Peras,” described as faster finality of approximately 2 minutes compared with approximately 12 minutes today, alongside “resilience, scalability, developer tooling, and network observability.” 

### Evidence 3

The listed work packages are Peras v1, History Expiry, and Conformance Testing, including “production cryptography, KillSwitch, mainnet readiness,” “partial-history nodes to reduce SPO storage costs,” and the “CTC framework extended for Peras and Leios.” 

### Evidence 4

The Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 1 as keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” and specifically includes L1 protocol improvements to “raise throughput and shorten finality.” 

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that without History Expiry, full-history requirements would become “economically prohibitive, threatening decentralization,” which supports secondary alignment with Sustainability & Resilience.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Infrastructure & Research is the strongest Primary Pillar because the proposal’s central deliverables are protocol-level infrastructure work: Peras mainnet readiness, protocol cryptography, conformance testing, and support for Peras and Leios. These directly match Vision 2030’s Pillar 1 focus on L1 protocol improvements, scalability, finality, security, and resilience.

Sustainability & Resilience is not selected as Primary because only one of the three core work packages, History Expiry, directly targets SPO cost reduction and decentralization risk. That is meaningful enough for a Secondary Pillar, but it does not outweigh the proposal’s broader and dominant L1 infrastructure purpose.

Adoption & Utility is not selected because the proposal may indirectly enable future transaction volume, TVL, or developer activity, but it does not primarily fund DeFi, RWA, payments, enterprise UX, user adoption, or market-facing utility. Governance is also not selected because Intersect administration, milestone assurance, and treasury contract handling are implementation controls rather than the proposal’s strategic purpose. Community & Ecosystem is not selected because public demos and status updates are engagement practices, not the core funded outcome.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own strategic alignment statement identifies Pillar 1 for all three packages and Pillar 5 for History Expiry.
