## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to fund a structured research and applied engineering programme that translates foundational research into implementation-ready Cardano capabilities. Its strongest alignment is with **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, because the core deliverables are papers, technical reports, CPSs, prototypes, and CIPs focused on protocol security, scalability, post-quantum readiness, L2/ZK infrastructure, consensus improvements, node security, interoperability, and formal methods.

The proposal also has substantial direct alignment with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, especially through developer experience, user experience, fee abstraction, light clients, bridges, atomic swaps, liquidity, and enterprise-facing interoperability. However, these adoption outcomes are mostly enabled through infrastructure and research outputs rather than being the proposal’s primary purpose. The Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 1 around keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” including L1 improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and threat detection/recovery. The proposal’s main workstreams map directly to those areas.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that **“Cardano Vision 2026 delivers a structured, end-to-end innovation pipeline that translates foundational research into deployable system capabilities and measurable impact,”** combining research, applied engineering, and product alignment. This directly supports classification under Infrastructure & Research because the proposal is organized around advancing technical capabilities rather than primarily funding market activation or governance operations. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies **scalability** and **post-quantum security** as two of its three anchor priorities, including **Leios/Peras integration, L2 execution, ZK-enabled scaling, sharding research, and migration of quantum-resistant cryptographic primitives**. These areas directly match Vision 2030 Pillar 1 focus areas such as L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, core ZK capabilities, and post-quantum readiness.

### Evidence 3

The proposal says CV26 will deliver **“38 papers and technical reports,” “8 Cardano Problem Statements,” “12 prototypes,” and “5 Cardano Improvement Proposals,”** including outputs for identity services, ZK-enabled L2 scalability, and post-quantum security. This evidence strongly supports Infrastructure & Research as the primary pillar, since the core funded outputs are research artefacts and implementation-readiness mechanisms.

### Evidence 4

WP1 and WP2 are explicitly technical infrastructure workstreams: **“Trust, Security & Reliability Infrastructure”** covers consensus assurance, post-quantum cryptography, Ouroboros analysis, and node security, while **“Scalability & Execution Layer”** covers fee markets, data availability, sharding, Byzantine-resilient networking, L2, and ZK execution infrastructure. These workstreams are the clearest basis for the primary classification.

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes direct Adoption & Utility objectives through WP3 and WP4: **“Developer Platform & User Experience”** aims to reduce friction for builders and users, while **“Applications, Adoption & Liquidity”** aims to enable cross-chain liquidity, bridges, atomic swaps, and enterprise use cases. This justifies a Secondary Pillar of Adoption & Utility, but not the Primary Pillar, because these adoption outcomes depend on the underlying research and infrastructure programme.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal is fundamentally a technical R&D and implementation-readiness programme. Its largest and most central objectives concern protocol evolution, consensus, cryptography, scalability, ZK infrastructure, L2 settlement, node security, formal methods, interoperability, and implementation pathways through CPSs and CIPs.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes substantial work on developer experience, user experience, light clients, fee abstraction, bridges, atomic swaps, liquidity, and enterprise-oriented capabilities. However, those outcomes are downstream from technical research and infrastructure development.

**Governance** and **Sustainability & Resilience** also appear in meaningful workstreams, especially WP5 Economic Systems & Incentives and WP6 Governance, Identity & Social Infrastructure. However, they do not outweigh the proposal’s broader technical R&D purpose and are not stronger secondary candidates than Adoption & Utility.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes, including Infrastructure & Research, Adoption & Utility, Governance, and Sustainability & Resilience. The Primary Pillar is still identifiable, but the proposal is broad enough to warrant manual review.

### Additional Notes

The proposal itself includes internal pillar mappings across several Vision 2030 pillars. These mappings are useful context, but the classification here prioritizes the proposal’s overall strategic purpose and deliverable structure rather than assigning the Primary Pillar based on every individual work package.
