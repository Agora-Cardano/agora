## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is production deployment of a real-world supply-chain traceability system on Cardano for Vietnam’s durian sector. This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, because the Vision 2030 framework explicitly includes supply chain/provenance, decentralized identity, enterprise/security compliance, and friction-reducing user experience within Adoption & Utility. 

The proposal is not mainly protocol research, core infrastructure, governance tooling, or treasury management. Its central deliverables are real-world usage targets: 12,000 hectares, 180M fruits identified per season, 24,000 Cardano DIDs, 12,000 CIP-68 Farm NFTs, and approximately 1M–1.2M annual on-chain events at full scale. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Community & Ecosystem** is justified because the proposal contains substantial localized adoption objectives in Vietnam, including farmer onboarding across 46 communes, institutional relationships, and a provincial-scale agricultural deployment. This directly supports the Vision 2030 emphasis on localized adoption and regional growth where blockchain solves real frictions.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

“OriLife Alliance proposes 2,400,000 ADA from the Cardano Treasury to deploy tamper-evident traceability across 12,000 hectares of durian in Đắk Lắk, Vietnam.” 

### Evidence 2

Treasury-funded deliverables include “180M durian fruits/season identified via Bio-ID,” “24,000 PhoenixKey DIDs on Cardano,” “12,000 CIP-68 Dynamic Farm NFTs on Mainnet,” and “~1M–1.2M on-chain events/year at full scale.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal states that Vietnam’s Circular 11 makes traceability connection a legal obligation for food production establishments by December 1, 2026, and that OriLife is intended to provide the production-grade traceability layer and bridge into the national system. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal states that farmers across 46 communes have been onboarded over two harvest seasons, and that DDC holds field operations and government relations with trust relationships across those communes. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes an MIT-licensed SDK and describes future expansion to coffee, melon, and aquaculture, but these are extensions of the adoption deployment rather than the primary purpose of the proposal.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to generate non-speculative, real-world Cardano usage through agricultural traceability, identity, compliance, and supply-chain provenance. This is the cleanest fit against the Vision 2030 framework, which places supply chain/provenance, DID/SSI, enterprise compliance, invisible UX, and developer experience under Pillar 2. 

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as Primary because the proposal repeatedly frames the work as production deployment rather than research. Technical components such as Bio-ID, VeData, PhoenixKey, LampNet, Midnight fallback design, and CIP-68 NFTs support the application deployment, but they do not make the proposal primarily about Cardano protocol infrastructure or core research.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as Secondary despite references to locked ADA, user-paid transaction fees, post-grant revenue streams, and a treasury fee mechanism. Those are financial return mechanics and sustainability claims attached to the deployment. They do not directly implement the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus areas: multi-asset treasury, managed treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention at the ecosystem-tokenomics level, SPO role diversification, or decentralization targets. 

**Community & Ecosystem** was selected as Secondary because the proposal goes beyond generic ecosystem benefit: it targets a specific high-impact regional market, includes local institutional support, farmer onboarding, and provincial-scale deployment in Vietnam. That directly maps to localized adoption and regional growth where blockchain solves real frictions.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own statement that it aligns with “Ecosystem Growth & Real-world Adoption” is consistent with the classification, but it is not treated as determinative. The classification is based on the Cardano Vision 2030 pillar definitions and the proposal’s stated objectives and deliverables.
