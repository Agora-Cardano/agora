## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund a high-visibility Cardano presence at TOKEN2049 Singapore, centered on builder showcases, conference access, branding, networking, content capture, and regional ecosystem exposure. That maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on global engagement, demonstrating ecosystem value, localized adoption, and building confidence with partners and users. The Vision framework’s Pillar 4 specifically includes cultivating a public narrative around Cardano’s strengths and targeting high-impact regions such as East Asia through localized partnerships and communication strategies. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is also justified because the proposal contains direct adoption-oriented objectives: lead generation, partnership initiation, wallet sign-ups, post-event on-chain transaction tracking, and positioning Cardano use cases in DeFi, RWA, payments, and enterprise applications. However, Adoption & Utility is not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily fund deployment of DeFi, RWA, payment, UX, identity, or enterprise-security infrastructure. Its main funded activity is ecosystem visibility and market engagement, not direct product utility delivery.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal is fundamentally an event activation and ecosystem visibility initiative. Its main deliverables are a sponsorship package, booth, builder stage, conference tickets, branding, networking access, content capture, and community/project showcase opportunities. These map directly to Pillar 4’s focus on global engagement, public narrative, regional adoption, and demonstrating Cardano’s ecosystem value.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes measurable adoption targets and explicitly aims to convert event engagement into wallet sign-ups, strategic partnerships, user acquisition, and on-chain activity. It also targets DeFi, RWA, payments, and enterprise applications, which are named in the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility pillar. Still, those are intended downstream outcomes of the event platform rather than the central funded deliverable.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected because the proposal does not fund protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain infrastructure, ZK capabilities, client diversity, or threat recovery.

**Governance** is not selected because governance-related content appears mainly in budget administration, legal compliance, and treasury withdrawal mechanics. Those are procedural requirements, not the proposal’s strategic purpose.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not selected because the proposal does not directly advance treasury tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, decentralization targets, or long-term operational integrity of network infrastructure.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably be read as fitting both **Community & Ecosystem** and **Adoption & Utility**. The Primary Pillar is Community & Ecosystem because the core funded mechanism is global event presence and builder visibility, while the Adoption & Utility case is substantial but mainly expressed through intended conversion outcomes.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own Vision 2030 alignment section claims both Pillar 2 and Pillar 4. This classification accepts that both are relevant, but assigns Pillar 4 as primary because the sponsorship’s direct deliverables are overwhelmingly event, visibility, builder showcase, and regional ecosystem engagement activities.
