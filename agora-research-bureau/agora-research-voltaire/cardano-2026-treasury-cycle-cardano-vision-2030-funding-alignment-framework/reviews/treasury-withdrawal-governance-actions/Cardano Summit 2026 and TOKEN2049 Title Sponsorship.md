## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund a large-scale Cardano event strategy in Singapore, combining the Cardano Summit 2026 with a TOKEN2049 Title Sponsorship. Its main deliverables are event organization, sponsorship, exhibition presence, media exposure, ecosystem showcases, builder presentations, networking, lead generation, and global brand positioning.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, because the Vision 2030 framework defines that pillar around global engagement, demonstrating ecosystem value, localized market adoption, talent/community development, and external perception. The proposal is fundamentally about taking Cardano into a high-traffic global venue, showcasing ecosystem projects, engaging Southeast Asia, and improving Cardano’s market-facing presence.

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal contains substantial adoption-oriented objectives and KPIs, including enterprise/institutional audiences, marketing qualified leads, strategic meetings, live demos, wallet sign-ups, strategic collaborations, and tracks focused on enterprise adoption, RWA, identity, programmable liquidity, and financial infrastructure. However, these are outcomes pursued through an event and sponsorship strategy rather than direct deployment of new utility, products, infrastructure, or vertical-specific adoption programs. Therefore Adoption & Utility is secondary, not primary.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes a “dual-event strategy” in Singapore designed to “maximize the reach of Cardano and its ecosystem,” combining the Cardano Summit 2026 and a TOKEN2049 title sponsorship. This directly supports a Community & Ecosystem Growth classification because the funded work is primarily event-based ecosystem reach, visibility, and engagement. 

### Evidence 2

The Cardano Summit is described as a showcase for “Cardano's community innovation and technology, enterprise applications, institutional use cases, regulatory roundtables, and governance maturity,” while TOKEN2049 includes a booth, keynote, media interviews, and a showcase from top Cardano projects. This supports Pillar 4 because the proposal centers on public ecosystem demonstration and global engagement. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal states that the strategy aims to “break out of our ecosystem ‘echo chamber’ by engaging broader audiences at high-traffic events,” which closely matches the Vision 2030 Pillar 4 focus on global engagement, market adoption, and proactively demonstrating ecosystem value. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal includes adoption-oriented objectives such as “onboarding new projects, builders, use-cases” and connecting “community, enterprises, and institutions with proven infrastructure, standards, and implementation experience.” This supports Adoption & Utility as a secondary pillar, since adoption is a meaningful objective but is pursued through an event and visibility mechanism. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s KPIs include 1,200 Summit attendees, 700 senior executives and decision makers, 250 enterprise marketing qualified leads, 50 strategic meetings, 15 new strategic collaborations, 200 Cardano wallet sign-ups, media reach, sentiment, and share-of-voice targets. These KPIs reinforce that the proposal is primarily about ecosystem visibility, engagement, and market-facing growth, with adoption-related secondary outcomes.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main funded activities are events, sponsorship, ecosystem showcases, media exposure, networking, builder demos, regional engagement, and brand positioning. These activities align most directly with Vision 2030 Pillar 4, which focuses on global engagement, market adoption, talent/community growth, and demonstrating ecosystem value. 

**Adoption & Utility** is a strong secondary fit because the proposal targets enterprise audiences, institutional finance, strategic collaborations, wallet sign-ups, demos, financial market infrastructure, digital identity, RWAs, and programmable liquidity. However, the proposal does not directly fund the creation, deployment, or scaling of specific DeFi, RWA, payment, supply-chain, identity, or enterprise utility products. It funds an event strategy intended to create adoption opportunities.

**Governance** was not selected as a secondary pillar. Although the proposal includes governance sessions, DRep workshops, and governance discussions, these are agenda components within a broader event strategy rather than the core purpose of the proposal. The proposal does not primarily develop governance tooling, governance incentives, turnout mechanisms, treasury seasons, or governance infrastructure.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected. The proposal includes discussion topics such as open source development, cross-chain interoperability, and infrastructure, but it does not primarily fund protocol development, client diversity, L1/L2 integration, ZK capabilities, security resilience, or research deliverables.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected. The proposal includes revenue generation and reinvestment for future events, but it does not primarily address treasury management, tokenomics, SPO incentives, L2-to-L1 value retention, or long-term network economic resilience as defined by Vision 2030.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably be interpreted under multiple pillars. Its delivery mechanism is clearly an ecosystem-growth and global-engagement event strategy, but it also contains substantial Adoption & Utility objectives and KPIs related to enterprise adoption, institutional engagement, wallet sign-ups, demos, strategic collaborations, and real-world use-case positioning.

### Additional Notes

The strongest classification is **Primary: Community & Ecosystem** with **Secondary: Adoption & Utility**. The ambiguity arises because the proposal frames the event strategy as a means to drive adoption, but the actual funded deliverables are event production, sponsorship, media visibility, ecosystem showcasing, and engagement infrastructure rather than direct adoption deployments.
