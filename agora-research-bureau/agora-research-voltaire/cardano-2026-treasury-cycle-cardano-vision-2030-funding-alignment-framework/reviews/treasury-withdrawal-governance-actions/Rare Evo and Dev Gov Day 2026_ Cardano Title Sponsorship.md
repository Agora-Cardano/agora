## AI Rationale

The proposal primarily aligns with **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth** because its central purpose is to expand Cardano’s visibility, participation, onboarding, industry outreach, and ecosystem coordination through Rare Evo 2026 and Rare Dev Gov Day 2026.

Its largest and most prominent deliverables concern title sponsorship, public branding, livestream distribution, media coverage, free community admission, project exhibition space, travel support, networking, workshops, startup exposure, and engagement with developers, enterprises, investors, and external blockchain communities. These activities directly correspond to Pillar 4’s focus on proactively demonstrating Cardano’s ecosystem value, building confidence among partners and users, cultivating practical ecosystem engagement, and supporting broader market participation. The Vision 2030 framework specifically places public narratives demonstrating Cardano’s strengths and localized or market-focused partnerships under Community & Ecosystem Growth. 

**Governance** is assigned as a Secondary Pillar because Rare Dev Gov Day includes substantial and explicit governance objectives, including workshops, programming, education, coordination among DReps and SPOs, and measurement of governance participation. However, these activities form one component of a broader conference sponsorship and ecosystem-marketing package. The proposal does not principally develop governance infrastructure, voting mechanisms, delegation tools, governance incentives, or treasury-season processes of the kinds specifically prioritized under Pillar 3. 

The proposal’s claimed alignment with **Pillar 5: Sustainability & Resilience** is not sufficient for classification. Preserving an existing conference during difficult market conditions may support organizational continuity, but the proposal does not directly address multi-asset treasury management, treasury yield generation, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO role diversification, or the network decentralization targets that define Pillar 5.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that its primary mission is to “strengthen and expand Cardano's global ecosystem presence” through two community-led initiatives serving as platforms for “coordination, onboarding, networking, governance, and industry outreach.” This establishes ecosystem visibility and engagement as the proposal’s overarching purpose. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies direct ecosystem value through “Global Cardano brand visibility,” “Ecosystem onboarding and education,” “Developer and startup exposure,” “Enterprise and institutional outreach,” “Cross-chain industry engagement,” and “Community retention.” These outcomes most directly match Pillar 4’s external engagement and ecosystem-growth objectives. 

### Evidence 3

The funded deliverables include Cardano title sponsorship, main-stage branding, livestream advertising, tier-one media coverage, free General Admission tickets, complimentary project booths, community travel assistance, startup showcases, and networking with enterprises and investors. The dominant expenditure and delivery structure is therefore centered on ecosystem promotion, access, visibility, and industry engagement. 

### Evidence 4

Governance alignment is supported by the stated objective to “Create accessible opportunities for DReps, SPOs, developers, governance contributors, and community members to collaborate through governance workshops, technical discussions, and ecosystem coordination activities at Cardano Dev Gov Day.” Governance sessions, participation, and post-event governance-engagement reporting are explicit deliverables rather than merely speculative benefits. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s main Work Package combines event production, Cardano-focused programming, media distribution, ecosystem networking, startup showcases, industry outreach, technical and governance education, free community access, and project exhibition support. Although governance is material, the combined package is primarily designed to increase Cardano’s overall ecosystem presence and external visibility.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** was selected over Governance because the proposal’s main strategic justification, budget structure, intended audience, and expected outcomes concern ecosystem visibility, community access, onboarding, media exposure, networking, project promotion, enterprise engagement, and Cardano’s position within the broader blockchain industry.

The title sponsorship of Rare Evo, main-stage branding, livestream advertising, media sponsorship, free tickets, booth allocations, travel support, community giveaways, and project showcases all serve Pillar 4 objectives directly. Governance programming is substantial, especially through Rare Dev Gov Day, but remains embedded within a wider ecosystem conference and promotional initiative.

Governance was therefore retained as a Secondary Pillar rather than selected as Primary. The proposal supports governance education and coordination, but does not primarily improve Cardano’s formal governance architecture, accessibility tooling, voting safeguards, role-based incentives, or treasury-season structure.

Pillar 5 was not assigned because the proposal’s continuity and resilience arguments concern the continued operation of an event platform rather than the specific financial stewardship, tokenomics, SPO incentives, infrastructure diversification, or decentralization outcomes defined by Vision 2030. The return of 20% of VIP ticket revenue to the treasury is financially relevant, but it is not developed into a managed-treasury, multi-asset, yield-generation, or network-economic mechanism.

### Additional Notes

Confidence is Medium because Governance is supported by explicit objectives, deliverables, participant groups, sessions, and reporting metrics. Nevertheless, the proposal retains a clearly dominant Community & Ecosystem purpose, so manual review is not required.
