## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund Cardano protocol and infrastructure engineering across Peras, Leios-related testing, History Expiry, conformance testing, adversarial testing, network observability, block-cost investigation, and ongoing node-related maintenance. That maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, which covers keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” including L1 protocol improvements that raise throughput and shorten finality, plus security and resilience work such as observability and recovery tooling. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Sustainability & Resilience** is justified because the proposal does more than merely improve infrastructure in the abstract. It directly includes History Expiry to reduce SPO storage costs, Hoarding Node work for network observability, and maintenance of ecosystem continuity tools, all framed around long-term network health and operational integrity. The Vision 2030 definition of Pillar 5 focuses on “long-term financial health and operational integrity of the network infrastructure,” which fits those workstreams. 

The proposal also includes developer tooling, such as the Plutus Script Re-Executor, but that is not the main purpose of the request. It is one item inside a broader infrastructure delivery pipeline, not enough to make Adoption & Utility the Primary Pillar.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal requests funding for “Tweag by Modus Create's delivery of 17 work packages across 9 core infrastructure areas for the Cardano ecosystem over 2026–2028.” 

### Evidence 2

The stated primary focus is “the mainnet deployment of Peras,” with improvements to “resilience, scalability, developer tooling, and network observability,” including Peras v1/v2, History Expiry, conformance testing, adversarial fork testing, Hoarding Node, block-cost investigation, and node emulator maintenance. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal states that Cardano’s growth phase depends on “Peras (faster finality) and Leios (higher throughput),” and that these require production-grade mainnet delivery, correctness scaffolding, network observability, incident response, and ecosystem continuity. 

### Evidence 4

For secondary alignment, the proposal states that without History Expiry, increased Leios throughput would make full-history requirements “economically prohibitive, threatening decentralization,” and that the Hoarding Node addresses difficulty detecting orphaned blocks, invalid slot leader claims, mempool anomalies, and adversarial behavior. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s own strategic alignment section states: “Pillar 1: Infrastructure and Research Excellence — all 17 packages directly advance L1 protocol infrastructure” and “Pillar 5: Ecosystem Sustainability and Resilience — History Expiry, Hoarding Node, and maintenance work directly protect long-term network health.”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Infrastructure & Research is selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main reason for existing is delivery of L1 and core infrastructure work: Peras mainnet readiness, protocol correctness testing, fork testing, conformance testing, ledger state standardization, node-related tooling, network observability, and performance/cost investigations. These map directly to Vision 2030 Pillar 1’s focus on protocol improvements, scalability, security, resilience, and technical infrastructure.

Sustainability & Resilience is selected only as a Secondary Pillar because several work packages directly support long-term network health, especially History Expiry, Hoarding Node, and maintenance of Genesis Sync Accelerator and Cardano Node Emulator. However, those sustainability outcomes are embedded within a broader core infrastructure program rather than forming the dominant strategic purpose.

Adoption & Utility is not selected as Primary because the developer tooling component, especially the Plutus Script Re-Executor, is only one workstream within a much larger protocol infrastructure portfolio. Any adoption effect is downstream from improved infrastructure, not the proposal’s central objective.

Governance is not selected because the proposal’s governance-related content concerns administration, contracts, disbursement, audits, and activation dependencies. Those are delivery controls, not governance-system reform or governance participation infrastructure.

Community & Ecosystem is not selected because demos, updates, Discord, and Slack coordination are engagement mechanisms around delivery, not the substantive strategic purpose of the proposal.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains **17 major work packages across 9 core infrastructure areas**, including protocol deployment, testing infrastructure, SPO cost reduction, observability, incident response, developer tooling, and maintenance. Although Infrastructure & Research is the dominant classification, the presence of substantial Sustainability & Resilience workstreams creates enough multi-pillar relevance to justify manual review under the framework rules.

### Additional Notes

The classification should not be based on the proposer’s self-classification alone. However, the proposal’s self-classification is consistent with the work package structure and with the Vision 2030 pillar descriptions.
