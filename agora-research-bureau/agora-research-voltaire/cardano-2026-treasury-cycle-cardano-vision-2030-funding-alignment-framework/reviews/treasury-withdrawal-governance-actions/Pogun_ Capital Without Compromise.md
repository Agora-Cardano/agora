## AI Rationale

Pogun’s primary strategic purpose is to create Cardano-native Bitcoin DeFi utility through an integrated credit market, yield product, and BTC bridge. The proposal repeatedly frames its core objective as bringing Bitcoin liquidity, credit, lending, yield, TVL, transaction activity, and institutional capital onto Cardano. That maps most directly to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, especially the Vision 2030 focus on high-value verticals such as DeFi and RWA, including “institutional-grade liquidity onramps (incl. BTC)” and more usable Cardano-native DeFi.

Infrastructure & Research is justified as a Secondary Pillar because the BitVM bridge is a substantial technical deliverable and is described as shared cross-chain infrastructure for Cardano DApps. This directly relates to Pillar 1’s cross-chain interoperability objective, which includes secure bridges and state-proofs to other chains and legacy infrastructure. 

Pillar 5 is also relevant, but not selected as the Secondary Pillar because the treasury repayment and perpetual EBITDA return model is a funding/economic structure attached to the proposal, while the dominant funded work is still the delivery of DeFi products and interoperability infrastructure. Pillar 5 should be noted, but it does not outweigh the direct product-delivery alignment with Pillars 2 and 1.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that “Pogun is an end-to-end Bitcoin liquidity and credit engine built to position Cardano as the definitive home of Bitcoin DeFi,” and identifies Bitcoin DeFi as the central market opportunity. 

### Evidence 2

Pogun’s three integrated components are a non-margin peer-to-peer Credit Market, a Yield layer routing user capital into yield strategies across private credit, RWAs, and structured fixed-income products, and a BitVM-powered bridge for BTC to Cardano. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says that bringing Bitcoin to Cardano introduces “a massive pool of capital that drives TVL, transaction volume, and DeFi activity,” which aligns directly with Adoption & Utility outcomes rather than governance, community engagement, or base-layer sustainability alone. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal explicitly maps Pogun to Vision 2030 Pillar 2 because it targets DeFi through the credit market and yield product, and RWA through tokenised fixed-income strategies via the yield layer. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal describes the BitVM bridge as “shared infrastructure benefiting any application on Cardano,” making Bitcoin accessible to Cardano applications through a trust-minimized bridge.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is the strongest primary classification because the proposal’s core purpose is not merely to research or maintain infrastructure, but to create active Cardano usage through Bitcoin-backed lending, yield, BTC bridging, DeFi TVL, transaction volume, and institutional participation. The Vision 2030 framework’s Pillar 2 explicitly includes DeFi, RWA, and BTC liquidity onramps, which closely matches the proposal’s main objective. 

Infrastructure & Research is a strong secondary classification because the BitVM bridge, Mithril/Groth16 state attestation, operator security model, and cross-chain BTC movement are substantial technical deliverables. However, these infrastructure components mainly serve the broader Bitcoin DeFi adoption strategy, so they do not displace Pillar 2 as the Primary Pillar.

Pillar 5 is plausible but weaker as a secondary candidate than Pillar 1. The proposal’s 20% EBITDA repayment and 5% perpetual return directly support treasury sustainability, and the Vision 2030 framework includes managed treasury and growth-focused capital deployment under Pillar 5.  Still, this is an economic return structure rather than the main funded product scope. The main deliverables are DeFi and interoperability products, not treasury portfolio management itself.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple pillars. It contains substantial workstreams linked to Adoption & Utility, Infrastructure & Research, and Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience. The strongest primary classification is Adoption & Utility, but the BitVM bridge and treasury return model create genuine classification ambiguity.

### Additional Notes

This classification treats the proposal’s own “Strategic Pillar Alignment” section as supporting evidence, not as determinative. The classification is based on the proposal’s core deliverables and the Cardano Vision 2030 pillar descriptions, not simply on the proposal’s self-labeling.
