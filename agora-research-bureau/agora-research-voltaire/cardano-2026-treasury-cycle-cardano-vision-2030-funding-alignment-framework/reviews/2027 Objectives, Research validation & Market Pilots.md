## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to move Cardano’s strategy from abstract planning into market validation and real-world adoption pilots. Its core pipeline is explicitly described as “Vision & Strategy → Product Research → Market Pilots,” with the stated purpose of validating assumptions through business engagement and supporting high-potential pilots to drive adoption. That maps most directly to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, which focuses on non-speculative utility, high-value verticals, user-facing use cases, and enterprise-grade relevance in the Cardano Vision 2030 framework.  

The strongest evidence is WP3, which is the largest work package by budget and focuses on selecting and supporting 2–5 pilots with product-market fit potential, adoption signals, business development, partnerships, go-to-market support, and possible enterprise or government use cases. WP2 also supports this primary classification by grounding strategy in business validation, market intelligence, sectors, regions, and market opportunities. Strategy workshops and governance validation are meaningful, but they appear to serve the broader adoption pipeline rather than being the proposal’s central strategic purpose.

Community & Ecosystem is assigned as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal contains substantial community and ecosystem coordination components: workshops, stakeholder participation, regional coverage, ecosystem visibility, and case studies. Governance is not selected as Secondary because the proposal uses governance mechanisms, such as an info action and DRep approval, but does not directly deliver governance infrastructure, DRep incentives, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, treasury seasons, or governance accessibility tools as defined in Pillar 3.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will “operationalise the community-defined 2030 strategy” by “translating it into actionable yearly goals,” “validating assumptions through direct engagement with businesses,” and “supporting high-potential pilots to drive real adoption.” 

### Evidence 2

WP3 is described as converting “product research insights into actionable market pilots” and supporting “a small number (2–5) of high-potential pilots” with a realistic path toward “product-market fit.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says WP3 aims to generate “real-world proof points,” “meaningful adoption opportunities,” and potentially “large-scale enterprise or government use cases,” which directly supports the Adoption & Utility focus on real-world, non-speculative utility. 

### Evidence 4

WP2 focuses on “business validation calls across multiple sectors and regions” to “identify market opportunities” and uncover “barriers to adoption,” supporting a market-driven adoption classification rather than a primarily governance classification. 

### Evidence 5

WP1 includes structured workshops, stakeholder alignment, and an info action for DRep approval, but these activities mainly operationalize strategy and coordinate ecosystem input rather than directly improving Cardano’s governance mechanisms as defined under Pillar 3.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility was selected because the proposal’s main justification is strategy validation through market engagement and real-world pilots. WP2 and WP3 are directly adoption-oriented: they target business needs, market opportunities, product-market fit, adoption signals, enterprise/government use cases, and pilot execution. WP3 also represents the largest allocation of the proposal budget, reinforcing that the primary substance of the proposal is adoption execution rather than governance process or general ecosystem coordination.

Governance was not selected as Primary because the governance-related elements are procedural: workshops, community participation, info action submission, and DRep approval. These may involve governance, but they do not primarily improve governance infrastructure, governance accessibility tools, DRep/SPO/CC incentives, adaptive thresholds, delegator safeguards, or treasury-season mechanics.

Community & Ecosystem was selected as Secondary because the proposal includes substantial ecosystem engagement through workshops, stakeholder participation, regional coverage, ecosystem visibility, and case studies. However, these components support the adoption pipeline rather than replacing it as the main strategic purpose.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic functions: yearly strategic goal-setting, business validation and market intelligence, and market pilot support. The proposal also self-identifies alignment with Pillars 2, 3, and 4, creating reasonable classification ambiguity. The strongest primary classification remains Adoption & Utility, but the multi-workstream structure warrants manual review.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s Governance alignment is weaker than its self-classification suggests under a strict Vision 2030 reading. It uses governance as a validation path, but it does not directly deliver governance-system improvements. The Community & Ecosystem alignment is more substantial than Governance because the proposal includes structured workshops, stakeholder engagement, ecosystem visibility, and public case studies.
