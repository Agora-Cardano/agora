## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to generate practical demand for Cardano DeFi/OpenFi rails through app-layer activation, fintech workflows, treasury tools, wallets, payments, stablecoin usage, allocator/fund channels, distribution partners, and US-linked institutional or venture pathways. That maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, which focuses on high-value verticals such as DeFi and payments, user-facing adoption, enterprise-grade use cases, and non-speculative utility. 

The proposal does not primarily build protocol infrastructure, governance mechanisms, treasury portfolio systems, SPO incentive systems, or L2-to-L1 value-retention mechanisms. It references Pillar 5 and includes Treasury-protection logic, repayment/non-release conditions, and “Treasury-return consideration” for future opportunities, but those are accountability safeguards and future-facing review criteria rather than direct implementation of Vision 2030 Pillar 5 objectives. So, naturally, because humans enjoy making classification messier than necessary, **Secondary Pillar = None** under the stricter rules.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the proposal exists to activate demand-side Cardano usage. Its intended outcomes are recurring on-chain activity, user adoption, stablecoin flows, capital routing, payment and settlement workflows, DeFi/OpenFi usage, and app-layer integrations.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal explicitly states that it “does not build infrastructure directly.” It may identify wallet, routing, UX, compliance, data, and user-access gaps, but identifying blockers is not the same as delivering infrastructure or research.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal includes reporting, evidence bundles, administrator review, and accountability processes, but these are project controls rather than governance-system improvements such as DRep incentives, voting systems, treasury seasons, or governance accessibility tools.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected because the proposal is not primarily about education, talent development, community growth, public narrative, or localized ecosystem engagement. It is execution-focused demand activation, not general ecosystem development.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal does not directly implement Vision 2030 Pillar 5 mechanisms such as multi-asset treasury management, managed treasury yield generation, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO role diversification, or decentralization targets. Its Treasury-return considerations and conditional payment gates are useful safeguards, but they are incidental to the main activation program rather than substantial Pillar 5 deliverables.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own pillar selection includes Pillars 2, 5, and 1. Under the stricter classification rules, only Pillar 2 is directly and substantially supported by the core objectives, deliverables, milestones, and KPIs.
