## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to deliver an open-source Rust client library for Ogmios that improves the developer experience for teams building Cardano backend systems. Its main deliverables are a Rust-native client, support for Ogmios mini-protocols, benchmarks, tests, documentation, an example application, and validation on preprod and mainnet.

Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, developer tooling, open-source SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure, migration support, and compatibility are placed under **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the Developer Experience section. The proposal does not directly modify the L1 protocol, create an L2 integration, standardize cross-chain bridges, develop ZK capabilities, improve protocol-critical cryptography, increase client diversity at the node-client level, or implement threat detection and recovery systems. Therefore, although the proposal describes itself as infrastructure, its direct Vision 2030 alignment is best understood as **Developer Experience within Adoption & Utility**.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will fund “the implementation of a robust high-performance Ogmios client library written in Rust” supporting “all four Ogmios mini-protocols: chain synchronization, ledger state queries, transaction submission, and mempool monitoring.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal describes the work as “open-source developer infrastructure” focused on “correctness, performance, and production usability,” with an example application, unit and end-to-end testing, and validation on preprod and mainnet data. 

### Evidence 3

The stated expected value is to “lower the barrier for Rust developers building on Cardano,” improve tooling for “low-latency and high-throughput applications,” and strengthen “open-source backend infrastructure.” This maps directly to the Vision 2030 Developer Experience focus on open-source SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure, and lower switching costs.  

### Evidence 4

The work package objective is to deliver “an open-source Rust client library,” provide a “Rust-native interface for Cardano backend applications,” reduce integration effort for Rust developers, publish benchmarks and testing artifacts, and validate the library against live preprod and mainnet data. 

### Evidence 5

The Vision 2030 reference places “Open-source incentives” for “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure,” developer education/migration, and compatibility with common tooling under **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, not Pillar 1.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is improving developer experience through a Rust-native Ogmios client. The deliverables are SDK/library-level tooling, documentation, benchmarks, testing, and adoption support for backend developers.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as the Primary Pillar because, under the Vision 2030 framework, Pillar 1 is focused on protocol-level scalability and interoperability, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and threat detection/recovery. The proposal does not directly deliver those outcomes. Its “infrastructure” character is real in a generic engineering sense, but the Vision 2030 taxonomy places this type of open-source developer infrastructure under Developer Experience in Pillar 2.

### Additional Notes

The proposer selected Pillar 1 as primary and Pillar 2 as secondary. Based strictly on the Cardano Vision 2030 pillar descriptions, that ordering appears reversed. The proposal is more directly justified by Pillar 2’s Developer Experience section than by Pillar 1’s protocol and resilience focus areas.
