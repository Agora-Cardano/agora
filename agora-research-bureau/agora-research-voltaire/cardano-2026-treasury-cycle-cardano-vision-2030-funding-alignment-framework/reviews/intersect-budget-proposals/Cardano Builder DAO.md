## AI Rationale

The proposal aligns most strongly with **Governance** because its core purpose is to operate a smart contract-governed treasury allocation mechanism: launching and assuring the DAO, onboarding members, deploying governance contracts, establishing treasury infrastructure, running temperature checks, validating participation, maintaining governance records, and administering funding rounds. This maps most directly to the Vision 2030 Governance pillar, which emphasizes governance that is hard to capture, easy to use, and supported by participation tools and structured treasury processes.

A **Secondary Pillar** of **Adoption & Utility** is warranted because the proposal repeatedly states that the DAO exists to fund builders who can improve ecosystem KPIs such as monthly active users, monthly transactions, and TVL. Those are adoption-oriented outcomes within the Vision 2030 framework. However, those outcomes are pursued indirectly through funding selection and governance administration, not through the proposal itself delivering DeFi, payments, RWA, UX, or developer-experience products. For that reason, Adoption & Utility is substantial but secondary rather than primary.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes itself as an “ecosystem-wide, purpose-built, smart contract-governed funding mechanism” whose role is to allocate capital through a DAO structure rather than through fragmented proposal-by-proposal funding. That language supports Governance as the main strategic purpose.

### Evidence 2

In its description of the Initiative DAO Framework, the proposal says funding is organized into “specialized DAOs with clear mandates, transparent reporting, aligned incentives, and built-in accountability.” These are governance and treasury-process characteristics, not direct product-delivery outputs.

### Evidence 3

The first work package centers on “deployment and verification of the DAO’s governance smart contracts,” establishing the on-chain treasury address, minting membership units, onboarding members, and providing third-party assurance that the DAO launched correctly. These are direct governance infrastructure deliverables.

### Evidence 4

The later work packages focus on approving governing documents, validating member eligibility, creating snapshots, publishing temperature checks, collecting feedback, tracking participation, and creating traceable funding decisions. This is a governance process for treasury allocation at ecosystem scale.

### Evidence 5

The proposal also states that funded projects are required to track “monthly active users, TVL, and on-chain transactions,” and that the DAO was designed to fund builders whose work can improve those outcomes. This supports secondary alignment with Adoption & Utility.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Governance** was selected over other plausible pillars because the proposal’s own deliverables are overwhelmingly about treasury process and governance operations: DAO deployment, governance smart contracts, treasury setup, member onboarding, governing documents, eligibility checks, proposal signaling, participation tracking, accountability, and public governance records. These functions align directly with the Vision 2030 Governance pillar’s emphasis on accessible governance processes and structured treasury action cycles.

**Adoption & Utility** was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not itself deliver a DeFi product, payments solution, RWA application, enterprise UX improvement, SSI capability, or developer tooling deliverable defined in that pillar. Instead, it funds and governs the selection of other builders who may produce adoption outcomes. That makes Adoption & Utility a real but secondary alignment.

**Community & Ecosystem** is also a plausible interpretation because the proposal describes itself as a builder coordination layer and a way to strengthen the builder base. However, those benefits are subordinate to the proposal’s more concrete governance and treasury-administration machinery. The proposal does not primarily focus on youth engagement, education programs, localized market adoption, or other direct Community & Ecosystem focus areas from the framework.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams and could reasonably be read through more than one pillar lens. Governance is the strongest fit, but the proposal also makes substantial claims about adoption KPI growth through builder funding, which creates genuine classification ambiguity at the margin.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s self-selected alignment to Pillars 2, 3, and 4 was considered, but the classification above is based on the proposal’s stated objectives and work packages measured against the Vision 2030 pillar descriptions, with the Vision 2030 document treated as the authoritative reference.
