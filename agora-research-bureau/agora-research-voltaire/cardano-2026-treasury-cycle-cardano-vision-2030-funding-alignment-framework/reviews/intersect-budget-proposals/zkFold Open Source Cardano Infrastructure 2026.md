## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund open-source technical infrastructure for Cardano: a ZK Rollup mainnet launch, Smart Wallet v2 backend and send-to-email DApp, and Bitcoin-Cardano Atomic Swaps. The strongest alignment is with **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, because the proposal directly targets scalability, ZK capabilities, interoperability, mainnet launch readiness, monitoring, open-source infrastructure, and maintained technical services. The Vision 2030 framework defines this pillar around keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” and explicitly includes L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, and core ZK capabilities. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because Smart Wallet v2 and the send-to-email DApp directly aim to reduce user friction, improve payment UX, lower transaction/claim costs, and support practical Cardano usage. Atomic Swaps and ZK Rollup also support payment, DeFi, Bitcoin liquidity, and institutional/private transaction use cases. However, these adoption outcomes depend on infrastructure being delivered first, so they do not override Pillar 1 as the primary classification.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will “turn three advanced zkFold workstreams into public, auditable infrastructure” and deliver three mainnet launches: ZK Rollup, Smart Wallet v2/send-to-email, and Bitcoin-Cardano Atomic Swaps.

### Evidence 2

The ZK Rollup work package aims to turn a “working testnet prototype into a maintained mainnet-facing privacy and scalability service for Cardano,” including transaction privacy, optimization, mainnet launch, monitoring, maintenance, and documentation.

### Evidence 3

The Atomic Swaps work package is described as “live, open-source infrastructure for trustless BTC/Cardano exchange,” including mainnet launch, open-source release, edge-case recovery, monitoring, and builder integration documentation.

### Evidence 4

The Smart Wallet v2 work package will publish an open-source backend server component that builders can reuse, self-host, or integrate, while also launching a send-to-email DApp for simpler Cardano payments.

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that Smart Wallet v2 targets a reduction in email-backed claim/send costs from roughly 4-5 ADA to roughly 0.3-0.4 ADA, and that the DApp will be compatible with existing Cardano wallets.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the best primary classification because the proposal is mainly funding technical infrastructure: ZK rollup infrastructure, cross-chain atomic swap infrastructure, wallet backend infrastructure, open-source codebases, mainnet launches, monitoring, maintenance, and developer documentation. That is the thing that justifies the proposal’s existence, not merely the pleasant downstream dream that users may eventually click buttons without needing a ceremonial engineering degree.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid secondary pillar because the Smart Wallet/send-to-email flow and lower-cost payment UX directly support easier Cardano use. Atomic Swaps also support Bitcoin/Cardano liquidity movement and cross-chain DApps. These are substantial adoption-oriented objectives, but they are delivered through infrastructure work, so Adoption & Utility is secondary rather than primary.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as a secondary pillar. The proposal includes maintenance, monitoring, issue response, and operational support, but it does not directly address the main Pillar 5 focus areas in the Vision 2030 framework, such as treasury management, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or decentralization targets. Generic operational maintenance alone is not enough to classify the proposal under Pillar 5.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams and has substantial alignment with more than one pillar. The dominant pillar is Infrastructure & Research, but Smart Wallet v2 and send-to-email payments create meaningful secondary alignment with Adoption & Utility.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own stated alignment includes Pillars 1, 2, and 5, but the proposal-specific analysis supports Pillar 1 as primary, Pillar 2 as secondary, and does not find enough direct evidence for Pillar 5 as a classification pillar under the stricter CVTFAF rules.
