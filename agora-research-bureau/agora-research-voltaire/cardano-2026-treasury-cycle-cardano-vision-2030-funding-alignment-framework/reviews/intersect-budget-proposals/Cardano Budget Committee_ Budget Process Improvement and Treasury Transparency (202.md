## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to improve Cardano’s treasury governance process, especially the budget cycle, DRep review experience, proposal evaluation workflows, Ekklesia tooling, transparency standards, and governance-facing reporting.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 3: Governance**, especially the Vision 2030 emphasis on governance being “hard to capture, easy to use, and paced,” as well as its focus on governance accessibility tools and Treasury Seasons.

A **Secondary Pillar: Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is justified because one work package directly delivers treasury projection models, Net Change Limit scenario modeling, stress testing, and treasury runway analysis. These are not merely generic benefits; they are explicit deliverables aimed at improving treasury financial stewardship and long-term capital planning. However, this is secondary because the proposal does not directly implement multi-asset treasury management, yield generation, SPO incentives, or L2-to-L1 value-retention mechanisms. Its core function remains governance process improvement.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it supports “continued evolution of Cardano’s treasury governance through two core work packages: (1) budget process and tooling improvement, and (2) treasury transparency and analytics.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the work is designed to make “the budgeting process more repeatable, accessible, and scalable,” while strengthening transparency and enabling “data-informed decision-making.”

### Evidence 3

Work Package 2 explicitly focuses on improving the budget process through surveys, workshops, a retrospective report, a Product Requirements Document, updated Ekklesia tooling, an updated 2027 Budget Framework, and an on-chain governance info action for DRep approval.

### Evidence 4

The proposal says Work Package 1 will deliver a “Treasury Projection Model,” a “Net Change Limit (NCL) Scenario Framework,” stress-test scenarios, and dashboard views enabling DReps and the Budget Committee to compare treasury scenarios.

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s stated governance value is to reduce “heavy DRep review workloads,” improve proposal submission and evaluation workflows, and support Treasury Seasons by making them easier to navigate.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Governance** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal’s main activities are governance-process design, budget-cycle improvement, DRep-facing tooling, proposal review standardization, transparency reporting, and governance action preparation.

Although the proposal contains financial analytics and treasury runway modeling, those activities are presented as decision-support infrastructure for DReps and the Budget Committee, not as direct treasury asset management or tokenomics reform. So the Pillar 5 alignment is real, but not dominant.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as a secondary pillar. The proposal includes workshops, documentation, and community testing, but those appear to support adoption of the governance process and tooling rather than directly driving global engagement, talent acquisition, localized adoption, or ecosystem market growth as described in Pillar 4.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains two major workstreams with partially different strategic purposes:

1. Budget process and tooling improvement, which strongly supports **Governance**.
2. Treasury transparency, forecasting, and NCL scenario modeling, which substantially supports **Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience**.

The dominant pillar is still Governance, but the Pillar 5 component is substantial enough to justify manual review rather than pretending the classification is perfectly clean. It never is.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own selected pillars include Governance, Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience, and Community & Ecosystem Growth. Based strictly on the Vision 2030 framework and proposal deliverables, only Governance and Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience are directly substantial enough for classification.
