## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to build a structured African innovation and talent pipeline that moves CATS Hackathon teams into sustained Cardano participation through incubation, mentorship, hub activation, technical training, business support, and startup readiness. That maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on cultivating a skilled developer base, hands-on experience, structured funding routes, and localized adoption in high-impact regions.

A secondary alignment with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** is also substantial, not merely incidental, because the proposal targets the delivery of open-source, smart-contract-verified MVPs on Cardano mainnet/testnet, with explicit real-world use cases in agritech and RWA. Still, the proposal exists primarily as an incubation and ecosystem-capacity program, not as a direct deployment of a single adoption product.  

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes itself as “a grassroots innovation pipeline designed to transition projects from the 2026 Cardano Africa Tech Summit (CATS) Hackathon into viable, investor-ready startups.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that it will provide “structured, end-to-end support over 12 months,” including incubation, technical mentorship, business mentorship, operational mentorship, post-incubation support, iteration, validation, and external readiness.

### Evidence 3

The proposal says it will leverage Wada’s decentralized hub network to ensure solutions are “locally grounded, collaboratively developed, and built on Cardano infrastructure,” which directly supports localized ecosystem growth and regional talent development.

### Evidence 4

WP1 aims to onboard “10–12 regional hubs” and at least “20 high-potential projects,” while delivering training, mentorship, cross-hub collaboration, MVP development, business modeling, pitch coaching, and Demo Day preparation.

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that it will directly result in “a minimum of 20 open-source, smart-contract-verified MVPs deployed on Cardano mainnet/testnet,” explicitly targeting “non-speculative utility in African agritech and Real World Assets (RWA).”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal’s core mechanism is ecosystem capacity-building: onboarding hubs, training developers, mentoring project teams, strengthening regional collaboration, and creating a structured pathway from hackathon participation to long-term contributor and startup formation.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid secondary pillar because the proposal includes concrete deliverables tied to real-world applications, MVP deployment, smart contract validation, and non-speculative utility in agritech and RWA. However, these adoption outcomes are produced through an incubation pipeline rather than being the proposal’s sole or dominant strategic purpose.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily improve Cardano’s base-layer infrastructure, L1/L2 scalability, interoperability standards, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, threat recovery, or protocol-level research. Its technical outputs are project-level MVPs and smart contract validation, not ecosystem-wide infrastructure.

**Governance** was not selected because governance education and forum participation appear as supporting activities, not as the proposal’s main deliverable or strategic purpose.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily address treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or long-term network infrastructure economics.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal has substantial alignment with both **Community & Ecosystem** and **Adoption & Utility**. The primary classification is clear enough to assign Community & Ecosystem, but the proposal could reasonably be debated as Adoption & Utility due to its direct MVP deployment, real-world use case focus, and stated intention to drive non-speculative utility.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own selected pillars are Pillar 4 and Pillar 2. That self-classification is consistent with this analysis, but the primary pillar is determined here by the proposal’s core operating model: structured regional incubation and ecosystem growth.
