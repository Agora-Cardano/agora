## AI Rationale

This proposal focuses on onboarding startups to Cardano’s mainnet and driving on-chain activity. Its core objectives are to take European startups from no blockchain presence to a deployed Cardano solution, measured by transactions. This aligns directly with the **Adoption & Utility** pillar, which emphasizes widespread, non-speculative utility and real-world usage of Cardano. The proposal’s deliverables and KPIs are about increasing Cardano’s usage and utility, not building infrastructure, governance mechanisms, or ecosystem outreach. Other pillars are not the main focus: the project is not about developing protocol technology, designing governance, general community engagement or talent programs, or financial stewardship. Therefore, Adoption & Utility is the best-fit pillar.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal’s main goal is to drive real Cardano usage by integrating startups onto the network. Its objectives, including on-chain deployment and transaction generation, mirror the Adoption & Utility pillar’s focus on growing non-speculative utility and real-world usage. The evidence above shows the proposal repeatedly measures success by transactions and explicitly calls adoption its value proposition. Other pillars do not match its core purpose. For example, it does not involve protocol development, governance processes, or long-term financial/tokenomics strategy. While the proposer mentions global startup networks, suggesting ecosystem connections, these serve the adoption goal rather than broad community or talent development. Thus, Adoption & Utility is clearly primary.

### Additional Notes

The proposer themselves lists Pillar 2 and Pillar 4, but our analysis finds Pillar 2 dominates. We assign no secondary pillar since community growth objectives are not substantial in the proposal’s content.
