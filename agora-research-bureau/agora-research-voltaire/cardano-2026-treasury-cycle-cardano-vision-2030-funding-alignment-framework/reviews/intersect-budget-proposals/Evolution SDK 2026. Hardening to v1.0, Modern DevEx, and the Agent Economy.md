## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to improve Cardano developer experience through a production-grade TypeScript/JavaScript SDK, simplified onboarding, DeFi composability, and AI-agent tooling. Under the official Cardano Vision 2030 framework, Developer Experience is part of **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically including the maintenance of Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure, education/migration materials, and compatibility with familiar multi-chain tooling.

Although the proposal describes the SDK as “foundational infrastructure,” its deliverables are not primarily L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, node client diversity, or protocol resilience. Therefore, **Infrastructure & Research** is not selected as the Primary Pillar, and is not strong enough as a Secondary Pillar under the official framework. The proposal’s intended impact flows mainly through more developers, faster dApp creation, DeFi integrations, and easier transaction generation, all of which map most directly to Adoption & Utility.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal identifies the core problem as developer onboarding and tooling: “Today, a TypeScript developer arriving from another chain finds no stable SDK, no familiar API patterns, no way to compose DeFi from a single library, and no AI-agent tooling.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that Evolution SDK will deliver “a stable SDK, a faster transaction pipeline, a DeFi composability layer, and an AI-agent toolkit,” all open-source and governed under Intersect. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal’s adoption case centers on reducing developer friction: a simplified API “cutting time-to-first-transaction from hours to under 5 minutes,” a DeFi plugin system with named integrations, and an AI-agent toolkit able to send ADA, mint tokens, swap, stake, and participate in governance. 

### Evidence 4

The work package objective includes “faster developer onboarding,” “DeFi composability from one SDK,” “Cardano’s first AI-agent toolkit,” and “developer education and community engagement.” These are direct Developer Experience and application-layer adoption deliverables, not base-layer protocol research deliverables. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal measures success with SDK-specific outputs: v1.0.0 on npm, sub-millisecond transaction construction, Effect v4 migration, time-to-first-transaction under 5 minutes, four DeFi plugins, and an AI-agent toolkit. These are concrete developer tooling/adoption-enablement targets.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the Cardano Vision 2030 framework explicitly places Developer Experience inside Pillar 2, including core SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure maintenance, migration materials, and compatibility with familiar tooling. The proposal is almost a checklist for that section: SDK hardening, simplified APIs, documentation, onboarding, DeFi plugins, and agent tooling.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily address L1 consensus, ledger, networking, L2 settlement, cross-chain state proofs, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or protocol recovery. Its “infrastructure” is developer/application infrastructure, which the official framework treats as Developer Experience under Pillar 2.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as Secondary because the documentation, recipes, videos, and office hours are support mechanisms for SDK adoption rather than a standalone talent acquisition, education pipeline, regional engagement, or ecosystem-growth program.

**Governance** was not selected as Secondary because governance actions are included as SDK functionality, but the proposal is not primarily about DRep incentives, governance accessibility tools, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, treasury seasons, or governance process design.

### Additional Notes

Under a separate internal spending-taxonomy override, this could reasonably be tagged as “technical tooling / SDK infrastructure.” But under the official Cardano Vision 2030 pillar definitions supplied here, the strongest classification is **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**.
