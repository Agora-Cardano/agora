## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to convert enterprise interest into active Cardano deployments that generate real-world, non-speculative utility. Its target use cases, including Digital Product Passports, EUDR compliance, supply chain/compliance, RWA, public deployments, humanitarian onboarding, and forensic data anchoring, map directly to Pillar 2’s focus on high-value verticals, enterprise-grade security/compliance, and real-world adoption. The proposal also aims to increase monthly transactions, MAU, and TVL through enterprise pilots, which aligns with the Vision 2030 adoption KPIs.

A Secondary Pillar of Community & Ecosystem is justified because the proposal includes substantial public-facing ecosystem deliverables: open-source regulatory frameworks, public case studies, pipeline reports, and reusable integration blueprints for builders and future enterprise work. These are not the main reason the proposal exists, but they are direct and substantial enough to support Pillar 4.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the program is “designed to convert enterprise interest into active on-chain deployments” and targets “Supply Chain/Compliance, Luxury DPPs, RWA, and Public Deployments” to generate “sustainable, high-volume transactional activity.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies Pillar 2 blockers as “regulatory ambiguity and the absence of structured technical onboarding,” and says it will remove those blockers to drive “execution into live deployments.” 

### Evidence 3

WP3 is described as the “core conversion engine” dedicated to converting sandbox evaluations into “signed pilot agreements and verifiable on-chain deployments.” 

### Evidence 4

The proposal states that WP3 will generate “verifiable network utility” through “measurable, active on-chain block space usage,” including “transactions, metadata anchoring, or TVL.” 

### Evidence 5

The secondary ecosystem alignment is supported by deliverables such as “open-source regulatory framework and case studies,” “quarterly pipeline reports,” and “sanitized Enterprise Pilot Case Studies” intended as reusable blueprints for the Cardano community.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is the strongest primary classification because the proposal is fundamentally an enterprise activation and deployment program. Its core work packages move from enterprise readiness, to enterprise pipeline management, to pilot execution and on-chain deployment. This directly matches the Vision 2030 definition of Pillar 2, which focuses on “widespread, non-speculative utility,” high-value verticals such as supply chain/provenance, RWA, payments, and enterprise security/compliance. 

Community & Ecosystem is plausible as a secondary pillar because the proposal includes public ecosystem assets, reporting, case studies, and market-facing proof points. However, those outputs support the enterprise adoption pathway rather than defining the proposal’s main strategic purpose. Governance is not selected because the Expert Council is an internal oversight mechanism for the sandbox, not a direct contribution to Cardano’s on-chain governance model, DRep participation, turnout-aware voting, treasury seasons, or governance accessibility tools.

### Additional Notes

The proposal is not classified as Infrastructure & Research because it does not primarily improve Cardano’s protocol, L1/L2 scalability, interoperability standards, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, or threat detection. It is not classified as Sustainability & Resilience because treasury management, SPO incentives, multi-asset treasury design, and L2-to-L1 value retention are not the proposal’s core objectives.
