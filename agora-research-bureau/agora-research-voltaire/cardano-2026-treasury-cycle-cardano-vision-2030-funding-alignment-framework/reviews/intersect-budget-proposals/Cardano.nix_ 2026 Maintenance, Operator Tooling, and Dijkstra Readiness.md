## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to maintain and improve Cardano infrastructure tooling for reproducible deployment, service module maintenance, operator workflows, and readiness for the Dijkstra hard fork. Its main deliverables focus on Cardano service deployment infrastructure, NixOS modules, dependency maintenance, test coverage, Mithril bootstrap support, SPO operator tooling, and protocol upgrade readiness. That maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, which covers keeping Cardano secure, fast, interoperable, and technically capable of hosting more economic activity.

A secondary alignment with **Pillar 5: Sustainability & Resilience** is justified because the proposal also directly supports operational integrity, reproducibility, rollback, disaster recovery, dependency hygiene, and SPO operational resilience. This is not merely a generic externality; several milestones are specifically aimed at reducing operational toil and improving the reliability of network infrastructure.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that **Cardano.nix is a Nix flake and NixOS module suite that gives developers and operators a declarative, reproducible deployment path for Cardano services**, including `cardano-node`, `ogmios`, `kupo`, `cardano-db-sync`, and surrounding infrastructure. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal’s work package covers **maintenance, feature delivery, and protocol readiness** across six milestones, including source-build restoration, service module completion, developer environment modules, SPO operator tooling, validation, and Dijkstra readiness. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the work supports Cardano by **reducing operator misconfiguration risk and lowering the practical barrier to running Cardano services in production**, which aligns with infrastructure reliability and operational resilience. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal includes SPO-focused deliverables such as **guild-operator workflow packaging, spo-anywhere integration assessment, SPO role/module refinement, testnet validation, Mithril signer prototyping, and final onboarding documentation**. 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 1 as keeping Cardano **secure, fast, and interoperable**, while Pillar 5 focuses on the **long-term financial health and operational integrity of the network infrastructure**.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal exists mainly to maintain and improve technical infrastructure used to deploy and operate Cardano services. The core outputs are software infrastructure, service modules, reproducible build/deployment paths, integration tests, Mithril bootstrap support, and Dijkstra hard fork readiness.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is a valid secondary pillar because the proposal contains substantial work directly tied to operational integrity: reproducibility, dependency hygiene, rollback, disaster recovery, SPO tooling, and testnet validation. However, this secondary alignment does not override the primary classification because those resilience benefits arise through infrastructure tooling rather than through treasury management, tokenomics, SPO economic incentives, or broader financial sustainability mechanisms.

**Adoption & Utility** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily target end users, enterprises, DeFi, RWA, payments, supply chain, or direct on-chain demand. Developer and operator experience may indirectly support adoption, but the funded work is primarily infrastructure maintenance and deployment tooling.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal does not focus on DRep participation, voting systems, treasury seasons, governance tooling, or governance safeguards.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily focus on talent acquisition, education, localized engagement, market adoption, or public ecosystem narrative.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own stated alignment with Pillar 1 as primary and Pillar 5 as secondary is consistent with the actual work described. The secondary pillar is justified because the operational resilience component is direct and substantial, not just a vague “this helps the ecosystem somehow” claim.
