## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to improve Cardano developer experience by making array support faster, more consistent, and better documented across Plinth, Plutarch, and Aiken. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, Developer Experience is explicitly placed under Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, including maintenance of core SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure, education/migration, and compatibility with common tooling. The proposal repeatedly frames its expected value around making Cardano easier to develop on, easier to learn, and easier to migrate to from other ecosystems.  

A plausible Pillar 1 argument exists because the work includes a new CIP and Plutus Core primitives. However, the Vision 2030 Pillar 1 focus areas emphasize L1 protocol scalability/interoperability, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, threat detection, and recovery. The proposal does not primarily aim to improve consensus, ledger/network throughput, finality, client diversity, protocol security, or L2 integration. Its Plutus Core work is best understood as a means to improve smart-contract language tooling and developer productivity, which maps more directly to Pillar 2 Developer Experience than to Pillar 1 Infrastructure & Research. 

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will “bring Plinth and Aiken up to parity with Plutarch for CIP-138 array support,” “propose and implement a new CIP introducing array primitives to Plutus Core,” apply optimization techniques across Plinth, Plutarch, and Aiken, and document the measured impact for the developer community. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that the result is for “Cardano script and dApp developers” to get array performance in the languages they already use, with documentation explaining how to use it, making Cardano “materially easier to develop on, easier to learn, and easier to migrate to from other ecosystems.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly identifies its primary alignment as “Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility through the Developer Experience focus area,” and says Cardano’s three smart contract languages do not currently offer the same developer experience for array work. 

### Evidence 4

The work package core objectives are technical-language deliverables: equal CIP-138 array support in Plinth, Plutarch, and Aiken; a new implemented CIP for array computations; optimization techniques for each language; and documentation of techniques and impact. 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places Developer Experience under Pillar 2 and includes open-source incentives for “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure,” education/migration for developers moving to Cardano/UTxO, and compatibility with common multi-chain tooling.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is selected because the Vision 2030 framework explicitly includes Developer Experience inside Pillar 2, and the proposal’s core purpose is to improve smart-contract language tooling, developer productivity, onboarding, migration, and compatibility.

Infrastructure & Research is plausible but not selected as the Primary Pillar. The proposal includes Plutus Core primitive work, a CIP, compiler/language optimization, and open-source infrastructure. However, within the official Vision 2030 taxonomy, this specific type of SDK/framework/language tooling work is more directly captured by Pillar 2 Developer Experience than by Pillar 1’s focus on L1 scalability, interoperability, security, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, threat detection, or recovery.

Secondary Pillar is set to None because the Infrastructure & Research connection is real but not clearly mapped to the specific Pillar 1 focus areas in the framework. The Plutus Core work functions mainly as an enabling component for the Developer Experience objective rather than as a standalone Pillar 1 strategic outcome.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple Primary Pillars depending on whether the classifier prioritizes the official Vision 2030 taxonomy or the economic substance of the funded work. Under the official framework, the strongest classification is Pillar 2. Under a resource-allocation lens, the proposal could be viewed as technical infrastructure/tooling.

### Additional Notes

This classification follows the official Cardano Vision 2030 taxonomy. It does not apply an analytical override that would relabel heavy SDK, compiler, language, or tooling work as Infrastructure & Research for spending-allocation clarity.
