## AI Rationale

The proposal’s strongest primary alignment is with **Infrastructure & Research** because its largest and most central workstream funds protocol-facing technical governance: parameter recommendations, benchmarking studies, CIP editorial support, and hard fork coordination. These activities directly support Cardano’s protocol evolution, security, uptime, scalability, node diversity, and structured improvement pipeline. In Vision 2030 terms, that maps most directly to keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” through protocol improvement, security, resilience, and client-diversity-related coordination. 

A **Governance** secondary pillar is justified because the proposal also funds independent technical reviews designed to help DReps and the community make better-informed funding and governance decisions. That is not merely incidental; WP3 is a dedicated governance-support mechanism.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the TSC requests funding for “community engagement, protocol governance, and independent technical review,” and describes its role as providing “robust technical governance infrastructure.”

### Evidence 2

WP2 funds the **Parameter Committee, CIP editors, and Hard Fork Working Group**, which are responsible for protocol parameter advice, evaluating Cardano Improvement Proposals, and coordinating Cardano hard forks.

### Evidence 3

The proposal says the Parameter Committee’s evidence-based advice on protocol settings directly supports scalability and security, while the Hard Fork Working Group ensures protocol upgrades are coordinated safely and effectively.

### Evidence 4

WP3 funds a pilot independent technical review programme to assess core infrastructure proposals and provide DReps with clear technical analysis before governance voting deadlines.

### Evidence 5

The proposal identifies its most direct KPI alignments as uptime reliability, DRep participation rate, and node client diversity, showing substantial overlap between infrastructure outcomes and governance-support outcomes.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s most concrete funded activities are technical infrastructure governance functions: protocol parameter evaluation, independent benchmarking, CIP pipeline support, hard fork coordination, and technical reports on topics such as throughput, finality, quantum resistance, and partner chains.

**Governance** is a strong secondary classification because WP3 directly supports DRep decision-making and proposal review. However, the governance function is primarily applied to technical and core infrastructure decisions, rather than broader governance accessibility, voting-system design, delegation tools, treasury seasons, or governance participation infrastructure as the main object of the proposal.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as a secondary pillar because WP1’s event attendance and technical communication are meaningful but appear to support technical coordination and information flow rather than serving as the proposal’s main strategic purpose. **Adoption & Utility** and **Sustainability & Resilience** appear mostly as downstream or indirect benefits rather than direct primary objectives.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes: protocol evolution and infrastructure support under WP2, governance-facing technical review under WP3, and community-facing coordination under WP1. The proposal could reasonably be argued as either **Infrastructure & Research** or **Governance**, although Infrastructure & Research is the stronger primary fit based on the funded deliverables.

### Additional Notes

The proposer self-describes the work as cross-cutting and aligned with all five pillars, but the classification should not accept that broad claim at face value. The direct deliverables support Pillar 1 most strongly, with Pillar 3 as a substantial secondary alignment.
