## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to add foundational zero-knowledge infrastructure to Cardano through new Pasta curve builtins for Plutus Core, a CIP, cardano-base implementation, and a proof-of-concept Kimchi PLookup verifier. This maps directly to Vision 2030 Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research, especially the “Core ZK capabilities” focus area, which concerns modular ZK infrastructure for private, verifiable computation.

Although the proposal mentions future enterprise privacy, compliance, and ZK dApp adoption, these are downstream effects rather than direct adoption deliverables. The funded work is not primarily a user-facing adoption initiative, enterprise deployment, market program, or vertical-specific application. Therefore, no Secondary Pillar is assigned.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Cardano currently cannot use Pasta/Kimchi capabilities on-chain because “the necessary curve operations aren't available as Plutus Core builtins,” and that the proposal “closes that gap.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal’s stated outputs include “a CIP describing UPLC builtins for the Pallas and Vesta curves,” “an implementation in cardano-base,” “the corresponding builtins in Plutus Core,” and “a proof-of-concept verifier for Kimchi-variant PLookup proofs.”

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly states that it aligns with Pillar 1 because “Core ZK capabilities are named directly as a focus area of Pillar 1” and describes the work as a “concrete contribution to that focus area.”

### Evidence 4

The proposal describes durable infrastructure outputs: “the cardano-base FFI logic becomes reusable,” “the Plutus Core builtins become permanent additions to Cardano's bytecode,” and the proof-of-concept becomes a reference for downstream ZK applications.

### Evidence 5

The work package objective is to make “Pasta curve operations available as Plutus Core builtins, merged into cardano-base and Plutus Core through accepted PRs,” confirming that the core deliverable is protocol/developer infrastructure rather than direct market adoption.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Infrastructure & Research is the strongest classification because the proposal is centered on cryptographic primitives, Plutus Core builtins, cardano-base implementation, CIP authorship, and ZK proof-system enablement. These are foundational technical infrastructure outputs, not user acquisition, governance process improvements, treasury management, SPO incentives, or community engagement programs.

Adoption & Utility is not selected as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal’s adoption relevance is mostly indirect. The proposal argues that stronger ZK capabilities may support future enterprise privacy, compliance, and ZK applications, but it does not itself deliver a production application, enterprise rollout, vertical adoption program, or measurable user-facing utility outcome. Humanity survives another classification boundary dispute.
