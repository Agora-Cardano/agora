## AI Rationale

This proposal’s core objective is to build fundamental blockchain infrastructure. It plans to develop a high-performance consensus protocol, Ouroboros Tachýs, and an open-source toolkit for deploying new partner chains connected to Cardano. These tasks directly map to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research Excellence**, which emphasizes protocol scalability, new consensus development, and interoperable infrastructure.

The proposal explicitly describes itself as “focused infrastructure investment” and repeatedly highlights technical improvements such as throughput, fast confirmations, partner chain deployment tooling, and production-grade bridging infrastructure. Other pillars are not the primary goal: it does not center on end-user application adoption or UX improvements, governance processes, or community education programs. The mention of making ADA more valuable and supporting the treasury is a secondary benefit, not the proposal’s core purpose. Thus, the project is best classified under Infrastructure & Research as its dominant purpose.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal is explicitly about developing Cardano’s core technology stack, including a new consensus variant, partner chain deployment tooling, and bridging infrastructure. This most directly matches **Infrastructure & Research**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on scalability, interoperability, L2 integration, protocol improvements, and secure infrastructure.

The proposal does reference adoption, utility, ecosystem growth, and treasury sustainability, but these appear mainly as downstream outcomes of the infrastructure being built. Pillar 2 is relevant as an expected consequence, since the proposal argues that partner chains may support RWA, DeFi, enterprise use cases, TVL, transactions, and MAU. However, the proposal’s actual deliverables are dominated by protocol engineering, system integration, bridging technology, tooling standards, documentation, demonstrators, and upstreaming into Cardano-maintained infrastructure.

Pillar 4 is also present through builder workshops, documentation, ecosystem engagement, and efforts to attract external builder teams. Still, these activities support the adoption of the technical infrastructure rather than forming the proposal’s primary strategic purpose.

Pillar 5 is referenced through claims about ADA demand, SPO incentives, L2-to-L1 value retention, and treasury sustainability. These are meaningful expected effects, but they are not the main deliverable structure of the proposal.

For that reason, Infrastructure & Research is the strongest and most direct classification. No Secondary Pillar is assigned because the other pillar alignments are mostly downstream or supporting effects rather than separate, substantial strategic workstreams strong enough to justify secondary classification.
