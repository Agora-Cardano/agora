## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant purpose is technical maintenance of Cardano’s Ledger integration across device firmware, Ledger Vault, Ledger Wallet, LedgerJS-Cardano, backend services, and hardfork compatibility. That maps most directly to **Infrastructure & Research**, especially the framework’s emphasis on keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” and improving security, resilience, and reliability. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal includes substantial wallet-facing and developer-facing deliverables: Ledger Wallet maintenance, send/receive/staking/DRep flow continuity, third-party wallet support, Developer Portal refreshes, and compatibility with new network features. These directly support user experience, enterprise readiness, and developer compatibility under Pillar 2, but they are not the proposal’s main strategic justification.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it seeks a yearly maintenance fee for Ledger Technologies SAS to maintain the Cardano application, ensuring “security, performance, and compatibility” and providing users with “timely updates, bug fixes, and seamless integration with new network features.” 

### Evidence 2

The first work package covers ongoing maintenance of Cardano hardware-rooted surfaces at Ledger, including app-cardano, app-cardano-new, SDK and security policy updates, bug fixes, native-token whitelist refreshes, device-target alignment, hardfork follow-ups, and Ledger Enterprise Vault HSM upkeep. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal defines the core objective as keeping Cardano device applications compatible with the latest Ledger BOLOS SDK and security standards, available on every shipping Ledger device, and maintaining Ledger Vault reliability with no unplanned outages. 

### Evidence 4

Success is measured by buildability and shippability against the latest BOLOS SDK, regular maintenance releases, no Critical/High security findings carried longer than 90 days, no unplanned Cardano-flow outages in Vault production, and Enterprise SLA triage. 

### Evidence 5

The second work package includes Ledger Wallet, LedgerJS-Cardano, backend services, governance-flow maintenance, Developer Portal refreshes, third-party wallet query handling, and hardfork absorption, supporting Adoption & Utility as a secondary pillar through wallet usability and developer compatibility.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal is fundamentally a technical maintenance and reliability proposal. Its central deliverables concern firmware, SDK compatibility, security review, Vault HSM maintenance, backend upkeep, regression testing, and hardfork readiness.

**Adoption & Utility** is relevant but secondary. The proposal does support user access, Ledger Wallet continuity, institutional readiness, governance flows, and third-party wallet/dApp developer integration. However, these outcomes depend on the successful maintenance of the underlying Ledger-Cardano integration. They are downstream effects of the technical maintenance work rather than the core reason the proposal exists.

**Governance** was not selected because the DRep/governance-flow support is one maintained feature among several wallet flows, not the proposal’s main objective. The proposal is not primarily about DRep incentives, governance tooling, turnout-aware voting, treasury seasons, or governance process design.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected because the proposal does not focus primarily on talent acquisition, education, regional engagement, ecosystem marketing, or community growth.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal does not focus on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or long-term network economics. Its resilience contribution is technical and operational, which is better captured under Infrastructure & Research.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains two work packages, but both serve the same strategic purpose: maintaining Cardano’s Ledger integration across device, wallet, enterprise, backend, and developer-support surfaces. Therefore, manual review is not required.
