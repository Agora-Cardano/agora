## AI Rationale

The proposal focuses on structured funding to maintain and improve Cardano’s core infrastructure through open-source development. Its key objectives include “Structured funding for mainnet infrastructure… to prevent infrastructure degradation,” and “Bug bounties and incident monitoring to reduce systemic protocol risk,” which directly support Pillar 1, Infrastructure & Research. These aims align with the goal to keep Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable” by ensuring the base-layer code and tools are well-maintained.

A secondary aspect of the proposal is developer engagement, especially “Developer Advocacy… to strengthen contributor flow into core repositories and community projects,” which aligns with Pillar 4’s emphasis on cultivating a skilled developer base. However, the core purpose is sustaining and enhancing infrastructure rather than broad adoption, governance reform, or new market utility. Therefore, Infrastructure & Research is the best primary fit, with Community & Ecosystem as a secondary supporting pillar.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal’s primary objective is to fund and sustain Cardano’s core software and infrastructure, which maps to Vision 2030 Pillar 1, Infrastructure & Research Excellence. The pillar’s description, “Keep Cardano secure, fast, and interoperable so it can host more economic activity,” directly aligns with the proposal’s focus on infrastructure maintenance and resilience.

In contrast, the secondary objective of improving the developer pipeline aligns with Pillar 4, Community & Ecosystem Growth, which emphasizes building a skilled developer base. Pillar 4 is therefore assigned secondarily because the proposal explicitly aims to engage developers.

Other pillars are less relevant. Pillar 2, Adoption & Utility, targets user-facing applications and industry verticals, which this proposal does not address as its primary purpose. Pillar 3, Governance, and Pillar 5, Sustainability & Resilience, concern on-chain governance processes and treasury/tokenomics, which are not the proposal’s focus.
