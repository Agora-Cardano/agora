## AI Rationale

The Indigo Innovation proposal’s primary focus is on delivering enterprise-grade financial primitives: tokenized real-world assets, Bitcoin DeFi integration, and privacy-enabled assets. These objectives map directly to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, which emphasizes high-value industry verticals such as DeFi and RWA.

The proposal aims to bring an institutional-grade tokenized RWA solution to the Cardano ecosystem through iUSDt, while also linking Cardano with institutional liquidity funds, powering Bitcoin DeFi markets on Indigo, and developing shielded iAssets on partnerchain ecosystems. These goals align directly with Pillar 2’s focus on non-speculative utility, high-value verticals, institutional-grade liquidity, tokenized assets, and enterprise-ready use cases.

The proposal also introduces a revenue-sharing model that returns 10% of net new protocol revenue from the funded products to the Cardano Treasury. This creates a direct and substantial secondary alignment with Pillar 5: Sustainability & Resilience, because the mechanism is designed to support the long-term financial health of the ecosystem through recurring Treasury revenue.

However, Pillar 5 is not the primary pillar because the revenue-sharing framework depends on the successful deployment and adoption of the proposed financial products. The proposal’s main strategic purpose is not treasury management in isolation, but the creation of new RWA, BTC-Fi, and privacy-enabled utility on Cardano. Therefore, Adoption & Utility is the strongest primary classification.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s core deliverables are financial products and infrastructure intended to create direct utility on Cardano: tokenized real-world assets, Bitcoin DeFi collateral rails, and privacy-enabled assets. These deliverables fit Pillar 2’s emphasis on high-value verticals, including DeFi, RWA, payments, enterprise-grade security, and non-speculative usage.

Sustainability & Resilience is a valid Secondary Pillar because the V2030RS revenue-sharing mechanism is not merely incidental. It is explicitly built into the proposal and commits a portion of net new protocol revenue from the funded products back to the Cardano Treasury on a perpetual basis. This supports the long-term financial health of the ecosystem.

Pillar 4 was not selected as a Secondary Pillar. Although the proposal mentions open-source tooling, RWA-specific SDKs, and a Treasury dashboard, these elements appear supportive of the main product and revenue objectives rather than forming a substantial standalone community or ecosystem-growth workstream. The proposal does not primarily focus on talent acquisition, education, localized adoption, community engagement, or structured contributor retention.

Pillar 1 was not selected because the proposal does not primarily focus on base-layer protocol improvements, scalability, L2 integration as core infrastructure, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, or threat detection and recovery at the Cardano protocol level.

Pillar 3 was not selected because the proposal does not primarily address governance participation, governance accessibility tools, turnout-aware voting, DRep incentives, delegator safeguards, or treasury season design.
