## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to drive real-world, non-speculative Cardano usage through regulated real-world asset infrastructure, institutional onboarding, recurring lifecycle transactions, marketplace participation, and Cardano-native TVL growth. This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, which focuses on high-value verticals such as RWA, payments, enterprise-grade security, compliance, and reducing adoption friction. The Vision framework describes Pillar 2 as focused on “widespread, non-speculative utility” through high-value verticals and enterprise-ready usage patterns, which is exactly the proposal’s center of gravity. 

Although the proposal includes technical work such as CIP-113 integration, APIs, SDKs, operational tooling, and marketplace infrastructure, these are primarily enabling mechanisms for institutional RWA adoption rather than protocol-level scalability, core research, client diversity, L2 integration, post-quantum readiness, or base-layer resilience. So no secondary pillar is assigned. The proposal also mentions long-term sustainability and possible treasury-aligned fee mechanisms, but those are not direct enough to qualify as a substantial Pillar 5 objective under the Vision 2030 definition.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Libertum brings “live institutional-grade financial infrastructure to Cardano” and focuses on “regulated asset issuance,” “institutional onboarding,” “recurring settlement and lifecycle transactions,” “marketplace activity,” and “compliance workflows.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies success metrics tied directly to Vision 2030 adoption KPIs, including “Cardano-native TVL growth,” “recurring transactions,” “issuer onboarding,” “institutional participation,” and “long-term production usage of regulated infrastructure.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal’s stated KPI targets include “$50M–$150M additional assets onboarded onto Cardano infrastructure,” “50,000+ recurring operational transactions generated annually,” “5–10 institutional issuers onboarded,” and “1,000+ investor and participant wallets onboarded through regulated workflows.” 

### Evidence 4

WP2 is explicitly designed to deploy “Cardano-native regulated issuance infrastructure,” operationalize “issuer and investor onboarding,” and generate “recurring Cardano-native transaction activity through lifecycle participation and regulated operational infrastructure.” 

### Evidence 5

WP4 focuses on creating a “Cardano Institutional RWA Adoption Programme” to reduce onboarding friction for regulated issuers and enterprise participants deploying production use cases on Cardano.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected because the proposal’s core justification is to create measurable Cardano-native usage through regulated RWA issuance, institutional onboarding, recurring transactions, marketplace participation, and enterprise-grade compliance infrastructure.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the technical components are application and operational infrastructure serving RWA adoption. The proposal does not primarily target L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, core ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or base-layer threat recovery.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal’s sustainability claims are mostly downstream effects of adoption: recurring activity, retained issuers, marketplace fees, and possible treasury-aligned mechanisms. The Vision 2030 Pillar 5 definition is more specific: treasury management, multi-asset treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, and decentralization resilience. The proposal does not directly deliver those.

### Additional Notes

The proposal has multiple work packages, but they all support the same strategic objective: institution-grade RWA adoption and recurring economic activity on Cardano. Therefore, manual review is not required.
