## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to build an integrated governance and treasury coordination platform for Cardano-native organisations. Its core framing is not merely software delivery, but a governance system connecting organisational purpose, contribution tracking, consent-based decisions, treasury actions, and impact assessment.

The strongest alignment is with **Pillar 3: Governance**, because the proposal directly develops governance workflows, organisational roles, consent-based decision processes, impact assessments, and governance-to-treasury links for DReps, SPOs, DAOs, and member-led Cardano organisations. This maps most directly to the Vision 2030 emphasis on accessible, usable, resilient governance.

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal also contains a substantial workstream focused on real operational use of Cardano: contributor reward distributions, on-chain metadata, wallet-linked receipts, bundle transactions, mainnet pilots, and recurring non-speculative transaction activity. This is direct enough to support a secondary classification, but it does not override the proposal’s dominant governance purpose.

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with distinct strategic purposes: governance tooling, treasury/reward utility, conditional side-chain scalability, and external oversight.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “combines the Swarm Treasury System with Sociocracy 3.0 (S3) governance patterns into a single integrated platform,” where organisations can “define their purpose and structure,” manage contribution data, execute treasury distributions, and run impact assessments.

### Evidence 2

The proposal describes the intended outcome as “a cohesive governance layer” where “diverse voices and perspectives are visibly recognised,” contributions are traceable, and decentralised organisations share “a common foundation for deciding and acting together.”

### Evidence 3

WP2 explicitly aims to “build Sociocracy 3.0 (S3) governance features integrated with the treasury platform,” including driver/domain/role management, consent-based workflows, impact assessment, and governance decisions that can trigger treasury actions.

### Evidence 4

The proposal defines WP2 objectives including replacing “binary on-chain voting with a consent-based decision workflow,” anchoring governance artefacts on-chain, and piloting real governance and reward distributions with three organisations.

### Evidence 5

The secondary Adoption & Utility alignment is supported by WP1, which aims to run contributor reward distributions and treasury operations on Cardano, execute bundle transactions with CIP-compliant metadata, reward at least 200 unique contributor wallets, and complete at least 100 bundle transactions during the pilot.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Governance** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s central strategic purpose is to create a shared governance operating layer for Cardano-native organisations. The governance layer is not incidental: it includes S3 organisational structures, consent-based decision workflows, on-chain governance artefacts, impact assessment, and governance-to-treasury integration.

**Adoption & Utility** is a valid Secondary Pillar because WP1 creates practical, recurring Cardano usage through treasury operations, rewards distribution, contributor dashboards, and mainnet pilots. However, these utility features appear to serve the broader governance system rather than define the proposal’s highest-level purpose.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as Primary or Secondary because the side-chain scalability workstream is conditional and supporting. It is relevant, but it does not best explain why the full proposal exists.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected because public workshops, pilots, documentation, and community review are supporting delivery methods rather than the proposal’s dominant strategic purpose.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal discusses organisational sustainability and treasury operations, but it does not primarily target the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus areas: multi-asset treasury, managed treasury strategy, L2-to-L1 value retention, or SPO incentives.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes. WP1 aligns substantially with Adoption & Utility, WP2 aligns strongly with Governance, WP3 conditionally aligns with Infrastructure & Research, and WP4 provides oversight and assurance. The dominant classification is Governance, but the proposal could reasonably be debated due to its combined treasury, governance, software, and scalability scope.

### Additional Notes

The proposal self-identifies alignment with multiple pillars. This classification does not rely on the proposer’s selected pillars alone; it prioritizes the proposal’s core objectives, deliverables, milestones, target users, and expected outcomes against the Vision 2030 pillar descriptions.
