## AI Rationale

The proposal’s main purpose is to improve Cardano developer experience through a shared compiler framework, better documentation, better error messages, examples, tests, simplified interfaces, and compatibility with multi-chain tooling. Conveniently, the Vision 2030 framework places “Developer Experience” inside Pillar 2, including open-source incentives, education/migration, and compatibility with common multi-chain tooling. The proposal’s core deliverables map directly to those areas.

Although the work is technical and infrastructure-like, its strategic purpose is not L1 protocol improvement, node diversity, L2 integration, security hardening, or protocol-level interoperability. The proposal is primarily about making Cardano smart-contract tooling easier to adopt, easier to maintain, and more compatible with external developer workflows.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Covenant “sits between high-level smart contract languages” such as Aiken, Plutarch, and Plinth and the scripts executed by Cardano, handling “analysis, optimization, and code generation” so language maintainers can focus on user-facing tooling rather than the whole compilation pipeline. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal explicitly says Covenant 2 will “improve the developer experience” through “better error messages, simpler interfaces, more examples, more tests, and clearer documentation.” These deliverables directly match the Vision 2030 Pillar 2 Developer Experience focus area.  

### Evidence 3

The proposal says Covenant will be extended to support languages beyond Cardano and become “universal glue” between high-level smart contract languages and different chains, while the Vision 2030 Pillar 2 Developer Experience section includes “Compatibility” with common multi-chain tooling to lower switching costs versus other L1s.  

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s stated objectives include making Covenant easier to adopt and contribute to, reducing the maintenance burden for Cardano language maintainers, establishing Covenant as target-agnostic, and demonstrating cross-chain compatibility through c2sbpf. These are developer tooling and compatibility objectives, not governance, treasury management, SPO incentives, community marketing, or protocol-layer security objectives.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility was selected because the Vision 2030 framework explicitly includes Developer Experience under Pillar 2, covering open-source incentives, education/migration, and compatibility. The proposal’s main deliverables are open-source developer tooling, documentation, examples, Hackage releases, c2uplc/c2sbpf improvements, and compatibility work for cross-chain development.

Infrastructure & Research is a plausible surface-level candidate because the work is technical compiler infrastructure and includes cross-chain functionality. However, Vision 2030’s Pillar 1 is focused on protocol scalability, L1 improvements, L2 integration, secure bridges/state-proofs, ZK capabilities, client diversity, and security resilience. This proposal does not primarily improve the Cardano base protocol, node/client diversity, L2 settlement, bridge standards, or protocol security. Its cross-chain component is developer-tool compatibility, which the Vision framework places more directly under Pillar 2.

No Secondary Pillar is assigned because the potential Pillar 1 relationship is indirect and tooling-mediated. The proposal may contribute to broader ecosystem infrastructure but its substantial objectives remain inside Pillar 2’s Developer Experience and Compatibility areas.

### Additional Notes

The proposal includes metrics such as repository activity, external framework adoption, issue/PR quality, adoption by language maintainers, and existence of c2sbpf as a verified compilation target. These are useful delivery and adoption indicators, but they do not shift the classification away from Pillar 2.
