## AI Rationale

The proposal’s dominant strategic purpose is to deploy and operate cross-chain infrastructure for Cardano. Its largest work package is the **“Infrastructure, Integration & 24-Month Operational Support”** package, and its core objectives focus on delivering the Wormhole messaging stack on Cardano, including **Core Contracts in Aiken, NTT, Token Bridge, Executor/Relayer infrastructure, SDK/CLI support, WormholeScan indexing, security audit coordination, and ongoing operational support**. This aligns most directly with Vision 2030 Pillar 1, which is about keeping Cardano **“secure, fast, and interoperable”** and specifically includes **cross-chain interoperability** through secure bridges and interoperability infrastructure.

A substantial secondary alignment exists with **Adoption & Utility** because the proposal also directly aims to bring **institutional RWAs, native multichain stablecoins, Bitcoin liquidity, multichain ADA, and bridge + swap / bridge + deposit flows** onto Cardano, with success metrics tied to transaction volume, stablecoin inflow, DeFi activity, and partner activation. Those are concrete utility-oriented outcomes, but they are downstream of, and dependent on, the infrastructure deployment that defines the proposal’s main reason for existing.

Manual review is required because the proposal contains **multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes**: a large technical interoperability build-out and a separate ecosystem-activation incentives package. That creates a real, though not equal, classification boundary between Pillar 1 and Pillar 2.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s central deliverable is the creation of interoperable, production-grade cross-chain infrastructure on Cardano. Vision 2030 Pillar 1 explicitly covers making Cardano **interoperable** and identifies **“Cross-chain interoperability”** as a focus area, with the stated strategy to **“Standardize secure bridges and state-proofs to other chains and legacy infra”** so Cardano can serve as **“an interoperability hub.”** The proposal’s largest budget allocation, most concrete milestones, and most foundational deliverables all map to that purpose.

**Adoption & Utility** is a strong Secondary Pillar because the proposal directly supports utility outcomes identified in Vision 2030 Pillar 2, especially through institutional RWAs, stablecoins, payments-like cross-chain flows, and user-facing bridge + swap / bridge + deposit experiences. However, those outcomes are enabled by the interoperability stack rather than replacing it as the proposal’s main strategic purpose. If the proposal could be associated with only one pillar, Pillar 1 best justifies its existence.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected as the Secondary Pillar even though the proposal includes co-marketing and activation programs, because those activities are framed primarily as mechanisms to drive **transaction volume, DeFi participation, and asset usage**, not as talent development, education, or broad ecosystem narrative-building of the type emphasized in Pillar 4. **Governance** and **Sustainability & Resilience** were also not selected because quarterly reporting, treasury fee generation, and repayment of unused incentive funds are supportive features, not the proposal’s direct strategic purpose under the Vision 2030 pillar definitions.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains **two major workstreams with different strategic purposes**: a **technical interoperability and infrastructure work package** and a **non-technical ecosystem activation and liquidity-incentive work package**. This makes **Infrastructure & Research** the strongest classification, but also leaves **Adoption & Utility** as a materially plausible alternative emphasis.
