## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core mission is to scale Cardano adoption by accelerating the development of startups and high-value use cases on the network. Its objectives emphasize onboarding users, integrating Cardano assets into real-world financial instruments, and driving on-chain activity, all hallmarks of **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**.

The proposal explicitly describes actions like supporting 1 million monthly users, targeting strategic verticals, and building adoption infrastructure on Cardano. While it also includes community/developer training and funding strategies relating to Pillars 4 and 5, these serve the primary goal of boosting utility and usage.

Pillar 2 best captures the proposal’s focus on expanding non-speculative network usage and industry adoption.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal’s main deliverables and milestones, including startup accelerators, developer pipeline development, adoption programs, and founder support, are aimed at increasing Cardano’s real-world usage and utility. This aligns directly with Pillar 2’s goal of driving widespread, industry-focused utility.

References to onboarding users, targeting high-value sectors, and integrating native assets show that the proposal is primarily adoption-oriented.

Other pillars, especially Community & Ecosystem Growth, appear as supporting elements. The proposal does include developer training, founder education, global outreach, and ecosystem-building activities, but these support the primary adoption mission rather than define it.

Pillars such as Infrastructure & Research, Governance, or Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience are not central to the proposal’s main objectives. Therefore, Adoption & Utility is the strongest primary pillar match.

### Manual Review Reason

Not required. The proposal clearly emphasizes adoption-focused goals, making the Pillar 2 classification straightforward and unambiguous.
