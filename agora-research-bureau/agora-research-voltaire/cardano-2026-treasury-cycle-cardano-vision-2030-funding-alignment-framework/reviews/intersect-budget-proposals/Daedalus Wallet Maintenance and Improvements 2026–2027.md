## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is maintaining and improving Daedalus as Cardano’s full-node desktop wallet, with emphasis on protocol compatibility, hard fork readiness, node and wallet backend updates, release security, platform reliability, and contingency audit capacity. That maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, especially the Vision 2030 emphasis on keeping Cardano secure, fast, interoperable, and resilient enough to host more economic activity. The reference framework places security, resilience, client diversity, protocol stability, L1/L2 readiness, and interoperability under Pillar 1, which is the cleanest fit here. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because a major work package adds CIP-30 dApp connectivity, hardware wallet support, Electron upgrades, and user-facing improvements that expand the utility of Daedalus for full-node users.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Daedalus is “Cardano’s only full-node desktop wallet” and that it “runs an embedded Cardano node and derives all wallet and governance data directly from the chain, with no third-party APIs or trusted backends.” This supports Infrastructure & Research because the proposal is centered on maintaining a self-sovereign full-node access path. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal’s first major workstream is “Protocol Maintenance & Hard Fork Readiness,” covering node upgrades, hard fork readiness for Leios, Peras, and Nested Transactions, and a compatible release at least two weeks before every mainnet hard fork. This directly supports protocol stability, resilience, and infrastructure continuity. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal identifies core objectives including “Zero-Disruption Hard Fork Transitions,” “Leios/Peras Compatibility,” “Maintained Security Posture,” and “Multi-Platform Reliability.” These are infrastructure-maintenance objectives rather than direct market adoption, governance participation, or community growth objectives. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal includes an “Ecosystem Expansion” work package with Electron 41, Ledger Flex and Keystone hardware wallet support, and a CIP-30 dApp connector that allows Daedalus users to access dApps without switching to a browser wallet. This provides direct but secondary alignment with Adoption & Utility. 

### Evidence 5

The Vision 2030 framework places “Security & Resilience,” “Client diversity,” “Threat detection & recovery,” and protocol stability under Infrastructure & Research, while Adoption & Utility includes user experience, enterprise-ready usage, and developer experience. The Daedalus proposal touches both, but its maintenance and protocol-readiness functions are the more fundamental justification.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Infrastructure & Research was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s core purpose is to maintain and upgrade a critical Cardano software component that embeds a full node, stays compatible with protocol changes, supports hard fork transitions, maintains build and release infrastructure, and preserves security posture.

Adoption & Utility was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily aim to drive a specific high-value vertical, enterprise deployment, payment use case, RWA use case, or broad user acquisition campaign. Its adoption-related elements, especially CIP-30 and hardware wallet support, are substantial enough for a Secondary Pillar, but they are built on top of the underlying infrastructure maintenance work.

Community & Ecosystem Growth was not selected because Japanese localization and ecosystem accessibility are present but not the dominant strategic purpose. Governance was not selected because governance support appears incidental to Daedalus functionality, not the proposal’s main strategic objective. Sustainability & Resilience was not selected because the proposal is not primarily about treasury management, tokenomics, SPO incentives, or long-term network economics.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required is set to Yes because the proposal contains multiple substantial workstreams with partially different strategic purposes: protocol maintenance and hard fork readiness align most strongly with Infrastructure & Research, while CIP-30 dApp connectivity and hardware wallet support create a meaningful Adoption & Utility component. The primary classification is still reasonably clear, but the secondary workstream is large enough to justify review.

### Additional Notes

The proposer’s own stated alignment also identifies Pillar 1 as primary and Pillar 2 as secondary, but this classification is based on the proposal content and the Vision 2030 framework rather than accepting the proposer’s selection automatically.
