## AI Rationale

The proposal is primarily an Adoption & Utility initiative because its core strategic purpose is to activate a regulated, non-speculative real-world industry vertical on Cardano: clinical research. Its main objectives are not to improve Cardano’s base protocol, consensus, node diversity, L2 interoperability, or core cryptographic infrastructure. Instead, the proposal aims to onboard sponsors, CROs, patients, regulators, investors, and clinical trial workflows into Cardano-based payment, document provenance, settlement, and compliance systems.

The proposal directly maps to Pillar 2’s focus on high-value verticals, enterprise-grade security/compliance, and real-world utility. It explicitly targets clinical research as a regulated vertical, includes a sponsor pilot, stablecoin stipend disbursement, document anchoring, AXC-based platform fee settlement, and projected contributions to monthly active users and transaction volume. Humanity, somehow, did manage to produce one proposal where the adoption thesis is not just vibes in a trench coat.

The technical work packages are substantial, but they are application-layer enablers for the clinical research use case. They do not directly correspond to Pillar 1’s official focus areas such as L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat detection. Therefore, Infrastructure & Research is not assigned as a Secondary Pillar under the official Vision 2030 taxonomy.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes Ecosera as “a live clinical research OS” covering EDC, eTMF, PHR, RWE ingestion, IRB workflows, and patient-trial matching, connecting “sponsors, CROs, patients, regulators, and investors” in a verifiable ecosystem.

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that it funds six components: stablecoin patient stipend escrow, RWD/document provenance anchoring, a stablecoin settlement adapter, FDA-aligned sponsor pilot integration, audit/legal/project management, and an integration layer connecting Ecosera with Libertum’s RWA tokenization and ResearchDAO governance.

### Evidence 3

The proposal claims that every sponsor contract, site onboarding, document anchoring, and patient stipend disbursement generates AXC transactions on Cardano, creating “a continuous, self-sustaining stream of real economic activity on-chain.”

### Evidence 4

WP4 is explicitly focused on onboarding EOM Pharmaceuticals as Ecosera’s first commercial sponsor pilot, integrating Cardano-native components into a live trial, and publishing transparency reporting to the Cardano community.

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s KPI section targets Monthly Active Users, Monthly Transaction Volume, Native Token Utility, Developer Ecosystem Growth, and Enterprise Adoption Signal, including projected sponsor pilots, monthly transaction activity, and new monthly active wallets/addresses.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is the strongest classification because the proposal’s strategic justification depends on bringing a regulated real-world vertical onto Cardano and generating recurring on-chain activity through clinical trial workflows. The proposal’s intended users are not primarily Cardano protocol engineers or core infrastructure maintainers; they are sponsors, CROs, patients, regulators, research investors, and healthcare/clinical research stakeholders.

Infrastructure & Research was not selected as Primary or Secondary because the technical deliverables are mostly app-layer components: Aiken contracts, SDKs, APIs, provenance anchoring, and settlement adapters. These may be reusable by other builders, but they do not directly advance the official Pillar 1 focus areas of protocol scalability, L2 integration, interoperability, core ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or network threat detection.

Governance was not selected because ResearchDAO governance is part of the proposed clinical research funding lifecycle, not a direct improvement to Cardano’s DRep, Constitutional Committee, SPO, treasury season, turnout, or delegation governance systems.

Community & Ecosystem Growth was not selected because outreach, documentation, and ecosystem events are supporting activities, not the proposal’s main strategic purpose.

Sustainability & Resilience was not selected because the revenue-share and repayment mechanisms may affect Treasury value capture, but the proposal does not primarily implement multi-asset treasury management, active treasury strategy, SPO incentive diversification, or L2-to-L1 value retention as defined by Pillar 5.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains significant technical work, but under the official Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this technical work is best understood as a means to enable regulated industry adoption rather than as protocol-level infrastructure. The adoption case is unusually explicit because the proposal includes a defined vertical, named sponsor pilot, transaction-generation mechanisms, and KPI claims tied to MAU and monthly transaction activity.
