## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core objective is to expand real-world use of Cardano by bringing a large Web2 ticketing platform onto the blockchain. It explicitly targets **“mass adoption”** by upgrading Sellout’s existing 200,000+ user base to a Cardano-native ticketing system. This aligns directly with Pillar 2, Adoption & Utility, which emphasizes “widespread, non-speculative utility” in high-value industries.

The proposal focuses on developing enterprise-grade features such as anti-scalping controls, an on-chain marketplace, user onboarding, organizer tools, and a marketing campaign aimed at broadening Cardano’s usage. In contrast, it does not propose protocol research or core infrastructure upgrades, nor does it involve governance mechanisms or general community programs. The financial elements, including revenue share and treasury repayment, serve the adoption focus rather than being the proposal’s primary strategic purpose.

Thus, the proposal’s strategic purpose — deploying Cardano into an existing large ticketing ecosystem — maps most directly to **Adoption & Utility**.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal is primarily about **driving adoption** of Cardano through a large enterprise use case. It invests in building and marketing a production-ready Cardano ticketing system for Sellout’s existing user base. This matches Pillar 2’s focus on industry verticals, non-speculative utility, user onboarding, and enterprise-grade use cases.

By contrast, the proposal contains no research or core protocol work, no on-chain governance mechanism, and no broad community development initiative. While it includes financial mechanisms such as revenue share and treasury repayment, these support the adoption strategy rather than indicating a primary focus on network sustainability.

Therefore, Adoption & Utility is the best fit.
