## AI Rationale

The proposal is primarily aligned with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** because its core purpose is to create and deploy a Real-World Asset tokenization framework on Cardano, with live pilots for real estate and agricultural assets, external investor onboarding, user participation, and recurring on-chain activity. The Cardano Vision 2030 framework explicitly identifies **RWA tokenization** as a high-value vertical under Adoption & Utility, including the tokenization of illiquid assets such as real estate. 

Although the proposal frames itself as Pillar 5, most funded work packages focus on building RWA infrastructure, legal/compliance templates, onboarding real assets, deploying to mainnet, enabling token issuance, and attracting users and partners. These activities are more directly explained by Adoption & Utility than by Pillar 5’s core focus on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, and SPO incentives. 

A **Secondary Pillar** is assigned to **Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** because the proposal includes an explicit mechanism to return up to 15% of net annual profits to the Cardano Treasury through on-chain donations. That said, this is secondary and conditional on profitability, while the main body of the proposal is still RWA adoption and utility.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will deliver “a fully open-source Real-World Asset (RWA) tokenization framework on Cardano,” built using CIP-68 and CIP-113, and intended to standardize “the creation, deployment, and growth of RWA projects” across asset classes. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies real deployments as part of the model: “a Vierci Development real estate project and three cattle feedlots across Paraguay and Ecuador,” with the goal of onboarding established businesses and existing customer bases. 

### Evidence 3

WP5 is explicitly focused on live usage: it “deploys the platform to mainnet and executes tokenization for onboarded assets,” including token minting, participation enablement, lifecycle validation, user onboarding, and public reporting. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal commits to measurable adoption-oriented outcomes, including “$200K–$250K in net-new external capital imported into Cardano,” “~$500K in mintable productive assets,” “2 new productive RWA asset classes,” and “2 new jurisdictions on-chain.” 

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes a treasury return mechanism: “up to 15% of realized net profits from both the platform and onboarded pilot operations will be transferred to the Cardano Treasury via donation transactions.”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to create real-world utility on Cardano through RWA tokenization, external user onboarding, asset issuance, and live economic activity. This maps directly to the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility pillar, especially its RWA vertical.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as Primary because the infrastructure being built is application-level RWA infrastructure, not core protocol scalability, interoperability, L2 integration, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat recovery infrastructure as defined under Pillar 1.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as Primary because the treasury return mechanism and sustainability claims are secondary to the proposal’s main execution path. The proposal may contribute to treasury sustainability through profit-sharing, but the funded work is mainly about RWA deployment and adoption rather than direct treasury management, SPO incentives, or L2-to-L1 value retention.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably appear to fit multiple pillars: it self-identifies as Pillar 5, includes application infrastructure and open-source frameworks, and contains direct RWA adoption deliverables. The dominant operational purpose is Adoption & Utility, but the proposal contains enough sustainability language and a treasury-return mechanism to justify human review.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own selected pillar is **Pillar 5**, but the classification here prioritizes the actual objectives, work packages, deliverables, target users, and expected outcomes over the proposer’s self-classification. Under the Vision 2030 taxonomy, RWA tokenization is explicitly positioned under Adoption & Utility, which makes Pillar 2 the stronger primary fit.
