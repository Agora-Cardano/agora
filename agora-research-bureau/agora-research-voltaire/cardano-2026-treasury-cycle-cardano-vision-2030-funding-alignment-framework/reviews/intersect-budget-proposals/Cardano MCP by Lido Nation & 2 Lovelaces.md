## AI Rationale

The proposal’s broad form is a technical AI developer MCP for Cardano, but its most explicitly differentiated and strategically central contribution is the 26-tool governance and DRep workbench. The proposal states that the governance module is its “defining contribution,” aimed at reducing DRep workload through proposal summaries, sentiment analysis, ratification forecasting, expiry tracking, and CIP-100-compliant rationale drafting. That maps most directly to the Vision 2030 Governance pillar, especially governance accessibility and DRep participation.

A strong secondary alignment exists with Adoption & Utility because the proposal also funds production-grade developer tooling across query, transaction building, tokens/NFTs, smart contracts, indexing, documentation, tutorials, npm distribution, and MCP registry listing. The Vision 2030 framework places Developer Experience under Adoption & Utility, including open-source tooling, onboarding materials, and compatibility with common tooling. 

However, the proposal contains two substantial strategic purposes: AI-assisted developer tooling and governance/DRep tooling. Because either Governance or Adoption & Utility could reasonably be argued as the primary pillar, confidence is Low and manual review is required.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal says it funds the production build of `cardano-mcp` into “54 production-grade tools across six modules: query, transaction building, tokens and NFTs, smart contracts, indexing, and a 26-tool governance and DRep workbench built specifically for CIP-1694 participation.” This shows both the broader developer-tooling scope and the major governance workstream. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states: “The governance module is the proposal's defining contribution,” and describes reducing DRep workload by enabling AI agents to read proposals, analyze sentiment, forecast ratification, track expiry, and draft CIP-100-compliant vote rationales. This directly supports Governance as the primary classification. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal identifies DRep participation rate as “the single KPI Cardano MCP is most directly engineered to move,” and says the governance module attacks voter fatigue, time cost, and rationale-writing burden. This maps directly to the Vision 2030 Governance KPI for DRep participation and governance accessibility. 

### Evidence 4

WP2 includes production builds of all 26 governance and DRep tools, including summaries, sentiment and voting bloc analysis, `draft_vote_rationale`, `get_my_voting_queue`, treasury intelligence, and proposal alerts, all working against live mainnet governance data. This is a substantial governance deliverable, not merely an incidental feature. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal also includes production-grade developer infrastructure: npm package publication, API clients for Koios, Blockfrost, and Maestro, CBOR datum decoding, CI/CD, documentation, tutorials, and MCP registry listing. This supports Adoption & Utility as a secondary pillar through Developer Experience, which the Vision 2030 framework explicitly places under Pillar 2.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Governance was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s most explicit differentiator is the governance and DRep module. The proposal itself calls this module its “defining contribution” and identifies DRep participation as the KPI it most directly targets. The intended governance outcomes are concrete: reducing DRep workload, improving proposal analysis, preventing missed votes, supporting rationale drafting, validating tools against live governance cycles, and integrating with GovTool and Tempo.vote.

Adoption & Utility was not selected as the Primary Pillar despite the proposal’s strong developer tooling component because the stated adoption effects, such as more developers shipping applications, higher transaction volume, and higher MAU, are mostly indirect. The developer tooling work is substantial enough for a Secondary Pillar, especially under Developer Experience, but the most direct strategic impact described in the proposal is governance participation and governance workflow support.

Community & Ecosystem Growth was not selected as Secondary because the outreach, documentation, workshops, and contributor onboarding appear to support adoption and governance delivery rather than forming a separate primary strategic purpose. They are execution mechanisms, not the core justification of the proposal. Harsh, but taxonomy is not a participation trophy.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual Review Required = Yes because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple Primary Pillars. It contains two major workstreams with different strategic purposes: a general AI developer MCP aligned with Adoption & Utility, and a governance/DRep workbench aligned with Governance. The proposal’s own framing emphasizes Adoption & Utility, while its strongest direct KPI and “defining contribution” point toward Governance.
