## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to fund 12 months of maintenance and enhancement for Plutarch and Ply, two developer-facing smart contract tools used by Cardano builders. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this maps most directly to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, specifically A.3 Developer Experience, which includes incentivizing maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure in line with open-source best practices. 

Although the work is technical, its direct strategic function is not L1 protocol improvement, L2 integration, client diversity, ZK infrastructure, or protocol security research. The proposal is about keeping developer tooling reliable, compatible, documented, and usable so application builders can continue maintaining smart contracts. That makes Adoption & Utility the strongest official Vision 2030 classification.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it “seeks annual funding for the maintenance and enhancement of Plutarch and Ply,” describing Plutarch as a Haskell eDSL for efficient Cardano smart contracts and Ply as tooling for serializing Plutarch scripts to and from CIP-57 blueprint-style artifacts. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the funded work will prioritize “critical breakages and serious vulnerabilities,” “protocol-era and hard-fork compatibility,” “bug fixes, correctness improvements, and optimizations,” and “documentation, examples, technical blog posts, and developer-experience improvements.” 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly identifies its own strongest Vision 2030 alignment as Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, because it “directly supports A.3 Developer Experience,” especially maintenance of core Cardano open-source SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure. 

### Evidence 4

The work package objective is to “keep Plutarch and Ply reliable and usable for existing Cardano smart contract teams” and to maintain compatibility with “Cardano ledger, Plutus, UPLC, and hard-fork-era evolution,” reducing migration risk and avoidable rewrites. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that Plutarch and Ply are “developer tools rather than user-facing products,” and that success will be measured through maintenance, compatibility, documentation, developer-experience outputs, issue fixes, optimizations, and quarterly maintainer reviews.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility was selected because the proposal’s core purpose is developer experience: maintaining open-source smart contract tooling so Cardano builders can write, compile, serialize, test, and maintain production on-chain applications.

Infrastructure & Research was not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily improve the L1 protocol, consensus, networking, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, full-node client diversity, or threat recovery systems. Its compatibility work responds to protocol evolution, but it does not itself constitute protocol-layer infrastructure research or core network hardening.

No Secondary Pillar is assigned because any Infrastructure & Research relevance is indirect and not substantial enough under the provided rules. The direct workstream remains developer tooling maintenance under Pillar 2.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains a single major work package focused on Plutarch and Ply maintenance, compatibility, and developer experience. The strategic purpose is clear, so manual review is not required.
