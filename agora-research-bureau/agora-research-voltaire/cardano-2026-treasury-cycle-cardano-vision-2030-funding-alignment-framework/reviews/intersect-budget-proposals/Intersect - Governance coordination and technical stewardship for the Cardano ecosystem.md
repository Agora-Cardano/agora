## AI Rationale

The strongest fit is **Infrastructure & Research** because the proposal’s most concrete and largest workstream is technical stewardship of Cardano’s core infrastructure: stewardship of the core Haskell repositories, protocol upgrade and release coordination, incident response, security initiatives such as bug bounties, repository governance, and coordination across a growing multi-client environment. In the Vision 2030 framework, Pillar 1 is the pillar for keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” and it explicitly includes security and resilience functions such as client diversity and threat detection and recovery. Those definitions align more directly with the proposal’s central technical objectives and deliverables than any other pillar.

A **Secondary Pillar of Governance** is also warranted because the proposal contains substantial and explicit governance work, including operation of the member-based organization, support for committees and working groups, board and committee elections, governance participation processes, and technical support for governance-related operations. However, those governance functions do not outweigh the proposal’s dominant technical stewardship purpose, especially because the largest work package is the infrastructure-focused WP2.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it funds “technical stewardship, incident response, and release coordination” needed to keep Cardano “secure, reliable, and operationally resilient,” which directly points to infrastructure security and operational resilience rather than adoption or community promotion.

### Evidence 2

WP2 says it will “steward the core Cardano Haskell repositories, coordinate protocol upgrades and releases, support governance-critical technical processes, and lead incident response across the ecosystem.” This is a direct match with Pillar 1’s focus on protocol reliability, client diversity, and threat detection and recovery.

### Evidence 3

WP2’s core objectives include maintaining “stability, security, and clear lifecycle management” for the core Haskell node and repositories, strengthening technical coordination across “multiple organizations, contributors, and client implementations,” and improving resilience through “bug bounties,” escalation pathways, and security practices. These are core infrastructure objectives.

### Evidence 4

The proposal also contains a substantial governance stream: WP1 is designed to “enable effective participation in ecosystem governance,” facilitate committees and working groups, and support elections and governance processes. That supports a genuine secondary alignment with the Governance pillar, but the proposal’s largest budgeted work package is WP2 at 18.8M ADA, compared with 6.0M ADA for WP1 and 0.6M ADA for WP3, reinforcing that technical stewardship is the dominant purpose.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was chosen over **Governance** because the Vision 2030 Governance pillar is specifically framed around incentivized and accessible governance, turnout-aware voting, delegator safeguards, and treasury seasons, while the proposal’s most central deliverables are stewardship of the core node and repositories, release management, incident response, security practices, and technical ecosystem coordination. Governance support is substantial, but it functions alongside the proposal’s larger and more operationally central infrastructure mandate.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as the primary pillar because the authoritative Vision 2030 definition of that pillar is mainly about financial stewardship and tokenomics, including a multi-asset treasury, managed treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention, and SPO incentive design. The proposal does mention resilience and continuity, but its direct deliverables do not primarily center on treasury design, tokenomics, or SPO incentive architecture. That makes Pillar 5 a weaker fit than Pillar 1.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes: a governance coordination workstream in WP1, a clearly infrastructure-focused technical stewardship workstream in WP2, and a reserved coordination workstream for critical processes in WP3. Although WP2 appears dominant, the proposal could still reasonably be read as having meaningful primary overlap with Governance, so human review is appropriate under the stated framework rules.
