## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to make CIP-113 programmable tokens usable, secure, and accessible through Ledger’s hardware wallet and Ledger Wallet/Ledger Live product suite. Its deliverables focus on clear signing, wallet balance display, token discovery, send/receive flows, transaction history, and user-facing integration for both retail and institutional users.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, especially its emphasis on superior user experience, enterprise-grade security, and reducing adoption friction. The proposal is not primarily about Cardano protocol scalability, node/client diversity, treasury management, SPO incentives, governance participation, or community education. 

Although the proposal mentions **Pillar 5: Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience**, the provided Vision 2030 description of Pillar 5 is focused on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, and SPO incentives. The proposal does not contain substantial objectives or deliverables directly supporting those areas. Therefore, no Secondary Pillar is assigned.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will integrate “Cardano’s CIP-113 token standard into the entire Ledger product suite,” including Ledger Signer, Ledger Live/Wallet, and Ledger Enterprise, so that Cardano’s token ecosystem becomes “accessible, secure, and transparent.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal identifies Clear Signing as a critical component because it lets users verify exactly what they are signing on the trusted device screen, eliminating “blind signing” risks for the new token standard.

### Evidence 3

The first work package aims to update the Ledger Cardano application, build clear signing logic for CIP-113 operations, support issuance, transfer, and third-party transfer hooks, preserve backward compatibility, and pass Ledger’s security audit and firmware certification process.

### Evidence 4

The second work package aims to update LedgerJS-Cardano, Ledger Live Desktop and Mobile, and the Crypto Assets List so users can discover CIP-113 tokens, view balances, send and receive tokens, and see human-readable transaction history.

### Evidence 5

The proposal states that “millions of Ledger Wallet users gain native, seamless access to the CIP-113 token ecosystem from day one,” explicitly framing the impact as removal of a major UX barrier to CIP-113 adoption.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the proposal is centered on making Cardano programmable tokens usable by end users, token issuers, and institutional participants through secure wallet support and improved UX.

The Vision 2030 reference describes Pillar 2 as focused on “widespread, non-speculative utility,” “superior user experience,” and “enterprise-grade security.” It also includes reducing friction through better user experience and supporting enterprise-ready trust and compliance patterns. The proposal’s clear signing, Ledger Wallet integration, dynamic token discovery, transaction labeling, and institutional/retail wallet support directly serve that purpose.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not modify Cardano’s core protocol, L1/L2 scalability, interoperability standards, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, node/client diversity, or disaster recovery. It is technical work, yes, but the Vision 2030 pillar fit is adoption-facing wallet utility rather than base-layer infrastructure.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not assigned as a Secondary Pillar because the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 areas are multi-asset treasury, managed treasury, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO role diversification, and decentralization targets. The proposal’s security and future-proofing benefits are real within the proposal text, but they do not directly map to those Pillar 5 focus areas.

### Additional Notes

The proposer’s self-selected alignment includes Pillar 2 and Pillar 5. Based strictly on the Vision 2030 pillar definitions, Pillar 2 is well supported, while Pillar 5 is not sufficiently direct or substantial to justify a Secondary Pillar.
