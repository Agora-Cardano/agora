## AI Rationale

The proposal aligns primarily with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** because its central purpose is to build, deploy, and validate a real-world institutional use case on Cardano. GOV.EXE is designed as an open-source platform for government public-project execution, using Cardano to anchor state transitions and create an auditable workflow across public-sector processes.

The proposal’s core deliverables are not protocol research, governance tooling, or treasury management. They are a functional Cardano-anchored platform, a government pilot in Entre Ríos, production usage data, on-chain state-transition records, and a replicable framework for future institutional deployments. This directly supports Cardano’s strategic goal of driving non-speculative utility in high-value real-world verticals. Humanity has apparently reached the point where procurement workflows need blockchains and AI agents to remember who approved what, but at least this proposal knows what problem it is trying to solve.

A **Secondary Pillar: Community & Ecosystem** is justified because the proposal includes substantial LATAM ecosystem engagement, builder working-group sessions, prospecting briefings, and localized adoption outputs. However, these are supporting mechanisms around the main pilot. They do not override the primary purpose, which is real-world institutional adoption and utility.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it is a “9-month pilot to build and validate GOV.EXE, an open-source platform that helps governments execute public projects with transparency and accountability, in a real government environment,” with the Secretariat of Modernization of the Province of Entre Ríos, Argentina serving as the institutional partner.

### Evidence 2

GOV.EXE is described as sitting above existing government systems and helping officials move public projects through five execution stages: “Mandate, Planning, Procurement, Payment, Closure.” The proposal states that “on-chain validators enforce the rules of progression, producing an audit trail no single institution can rewrite.”

### Evidence 3

WP2 aims to deliver a “functional platform on Cardano” that processes “real public-project workflows under the five-stage model, with state transitions anchored on Cardano.”

### Evidence 4

WP3 aims to run the platform “in production with government officials operating it on active workflows,” generating “real usage data,” “on-chain state-transition records,” and institutional feedback.

### Evidence 5

The proposal includes secondary ecosystem-facing outputs: a case study, replication framework, two builder working-group sessions, three LATAM prospecting briefings, and an aggregated lessons-learned document for Cardano builders pursuing localized adoption.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to create and validate a real-world, non-speculative Cardano use case in the public-sector digital government vertical. The proposal is centered on institutional deployment, government workflow execution, Cardano-anchored verification, real usage data, and measurable on-chain activity.

**Community & Ecosystem** is a valid secondary pillar because the proposal contains substantial localized adoption and ecosystem engagement components, especially in LATAM. The Argentina pilot, builder sessions, prospecting briefings, and replication materials directly support regional ecosystem growth. However, these activities exist to extend and disseminate the pilot’s adoption value; they are not the proposal’s core reason for existence.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily improve Cardano’s base-layer protocol, scalability, interoperability, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, or core infrastructure. Although it includes on-chain validators, audits, and open-source software, these are application-level components for a specific government use case.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal concerns public-sector project execution integrity, not Cardano governance participation, DRep tooling, voting mechanisms, treasury seasons, or constitutional governance processes.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily address Cardano treasury management, protocol revenue strategy, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or long-term network economics. The proposal may generate transaction fees if deployments scale, but this is an indirect consequence rather than the main strategic purpose.

### Additional Notes

The confidence level is **Medium** because the primary alignment with Adoption & Utility is clear, but the proposal also contains substantial Pillar 4 components through localized LATAM adoption, builder enablement, and ecosystem-facing replication work. The secondary alignment is meaningful, but not strong enough to change the primary classification.
