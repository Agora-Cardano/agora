## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to bring practical financial activity onto Cardano through fiat access, USDCx settlement, Cardano wallet provisioning, programmable accounts, agent-operated transactions, treasury/payment flows, and international financial deployment. That maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, which focuses on widespread non-speculative use, high-value verticals such as DeFi and payments, improved user experience, and enterprise-ready financial patterns. 

The proposal is not primarily protocol research, governance tooling, or treasury/network sustainability infrastructure. It may increase transactions, USDCx volume, active wallets, and protocol fee activity, but those are adoption outcomes rather than the proposal’s main strategic category.

A **Secondary Pillar: Community & Ecosystem Growth** is justified because the proposal includes substantial work on international expansion, partner coordination, reusable infrastructure patterns, documentation, ecosystem integrations, and non-US deployment.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it brings ClawBank’s “existing programmable financial infrastructure to Cardano” through “USDCx settlement, Cardano wallet provisioning, fiat on/off-ramping, MCP and CLI support, treasury coordination, entity-linked financial operations, and preparation for international deployment.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal defines its focus as “practical usage from day one: active wallets, USDCx settlement volume, account operations, transaction activity, and treasury/payment flows on Cardano.” 

### Evidence 3

WP1 aims to “Enable Cardano as a supported financial rail within ClawBank,” “Integrate USDCx into ClawBank financial flows,” “Provision Cardano wallets automatically during onboarding,” and “Enable fiat to USDCx on and off-ramping.” 

### Evidence 4

WP2 is intended to make the “Full ClawBank financial flows operational on Cardano,” with users actively transacting through Cardano wallets, fiat-to-USDCx conversion, and agents executing live Cardano transactions. 

### Evidence 5

WP3 and WP4 support secondary ecosystem growth through “international market assessment,” “partner outreach,” “first non-US deployment,” “active users transacting in new market,” and “developer documentation and integration resources.”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to drive real financial usage on Cardano: wallet creation, stablecoin settlement, fiat ramps, payment flows, treasury coordination, account operations, and agent-operated transactions. These directly support the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility focus on high-value financial use cases, payments, lower user friction, and practical non-speculative activity.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes direct work on global expansion, non-US deployment, ecosystem integrations, and developer resources. However, these activities serve the broader goal of activating financial usage, so they do not overtake Adoption & Utility as the Primary Pillar.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected as a Secondary Pillar. Although the proposal claims support for protocol revenue and recurring transaction activity, this is an indirect result of adoption. The proposal does not directly manage the Cardano treasury, improve SPO incentives, strengthen L2-to-L1 value retention policy, or address long-term network infrastructure economics as defined under Pillar 5. Also, the proposal states that no requested funds will be returned to the Cardano Treasury. 

### Additional Notes

The proposal includes measurable adoption-oriented targets, including wallets provisioned, fiat-to-USDCx settlement flows, operational accounts, Cardano transactions, USDCx settlement volume, entity-linked accounts, and monthly Cardano transactions by WP4. These reinforce the Adoption & Utility classification.
