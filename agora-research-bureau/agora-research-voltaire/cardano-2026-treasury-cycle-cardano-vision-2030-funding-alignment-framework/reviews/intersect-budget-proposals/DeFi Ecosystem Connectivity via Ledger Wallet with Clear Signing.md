## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to increase Cardano DeFi usage by allowing Ledger users to connect directly to Cardano dApps through Ledger Wallet / Ledger Button while reducing signing risk through Clear Signing and Transaction Check. This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, which explicitly covers DeFi as a high-value vertical, improved user experience, and enterprise-grade security patterns. The proposal is mainly about unlocking DeFi participation, improving usability, reducing user fear, and increasing TVL / MAU / DEX activity.

Pillar 1 is not selected as primary because the work does not primarily improve Cardano L1 consensus, networking, node/client diversity, L2 interoperability, core ZK capabilities, or protocol-level resilience. Pillar 5 is also not selected because the proposal does not return funds to the treasury, create treasury yield, implement multi-asset treasury mechanisms, improve SPO incentives, or address L2-to-L1 value retention. Its sustainability claim is indirect: more DeFi participation may strengthen the economy, but that is a secondary ecosystem effect rather than the proposal’s main deliverable.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it aims “to bridge the gap between secure cold storage and the Cardano DeFi ecosystem” and to allow retail ADA holders “to connect to Cardano dApps directly through Ledger Wallet.” This directly supports Cardano DeFi access and utility. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal explains that its rationale is to move ADA from being “idle” in cold storage into DeFi participation, stating that to move KPIs like TVL and MAU, Cardano must remove the “fear factor” of DeFi. This directly maps to Adoption & Utility KPIs. 

### Evidence 3

The first work package brings Clear Signing to six Cardano dApps covering “DEX swaps, liquidity provision, lending, and perpetuals,” which are DeFi use cases under Pillar 2’s high-value verticals. 

### Evidence 4

The second work package adds “first-class Cardano support to Ledger Button” so Ledger users can connect to Minswap, WingRiders, SundaeSwap V3, CSWAP, Liqwid, or Strike Finance directly from the browser, with Clear Signing and Transaction Check active. This is primarily a DeFi accessibility and UX/security integration. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal says Pillar 2 is supported because enabling DeFi connectivity for Ledger users can “unlock a massive pool of liquidity” and directly support TVL and DEX volume. That is adoption/utility logic, not protocol infrastructure or treasury sustainability.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the proposal exists to make Cardano DeFi safer and easier to use for Ledger users. Its main deliverables are user-facing DeFi connectivity, Clear Signing, Transaction Check, dApp integration, and Ledger Button support. These align directly with Pillar 2’s focus on DeFi, user experience, and secure adoption.

**Infrastructure & Research** is not selected as primary because the proposal does not materially target Cardano base-layer scalability, consensus, networking, interoperability standards, node diversity, or protocol-level resilience. The work is technical, yes, but “technical” is not automatically Pillar 1. Otherwise every budget proposal with a GitHub repo would become infrastructure, because apparently taxonomy needed another stress test.

**Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience** is not selected as primary or secondary because the proposal does not include direct treasury management, treasury returns, multi-asset treasury mechanisms, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or decentralization targets. The proposal’s claim that more active DeFi participation creates a sustainable circular ADA economy is plausible as an indirect benefit, but it is not a direct and substantial Pillar 5 deliverable.

### Manual Review Reason

Not applicable.

Although the proposal self-identifies Pillars 1, 2, and 5, the classification framework requires selecting the pillar that best justifies the proposal’s existence. Based on the actual objectives, work packages, milestones, and success measures, the dominant purpose is DeFi adoption through safer wallet/dApp connectivity. Secondary Pillar is set to **None** because the Pillar 1 and Pillar 5 links are indirect or insufficiently substantial under the stated classification rules.
