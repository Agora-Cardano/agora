## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to make Cardano easier to use for videogame developers by delivering an open-source inventory management system with native Cardano tokenization, smart contracts, server APIs, client libraries, and demonstration projects for Godot, Unity, and Unreal Engine. That maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, especially its focus on user experience, developer experience, practical utility, and reducing friction for builders.

The proposal is not primarily about base-layer scalability, protocol research, governance, treasury management, SPO incentives, or community engagement. It may indirectly support ecosystem growth by giving developers reusable tooling, but the direct deliverables are technical adoption infrastructure for a specific application domain: blockchain-aware games.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

“TrueInventory addresses this gap. It is a complete, open-source inventory management service that fits naturally into existing game architectures,” combining “items, bundles, recipes, generators, transfers, and crafting” with “native Cardano tokenization.”

### Evidence 2

“Players gain real ownership of in-game items as Cardano tokens, can freely transact those items on-chain, and can redeem tokens for in-game access.”

### Evidence 3

“The 1.0 release will include a server implementing three HTTP APIs, a set of open-source native scripts for tokenized items, and client libraries plus end-to-end demonstration projects for the three most widely used game engines: Godot, Unity, and Unreal Engine.”

### Evidence 4

The proposal states that its goal is “to make it materially easier for any team to bring a Cardano-aware game to production,” making “‘build your blockchain game on Cardano’ a more credible default.”

### Evidence 5

The work package is intended to achieve “a complete, easy-to-use videogame inventory management system with native Cardano tokenization” and “production-quality client libraries for the three most widely used game engines.”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest fit because the proposal is centered on practical Cardano usage by game developers and players. It reduces integration friction, creates reusable application tooling, and enables tokenized in-game ownership as a real use case.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal does not improve Cardano’s base protocol, consensus, networking, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, or node/client diversity.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal does not build governance tooling, improve DRep participation, change voting mechanisms, or support treasury season processes.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as a secondary pillar because any developer-community benefit is indirect. The proposal does not directly deliver education programs, talent pipelines, localized engagement, community campaigns, or structured funding routes.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the repayment language only concerns returning unused surplus funds. That is not a substantial treasury-management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value-retention, SPO incentive, or network resilience objective under the Vision 2030 framework.

### Additional Notes

The proposal itself claims alignment with Pillar 2, and that self-classification is consistent with the actual deliverables. The strongest basis is not the proposal’s mention of “gaming” as a vertical, since gaming is not listed among the specific high-value verticals in the provided Vision 2030 reference. The stronger basis is the proposal’s direct contribution to adoption, utility, developer experience, and practical Cardano integration.
