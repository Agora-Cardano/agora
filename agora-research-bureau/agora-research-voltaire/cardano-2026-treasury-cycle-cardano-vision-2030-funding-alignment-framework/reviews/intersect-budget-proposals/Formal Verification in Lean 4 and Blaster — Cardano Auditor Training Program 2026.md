## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to grow Cardano’s skilled technical community by training auditors and security engineers through two small live cohorts, while producing permanent open-source educational materials, recordings, exercises, and audit examples. This maps most directly to the Cardano Vision 2030 focus on cultivating a skilled developer base, practical experience in Cardano tooling, and long-term ecosystem capability.

Although the proposal includes security-oriented and formal-verification content, its main deliverables are training, curriculum publication, learner cohorts, office hours, recordings, and graduate visibility. The proposal does not primarily deliver L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, client diversity, interoperability infrastructure, or operational resilience mechanisms as described under Infrastructure & Research.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes its main intervention as “two small live cohorts” and a “permanent curriculum + recordings + worked-example audit artifacts” retained by the ecosystem under MIT, with the stated value being the reusable educational artifacts rather than only live-cohort headcount.

### Evidence 2

The work package is described as “an eight-session intensive auditor-training programme” covering Lean 4, theorem proving, SMT integration via Blaster, EUTxO contract modelling, and a practical audit workflow culminating in learner-produced audit artifacts.

### Evidence 3

The proposal’s core objectives include training at least 10 Cardano-focused auditors/security engineers, publishing the complete eight-session curriculum under MIT, creating a public auditor-graduate roster, maintaining community office hours, and delivering quarterly progress reports.

### Evidence 4

Cardano Vision 2030’s Community & Ecosystem Growth pillar includes talent acquisition and retention, hands-on experience, blockchain education, practical experience in Cardano tooling, and retention of skilled contributors.

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s security benefits are framed as downstream ecosystem effects: better-trained auditors may improve Cardano dApp and smart contract security. However, the proposal’s deliverables remain educational and capacity-building rather than direct protocol, client, L2, or resilience infrastructure.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Community & Ecosystem is the strongest classification because the proposal’s main strategic function is human-capacity development: training auditors, publishing learning materials, supporting cohorts, creating a graduate roster, and expanding practical Cardano security expertise.

Infrastructure & Research is a plausible but weaker classification. The proposal does create technical learning artifacts related to formal verification, reproducible environments, and worked audit examples. However, the Cardano Vision 2030 Infrastructure & Research pillar is focused on protocol scalability, interoperability, L2 integration, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and operational resilience. The proposal does not directly deliver those infrastructure outcomes.

Adoption & Utility is also not selected. While better audit capacity may reduce risk for future dApps and protocols, that benefit is indirect. The proposal is not primarily focused on DeFi, RWA, supply chain, payments, user experience, enterprise procurement, or direct adoption outcomes.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal contains a meaningful technical-security component and explicitly presents itself as aligned with Pillar 1 as a secondary pillar. However, under strict Vision 2030 classification rules, that alignment appears secondary, indirect, and insufficiently direct to assign Infrastructure & Research as either the Primary Pillar or a Secondary Pillar.

### Additional Notes

The proposal’s own stated primary alignment with Pillar 4 is consistent with the classification outcome. The main ambiguity concerns whether the formal-verification artifacts are sufficiently direct to justify a Secondary Pillar under Infrastructure & Research. Under a strict reading of the Vision 2030 pillar descriptions, they are better treated as supporting materials within an ecosystem skill-building proposal.
