## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to maintain Cardano’s hardware-wallet compatibility layer for Ledger and Trezor, including compatibility updates, security-sensitive signing support, interoperability libraries, `cardano-hw-cli`, release support, incident response, and vendor-required audits. This maps most directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, because the funded work preserves a secure, reliable, and interoperable access layer for Cardano users and integrators. 

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal includes substantial work on developer support, integration support, tooling, UX continuity, and compatibility for wallets, dApps, and ecosystem builders. However, those outcomes are secondary to the main strategic purpose: keeping a security-critical technical layer operational.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it requests “12 months of funding for production maintenance of Cardano hardware-wallet support,” including “Ledger and Trezor compatibility updates,” “supporting interoperability libraries and cardano-hw-cli,” “developer support for ecosystem integrators,” and “vendor-required product or security audits.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal defines its scope as “limited to compatibility, security, supporting libraries/tooling, release support, and vendor-required audit work needed to keep supported hardware-wallet flows operational.” 

### Evidence 3

The work package objective is to “keep supported hardware-wallet flows compatible with Cardano protocol changes and vendor-side changes,” “prevent security, signing, and usability regressions,” and provide “timely bug fixing, release support, and incident response.” 

### Evidence 4

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 1 as keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” with security and resilience focus areas including post-quantum readiness, client diversity, and threat detection/recovery. 

### Evidence 5

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places developer experience, open-source maintenance, and compatibility under Pillar 2, while the proposal includes developer support, partner integration support, supporting libraries, and tooling maintenance.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal exists mainly to preserve a security-critical and interoperability-critical technical access layer for Cardano. Its main deliverables are maintenance, compatibility assurance, release engineering, bug fixes, audits, incident response, and tooling/library support.

**Adoption & Utility** is relevant, but not primary. The proposal does support adoption indirectly by preventing hardware-wallet breakage, preserving secure user access, and reducing integration friction for builders. It also directly touches developer experience through `cardano-hw-cli`, supporting libraries, and integrator support. However, the proposal does not primarily create new user demand, launch a high-value vertical, expand payments/DeFi/RWA usage, or directly drive TVL, transactions, or MAU. Its adoption value depends on infrastructure continuity.

### Additional Notes

Secondary alignment is warranted because developer support, integration support, compatibility, and UX continuity are not merely incidental. They are explicit parts of the work package and budget. Still, the strongest justification for the proposal’s existence remains technical maintenance of secure hardware-wallet infrastructure.
