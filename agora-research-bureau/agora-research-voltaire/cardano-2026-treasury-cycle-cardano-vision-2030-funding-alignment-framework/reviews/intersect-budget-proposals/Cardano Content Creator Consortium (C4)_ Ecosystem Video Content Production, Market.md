## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to fund a coordinated consortium of Cardano video creators to produce marketing, education, news, interviews, project showcases, short-form content, livestreams, and ecosystem narrative campaigns. That maps most directly to **Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem Growth**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on global engagement, market-centric communication, and proactively demonstrating Cardano’s ecosystem value. The framework describes Pillar 4 as focused on “Driving global engagement through a market-centric approach” and “Proactively Demonstrate Ecosystem Value”.

Although the proposal claims secondary alignment with Adoption & Utility, the concrete deliverables are mainly content production, creator retention, reach, impressions, Earned Media Value, and reporting. The proposal does include educational and onboarding content, but it does not directly deliver product utility, infrastructure for high-value verticals, UX improvements, enterprise compliance patterns, or targeted adoption outcomes such as TVL, monthly transactions, or MAU growth. Therefore, Pillar 2 is treated as an intended downstream effect rather than a direct and substantial secondary alignment.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that “Cardano has a content creator sustainability crisis” and frames C4 as a response to Cardano being “outpaced in public visibility by ecosystems that invest in creator-driven marketing.”

### Evidence 2

C4 is described as “a 12-month pilot retaining six of Cardano's most established video creators as a coordinated marketing and education force for the ecosystem.”

### Evidence 3

The core output commitment is to “produce at least 850 original videos over 12 months across news, DeFi education, project showcases, short-form content, live streams, and interviews.”

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s stated objectives include retaining Cardano’s content creator base, delivering Earned Media Value, providing ecosystem-wide project coverage, and executing “coordinated narrative campaigns in response to major ecosystem events.”

### Evidence 5

Success metrics focus on content and reach: “5M+ combined views/impressions,” “850 original video content pieces,” “$360,000 USD minimum” Earned Media Value, “100% retention of the 6-creator consortium,” monthly reports, and two audits.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Community & Ecosystem** is the strongest fit because the proposal is fundamentally about ecosystem visibility, creator retention, marketing coordination, education, narrative building, and public engagement. These align directly with Pillar 4’s emphasis on global engagement and demonstrating ecosystem value.

**Adoption & Utility** was not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily build or deploy Cardano utility. It may help users discover wallets, DeFi, staking, governance, and projects, but those are mediated through content and awareness rather than direct utility creation. In other words, it points people toward adoption rather than delivering adoption infrastructure or measurable usage outcomes itself.

### Additional Notes

No Secondary Pillar is assigned. The proposal’s educational and onboarding content could indirectly support adoption, but the measurable targets are mainly marketing/content KPIs rather than direct Adoption & Utility outcomes under the Vision 2030 framework.
