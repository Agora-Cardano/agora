## AI Rationale

The strongest single fit is **Sustainability & Resilience** because the proposal’s formal ask is for the Cardano Treasury to deploy capital as a limited partner in a non-ADA, yield-bearing real-estate vehicle, with principal return and a target net IRR. That maps directly to the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus on a **multi-asset treasury** and a **managed treasury** that can “generate yields” and “strategically deploy capital into growth-focused investments,” with an expected enhancement of “10%+ ROI on non-ADA assets.” The proposal’s main work package is explicitly the Treasury’s LP subscription, and several of its core objectives center on treasury capital deployment, return generation, and capital recycling rather than grant-style spending.

A substantial and direct **Secondary Pillar** is **Adoption & Utility**. The Vision 2030 framework places RWA tokenization of illiquid assets such as **real estate** inside Pillar 2, alongside invisible UX and enterprise-ready compliance patterns. The proposal directly describes tokenizing institutional real-estate exposure on Cardano through CIP-113, pursuing DeFi composability, onboarding institutional participants, and making the blockchain layer largely invisible to end users through existing financial workflows. Those are not incidental benefits; they are major deliverables and intended outcomes.

Confidence is **Low** because the proposal contains two major and independently substantial strategic purposes: one is treasury diversification and yield generation, which points toward Pillar 5; the other is institutional RWA deployment and on-chain utility, which points toward Pillar 2. Reasonable reviewers could select either as the primary pillar depending on whether they emphasize the treasury action itself or the product and adoption outcomes it is meant to enable.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

“**Cardano as Limited Partner, not as donor: the Treasury becomes an LP for €15M … Capital returned at exit, target net IRR of 10%**.” This directly supports Pillar 5 because the proposal is structured as treasury capital allocation into a yield-bearing, non-ADA investment rather than as a one-way expenditure.

### Evidence 2

“**Generate a measurable financial return for the Cardano Treasury: target net IRR of 10% via annual yield distributions, full principal repayment at fund maturity**.” This closely mirrors the Pillar 5 managed-treasury objective of generating yield from non-ADA assets.

### Evidence 3

“**Make Cardano the native tokenization infrastructure for €100M of regulated European institutional capital**.” This is strong direct evidence for Pillar 2 because the proposal aims to create non-speculative RWA utility on Cardano, not merely hold an off-chain treasury investment.

### Evidence 4

“**Our mission: build the first prime real estate fund that is both tokenized AND liquid, through its native listing on Cardano's DeFi protocols**.” This reinforces the Pillar 2 alignment around RWA, DeFi utility, and on-chain usage, while also explaining why the primary classification remains contestable.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Sustainability & Resilience** was selected over **Adoption & Utility** because the proposal’s central governance action is the Treasury’s subscription into a managed, non-ADA investment vehicle designed to return principal and yield. That is a narrower and more exact match to Pillar 5’s treasury-focused language than Pillar 2’s broader adoption language. In other words, the proposal is not only asking Cardano to host tokenized real estate; it is specifically asking the Treasury to **diversify its asset exposure and earn investment returns**, which is the clearest single strategic justification under the Vision 2030 framework.

**Adoption & Utility** was not chosen as primary because, although the proposal directly advances RWA tokenization, institutional onboarding, invisible UX, and DeFi composability, those outcomes are bundled inside a treasury deployment structure whose first-order mechanism is managed treasury investment. Pillar 2 remains substantial enough to warrant a Secondary Pillar, but it was not selected as the best one-pillar explanation for why this proposal exists.

Pillar 1 is not primary because the proposal references CIP-113 deployment and audit, but the document frames those as implementation components of the fund model rather than as the main strategic purpose. Pillar 3 is not primary because the proposal does not primarily build governance processes or tools. Pillar 4 is not primary because institutional onboarding and ecosystem visibility appear as downstream effects of the RWA deployment, not as the proposal’s standalone objective.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit **multiple Primary Pillars**, especially **Sustainability & Resilience** and **Adoption & Utility**. It also contains **multiple major workstreams with different strategic purposes**: treasury diversification and ROI generation on one hand, and institutional RWA tokenization plus on-chain utility creation on the other. That combination makes the primary classification materially ambiguous even after comparing the proposal against the Vision 2030 framework.

### Additional Notes

The proposal document itself explicitly argues for Pillar 2 as primary and Pillar 5 as secondary, but this classification does **not** adopt that self-description automatically; it instead compares the proposal’s actual requested action, work package structure, deliverables, and outcomes against the Vision 2030 pillar definitions. On that basis, Pillar 5 is the strongest single fit, but not unambiguously so.
