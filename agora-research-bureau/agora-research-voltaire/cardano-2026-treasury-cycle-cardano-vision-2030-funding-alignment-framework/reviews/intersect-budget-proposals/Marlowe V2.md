## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core strategic purpose is to modernize Marlowe as an accessible, formally verifiable financial contract platform for Cardano DeFi. Its main stated value is enabling financial contracts such as escrows, swaps, bonds, and payment schedules, lowering development barriers for non-programmers and domain experts, and supporting DeFi DApp development on Cardano. That maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on DeFi, business/consumer experience, invisible technology, and developer experience. 

A substantial secondary alignment exists with **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, because the proposal also includes validator/runtime upgrades, state compression, Plutus primitive optimization, HTLC/Cardano Lightning integration analysis, and a Marlowe state-channel feasibility study. Still, these technical components appear to serve the broader adoption objective of making Marlowe useful for DeFi, not to improve Cardano’s base protocol infrastructure as the primary purpose.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal describes Marlowe as “a DSL designed specifically for financial contracts on Cardano” including “escrows, swaps, bonds, and payment schedules,” and says its purpose is to make smart contract development accessible to “financial professionals, lawyers, businesses” by abstracting away from Plutus/Haskell and Aiken. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that it will “modernise and update Marlowe to face the challenge of delivering Cardano DeFi,” which directly supports the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility focus on DeFi as a high-value vertical. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal says Marlowe provides “a complete environment for Dapp development in the DeFi space” and aims to further hide blockchain features such as transaction fees and signing, aligning with Adoption & Utility’s DeFi, developer experience, and invisible technology themes. 

### Evidence 4

WP1 will implement Marlowe V2.0 across the “on-chain validator,” “off-chain runtime,” and “wider ecosystem of tools,” including language changes, validator state compression, lazy closure, and optimized Plutus primitives. This creates a strong but secondary Infrastructure & Research alignment. 

### Evidence 5

WP2 includes an “in-depth analysis of HTLC and Cardano Lightning protocol integration with Marlowe” and a “Marlowe state channel design,” supporting secondary alignment with Infrastructure & Research, especially L2 integration and scalability research.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s reason for existence is not generic protocol hardening or base-layer research. Its main objective is to make Cardano financial contracts and DeFi development more usable, expressive, secure, and accessible through Marlowe V2.

**Infrastructure & Research** is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes substantial technical deliverables: validator updates, runtime upgrades, tooling infrastructure, state compression, HTLC research, Lightning integration analysis, and a Marlowe L2/state-channel feasibility study. However, these are best understood as technical means toward DeFi utility and adoption. The proposal itself repeatedly frames the strategic outcome around Cardano DeFi engagement, DApp development, and financial contract use.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple primary pillars. It contains one major workstream focused on implementing and upgrading Marlowe V2.0 tooling and runtime infrastructure, and another focused on future Marlowe V2.x design plus L2/state-channel feasibility. This creates a genuine Pillar 1 versus Pillar 2 ambiguity, although Pillar 2 is the stronger primary fit.

### Additional Notes

The proposal includes adoption-related success metrics, including the number of Marlowe V2.0 contracts deployed on Cardano and total value transacted by transactions submitted to Marlowe V2.0 contracts. However, the attached proposal text does not provide numeric target values for those metrics.
