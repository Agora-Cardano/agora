## AI Rationale

This proposal is fundamentally about building and improving Cardano’s technical infrastructure for cross-chain interoperability. Its core objectives include deploying a Decentralized Verifier Network (DVN), establishing a Cross-Chain Token standard, and enabling Generalized Messaging, all of which directly enhance Cardano’s interoperability and security. These align closely with Pillar 1, Infrastructure & Research, which emphasizes keeping Cardano secure, fast, and interoperable.

In contrast, the proposal does not primarily focus on user adoption campaigns, community engagement, or governance reforms, but rather on decentralized bridge architecture, security, technical standards, SDKs, and cross-chain messaging infrastructure. While the outcomes may indirectly support DeFi, RWA, TVL, transactions, and user growth, the proposal’s deliverables are clearly technical and infrastructure-oriented rather than market-facing adoption initiatives. Therefore, its primary strategic purpose is infrastructure development.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the DVN replaces federated signing with a stake-based BFT quorum of Cardano SPOs, while Mithril anchors outbound verification to Ouroboros itself, making Cardano-to-EVM verification trustless.

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that Janus decentralizes ChainPort’s Cardano infrastructure, publishes the DVN and CCT as open CIPs, and positions Cardano as a secure, interoperable hub for RWA and DeFi.

### Evidence 3

The proposal identifies that decentralized minting and release authorization is not live today and that Cardano still lacks generalized cross-chain messaging, then states that Project Janus funds the architecture to change that.

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s core engineering work includes implementing DVN consensus, deploying Plutus V3 CCT minting policies, implementing GMP Mailbox components, completing Mithril signature integration, publishing JavaScript/TypeScript and Rust SDKs, and deploying a functional testnet environment.

### Evidence 5

The production phase focuses on launching mainnet infrastructure, including EVM-to-Cardano and Cardano-to-EVM token ports, production Generalized Message Passing, decentralized bridge monitoring, and transfer of admin key control to a Governance Multi-sig.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Project Janus’s core objectives are technical infrastructure and interoperability: establishing a decentralized bridge authorization network, creating a unified cross-chain token standard, integrating Mithril-based verification, and enabling generalized cross-chain messaging. These objectives directly match Pillar 1, Infrastructure & Research, especially its focus on cross-chain interoperability, protocol-level infrastructure, security, resilience, and enabling Cardano to host more economic activity.

Adoption & Utility is not assigned as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal’s references to DeFi, RWA, TVL, transactions, MAU, and wallet integrations are framed primarily as downstream use cases or expected effects of the infrastructure, not as a separate adoption workstream. The proposal does not define direct adoption programs, market-facing execution, user acquisition activities, enterprise onboarding targets, or quantitative targets for TVL, monthly transactions, or monthly active users. Its measurable deliverables are instead concentrated on technical specifications, protocol engineering, SDKs, testnet and mainnet deployment, bridge decentralization, Mithril integration, security architecture, and operational handover.

For that reason, the proposal’s strategic justification rests on the infrastructure required to make future adoption possible, rather than on direct delivery of adoption outcomes. The existence of potential adoption benefits does not meet the threshold for a Secondary Pillar under the classification rules, because those benefits are dependent on later ecosystem usage and are not independently developed as a substantial proposal objective.

### Manual Review Reason

Not applicable.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains some governance-related components, such as a Governance Info Action, DAO treasury handover, and admin key transfer to a Governance Multi-sig. However, these appear to support decentralization and operational control of the technical infrastructure rather than constitute a governance reform proposal under Pillar 3. Governance is therefore not selected as a secondary pillar.
