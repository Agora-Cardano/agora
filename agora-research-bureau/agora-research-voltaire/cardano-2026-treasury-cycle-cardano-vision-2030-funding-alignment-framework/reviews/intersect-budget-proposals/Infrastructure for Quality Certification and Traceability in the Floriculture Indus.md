## AI Rationale

This proposal aligns most directly with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility** because its core purpose is to bring Cardano into a real-world enterprise use case: floriculture quality certification, plant licensing, intellectual property protection, compliance, and supply-chain traceability. The proposal is not primarily trying to improve Cardano’s base protocol, governance system, treasury design, SPO incentives, or developer community. It is building an application-layer system intended to generate non-speculative enterprise usage and recurring transactions through real commercial workflows.

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Adoption & Utility as driving “widespread, non-speculative utility” through high-value verticals, superior UX, and enterprise-grade security, including supply chain/provenance, RWA, invisible technology, and enterprise compliance. The proposal directly targets those areas through digital certificates, license verification, ERP integration, batch/export traceability, and a Plantec pilot.  

Although the proposal includes smart contracts, CIP-68 metadata, APIs, IPFS, audits, and open-source SDKs, these are implementation mechanisms for an adoption use case. They do not substantially align with Pillar 1’s focus on L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or protocol-level resilience. So no secondary pillar is assigned.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that it will “develop a lightweight, secure, and scalable infrastructure on Cardano” for “automatic generation of digital quality certificates and authorizations” after contracts are signed between propagators, growers, and breeders. This directly supports enterprise utility and real-world business workflows. 

### Evidence 2

The solution includes “immutable registration of certificates and licenses,” “QR code and web-based verification,” “integration with existing contract workflows,” and “scalable batch-level and export traceability for floriculture supply chains.” This maps directly to the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility focus on supply chain/provenance and business adoption.  

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly frames its value as “real-world adoption and enterprise utility for Cardano in a high-value industry,” with blockchain activity from “digital certification, licensing, and compliance,” plus projected recurring traceability transactions. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal identifies its own strongest alignment as “Pillar 2 Adoption & Utility,” explaining that it brings Cardano into “a traditional, high-value industry: global floriculture” and supports “critical real-world processes” including IP protection, regulatory compliance, and supply-chain trust. 

### Evidence 5

The work packages focus on real deployment and adoption: Plantec ERP integration, at least 25 real biological licenses on Mainnet, onboarding Plantec staff, engaging at least two additional breeders or producers, and publishing a sustainability/business model for continued use.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest classification because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to create enterprise adoption in a high-value industry vertical. Its intended outcomes are real-world certification, licensing, compliance, traceability, transaction generation, and onboarding of floriculture stakeholders.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the technical components are application-specific. The proposal does not improve Cardano’s L1 protocol, consensus, ledger, networking, L2 interoperability, cross-chain standards, ZK infrastructure, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or protocol-level threat recovery.

**Community & Ecosystem** was not selected because the outreach and additional partner onboarding are in service of scaling the enterprise use case, not primarily developing community capacity, talent pipelines, education, or broad ecosystem engagement.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the project’s sustainability model refers to its own post-grant commercial viability. It does not directly address Cardano treasury management, multi-asset treasury design, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or decentralization targets.

### Manual Review Reason

Not applicable.

### Additional Notes

The proposal contains technical deliverables, but their strategic role is to enable enterprise adoption and non-speculative Cardano usage. The strongest Vision 2030 justification is therefore Pillar 2.
