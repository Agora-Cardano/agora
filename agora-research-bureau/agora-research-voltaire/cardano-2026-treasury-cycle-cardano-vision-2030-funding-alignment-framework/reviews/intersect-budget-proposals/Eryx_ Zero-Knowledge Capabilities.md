## AI Rationale

This proposal’s core purpose is to expand Cardano’s zero-knowledge infrastructure by delivering reusable technical components for on-chain proof verification, verifiable computation, ZK developer tooling, and privacy primitives. That maps directly to **Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research**, especially the Vision 2030 focus on **Core ZK capabilities**, which calls for modular.

The proposal is not primarily an end-user application, commercial product, governance system, or treasury sustainability mechanism. Its main deliverables are technical infrastructure: a PLONK verifier in Aiken, RISC Zero zkVM integration, Aiken ZK library extensions, and Semaphore V4 for Cardano.

A **Secondary Pillar: Adoption & Utility** is justified because the proposal includes substantial developer-facing deliverables: documentation, tutorials, open-source tooling, compatibility with Circom/SnarkJS/RISC Zero, and reduced barriers for developers migrating or building ZK applications on Cardano. This aligns specifically with the Vision 2030 Adoption & Utility sub-area for developer experience, including open-source infrastructure, education/migration, and compatibility.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Cardano currently lacks “variety and accessibility of on-chain proof verification tools for most modern ZK systems” and that the proposal will deliver “concrete ZK framework solutions for Cardano, bringing ZK and privacy infrastructure directly to the L1.”

### Evidence 2

The listed contributions are infrastructure deliverables: “A PLONK verifier in Aiken,” “an adaptation of the RISC Zero zkVM,” “an extension of our ZK library in Aiken,” and “an implementation of the Semaphore protocol for anonymous on-chain group signaling.”

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly says that “this initiative delivers infrastructure rather than a product” and that it “expands what is possible to build on Cardano.”

### Evidence 4

For its own strategic alignment, the proposal identifies Pillar 1 as the primary fit and says it contributes directly to “L2 Integrations and Core ZK Capabilities,” including modular ZK infrastructure for “privacy-preserving, verifiable apps.”

### Evidence 5

The secondary Adoption & Utility alignment is supported by developer-facing outcomes: the proposal says its deliverables are “open-source, thoroughly documented, and designed for ease of use,” and multiple work packages aim to reduce developer barriers through documentation, examples, tooling, compatibility, and production-ready workflows.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Infrastructure & Research** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal exists to build foundational ZK infrastructure for Cardano. The work packages are technical primitives and developer infrastructure, not direct market adoption campaigns, end-user products, governance processes, treasury mechanisms, or community growth programs.

**Adoption & Utility** is valid as a secondary pillar because the proposal directly supports developer experience and compatibility, especially through open-source tooling, documentation, tutorials, and migration paths for existing ZK toolchains such as Circom, SnarkJS, and RISC Zero. However, these adoption benefits depend on future developers and applications using the infrastructure, so they do not override Pillar 1 as the primary classification.

**Governance** is not selected because Semaphore may support private voting or future MACI-like governance applications, but the proposal itself delivers a privacy primitive and ZK infrastructure, not a governance process, turnout mechanism, DRep tool, treasury season system, or governance-accessibility platform.

**Community & Ecosystem** is not selected because contribution guidelines, documentation, and developer onboarding are present, but they are supporting mechanisms for technical tooling rather than a primary talent acquisition, retention, education, or global engagement program.

**Sustainability & Resilience** is not selected because the conditional ADA refund mechanism is a budget/payment clause, not a strategic workstream for treasury management, SPO incentives, network economics, or L2-to-L1 value retention.

### Manual Review Reason

Not required.

### Additional Notes

The proposal has multiple work packages, but they are coherently grouped around a single strategic purpose: expanding Cardano’s zero-knowledge infrastructure and developer-accessible ZK stack. Therefore, the presence of multiple work packages does not create pillar-level ambiguity.
