## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to create real-world, non-speculative on-chain utility by implementing a revenue-based finance protocol on Cardano. Its core objective is not to improve Cardano’s base-layer infrastructure, governance system, treasury policy, or SPO economics. It is to enable hub-networks, grassroots ventures, and institutional capital providers to coordinate funding, revenue participation, demand signals, and redemption flows through Cardano-based smart contracts and related services.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, which focuses on widespread non-speculative utility, high-value use cases, business/consumer experience, and enterprise-grade patterns. The proposal explicitly frames itself around real ventures, real revenue, on-chain demand, institutional capital access, and use cases such as mobility, pharmaceutical supply-chain trust, escrow, and local-market coordination. The technical deliverables are means toward adoption and utility, not Cardano protocol-layer infrastructure.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that the protocol will enable hub-networks “to access institutional-scale sources of capital and distribute it to network edges,” using Revenue Participation Certificates to coordinate distribution pathways and redemptions. This supports Adoption & Utility because the intended outcome is capital flow into real-world Cardano-based economic activity, not core protocol improvement. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says the funding would demonstrate “liquidity across the full-stack, from hub-network gateway to on-the-ground teams and back,” with the scope being to generate “the first contract redemptions” from surplus value created at network edges. This directly supports practical, non-speculative usage. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly argues that its alignment is with Pillar 2 because it is “about generating real, non-speculative economic activity on-chain,” rather than building Cardano’s internal capabilities or governance systems. 

### Evidence 4

WP4 describes demand signals as real-world economic events generated by teams in the field, including “payments, contracts, transactions,” converted into protocol-legible signals and recorded through Cardano. This is direct evidence of on-chain utility tied to external economic activity. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal gives examples of Cardano-enabled use cases in mobility, pharmaceutical supply-chain verification, and escrow/trust for local markets. These examples align with Pillar 2’s focus on high-value verticals such as supply chain/provenance, payments, and broader real-world utility.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest primary classification because the proposal’s reason for existence is to create a Cardano-based protocol for real-world financial coordination, capital onboarding, demand signal recording, and revenue-linked redemption. The deliverables are technical, but their strategic purpose is adoption-oriented: enabling institutional capital, hub-networks, and community ventures to generate and account for real economic activity on-chain.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal does not primarily improve Cardano’s L1 protocol, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, client diversity, post-quantum readiness, or base-layer reliability. It builds an application/protocol stack on Cardano, which is not the same thing as improving Cardano’s own infrastructure. Apparently this distinction must be stated because every software proposal eventually tries to cosplay as civilization-critical infrastructure.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was also not selected because, although the proposal references grassroots networks, hubs, and local teams, the funded work packages are not primarily education, talent acquisition, ecosystem marketing, community engagement, or localized communication strategy. Those elements support the context of the proposal, but the main deliverable is a revenue-based finance protocol for on-chain utility.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because the proposal’s treasury and revenue-allocation mechanisms relate mainly to the proposed protocol’s internal financial flows, not Cardano’s treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, or SPO incentive structure.

### Additional Notes

Secondary Pillar is set to **None** because the proposal’s other plausible alignments are indirect or contextual. The technical build supports the Adoption & Utility objective, but it does not independently constitute a substantial Vision 2030 Pillar 1 objective. The grassroots and hub-network context supports adoption, but it does not rise to a direct Pillar 4 workstream under the supplied Vision 2030 framework.
