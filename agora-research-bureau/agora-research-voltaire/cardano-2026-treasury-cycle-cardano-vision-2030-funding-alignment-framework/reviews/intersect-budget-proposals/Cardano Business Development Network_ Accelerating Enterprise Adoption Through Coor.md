## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to accelerate enterprise and government adoption of Cardano-based solutions through coordinated regional business development hubs. Its main deliverables focus on qualifying enterprise leads, onboarding enterprise buyers, closing 7–10 deals, supporting integrations, and driving measurable adoption KPIs such as transactions, monthly users, and TVL.

The strongest alignment is therefore **Adoption & Utility**, because the proposal is explicitly centered on non-speculative, enterprise-facing usage of Cardano in real-world business contexts.

A **Secondary Pillar** of **Community & Ecosystem** is justified because the proposal also contains substantial work to onboard 20+ Cardano companies, establish regional BD teams, coordinate with ecosystem initiatives, and provide business development support to builders. That said, this builder and ecosystem support is primarily in service of enterprise adoption, not the dominant strategic purpose itself.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Cardano has an opportunity to “boost the adoption of Cardano based solutions by enterprise/government buyers through a coordinated network of regional hubs.” 

### Evidence 2

The Year 1 pilot targets are “5-8 regional BD teams, 20+ Cardano companies participating and 7-10 deals closed,” showing direct adoption and enterprise deal-execution objectives. 

### Evidence 3

WP2 is explicitly framed as “Enterprise Deal Execution: Closing 7-10 Deals & Delivering Year 1 KPI Targets,” with the goal of converting leads into active integrations. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal defines Year 1 KPI targets including “Transactions: 2.25M+,” “Monthly Users: 75K+,” and “TVL: $30M+,” which are direct utility and adoption outcomes. 

### Evidence 5

The secondary ecosystem alignment is supported by WP1, which aims to recruit and certify 5–8 regional BD teams and onboard 20+ Cardano companies into the network for leads, support, and deal facilitation.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal exists mainly to create enterprise and government adoption pathways for Cardano solutions. Its largest work package, WP2, is focused on enterprise deal execution, onboarding, integration, and KPI delivery. The proposal’s success metrics are also adoption-facing: closed deals, discovery workshops, transactions, users, and TVL.

**Community & Ecosystem** is appropriate as a Secondary Pillar because the proposal includes direct ecosystem-building activities: regional hubs, BD team certification, builder onboarding, technical team coordination, and support for Cardano companies. However, those activities function as enabling mechanisms for enterprise adoption, not as the primary reason the proposal exists.

**Governance** was not selected because the proposal includes oversight and reporting mechanisms, but these are administrative controls rather than the strategic purpose.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected because the proposal is non-technical and does not primarily develop protocol, developer tooling, research, or infrastructure.

**Sustainability & Resilience** was not selected because subscription revenue and Treasury repayment provisions are secondary operational details, not the proposal’s main strategic objective.

### Manual Review Reason

Not required.

### Additional Notes

The proposal has a clear primary adoption thesis, but the Secondary Pillar is substantial enough to reduce confidence from High to Medium under the framework’s confidence definitions.
