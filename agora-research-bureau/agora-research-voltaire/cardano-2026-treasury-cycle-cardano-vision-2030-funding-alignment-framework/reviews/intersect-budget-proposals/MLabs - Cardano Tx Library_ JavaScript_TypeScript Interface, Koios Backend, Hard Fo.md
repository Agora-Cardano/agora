## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is to improve Cardano developer experience by making CTL easier to adopt, easier to maintain, and more useful for dApp builders. Its main deliverables are a JavaScript/TypeScript interface, a Koios backend, and ongoing CTL maintenance for dependency updates and Dijkstra hard fork preparation.

Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, Developer Experience is explicitly placed under Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility, including open-source maintenance of Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure, education/migration for developers, and compatibility with common multi-chain tooling. That maps directly to the proposal’s stated goals: lowering the barrier for JS/TS developers, reducing dApp infrastructure friction, improving time-to-market, and maintaining foundational off-chain tooling.

Although the work is highly technical, its strategic function under the official framework is not core protocol research, L1 scalability, node-client diversity, or protocol-level security. It is developer tooling and builder adoption infrastructure, which the Vision 2030 document categorizes under Adoption & Utility.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that “CTL is a foundational off-chain transaction-building library” and that it is currently “reachable only from PureScript,” which keeps it out of the hands of JavaScript and TypeScript developers.

### Evidence 2

Work Package 1 aims to create a “JavaScript/TypeScript Interface” that “opens CTL to JS/TS developers without requiring them to learn PureScript.”

### Evidence 3

The proposal explicitly frames WP1 as a direct adoption lever, stating that it “improves time-to-market for any dApp team whose stack is already JS/TS,” lowers operational complexity, and broadens the developer population able to adopt Cardano transaction-building tooling.

### Evidence 4

Work Package 2 adds Koios as a first-class backend, giving builders an additional open-source query-layer option and reducing infrastructure dependency for downstream dApps.

### Evidence 5

Work Package 3 funds quarterly dependency updates and Dijkstra hard fork preparation so CTL remains current and usable by dApp teams through protocol changes.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Adoption & Utility is the strongest primary classification because the proposal is centered on developer experience, builder onboarding, tooling usability, and reducing friction for dApp development.

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places “Developer Experience” inside Pillar 2 and specifically includes maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure, migration support for developers, and compatibility with common tooling. The proposal’s JS/TS interface, Koios backend, npm package, documentation, examples, test suite, and maintenance work all directly support that area.

Infrastructure & Research is not selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal does not primarily target L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, core ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, full-node client diversity, or threat detection and recovery. Its technical work supports application builders rather than directly advancing the base protocol or core research agenda.

Governance, Community & Ecosystem, and Sustainability & Resilience are not selected because the proposal does not primarily focus on governance participation, community engagement, treasury/tokenomics, SPO incentives, or long-term network economics.

### Additional Notes

No Secondary Pillar is assigned. While the proposal has technical infrastructure-like characteristics, those benefits are already captured by the Developer Experience section of Pillar 2. The evidence does not establish a separate, substantial secondary alignment with another Vision 2030 pillar.
