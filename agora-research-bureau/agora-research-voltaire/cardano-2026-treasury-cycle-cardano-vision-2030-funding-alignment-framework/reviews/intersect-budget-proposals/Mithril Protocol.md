## AI Rationale

The proposal’s core purpose is technical infrastructure development for Cardano: faster node synchronization, trustless state verification, decentralized artifact storage, recursive SNARK proofs, certificate-chain optimization, and SPO onboarding improvements. That maps directly to Pillar 1, Infrastructure & Research, which focuses on keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” including cross-chain interoperability, state-proofs, ZK capabilities, client/light-client support, and resilience. Humanity survives another taxonomy exercise.

Pillar 2 and Pillar 4 are mentioned by the proposer, but their relevance is mostly downstream: faster sync and better tooling may help wallets, exchanges, monitoring tools, and community-built applications, but the proposal’s actual funded work is not primarily about user adoption, high-value verticals, market growth, education, or ecosystem engagement. Therefore, no Secondary Pillar is assigned.

The milestones include decentralizing Mithril network configuration, producing recursive SNARK proofs, certificate-chain caching and optimization, hard-fork compatibility, and production/testing infrastructure. These are protocol and infrastructure deliverables, not adoption or marketing deliverables. 

### Evidence 5

The Vision 2030 reference defines Pillar 1 as keeping Cardano “secure, fast, and interoperable,” including cross-chain interoperability, state-proofs, core ZK capabilities, client diversity, and security/resilience.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Infrastructure & Research is the strongest fit because the proposal is centered on improving Cardano’s technical base layer and supporting infrastructure. Its main deliverables are protocol improvements, decentralized configuration, trustless state verification, recursive SNARK proofs, artifact storage decentralization, caching, pruning, maintenance, and compatibility work.

Adoption & Utility is not selected as Primary because the proposal does not directly build a high-value vertical such as DeFi, RWA, supply chain, or payments, nor does it primarily deliver business or consumer UX improvements. Any adoption benefit comes from infrastructure enablement.

Community & Ecosystem is not selected as Primary because the proposal does not primarily fund education, talent acquisition, localized adoption, public narrative, or structured funding routes. Community-built tooling is described as an enabled outcome, not the proposal’s main workstream.

Sustainability & Resilience is not selected because the proposal does not primarily concern treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentive programs, or decentralization targets as defined in Pillar 5. Its resilience-related elements are better captured under Pillar 1’s security and infrastructure resilience scope.

Not required.

The proposal’s actual scope is overwhelmingly technical infrastructure, so Pillar 1 is the correct Primary Pillar and no Secondary Pillar is warranted under the stated rules.
