## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to maintain and enhance Pallas, a set of open-source Rust crates used by Cardano developers and downstream projects. Under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, this maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically **A.3 Developer Experience**, which includes incentivizing the maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure in line with open-source best practices. The proposal itself explicitly identifies Pallas as developer infrastructure that reduces friction for builders, supports downstream projects, and keeps tooling compatible with Cardano protocol changes.  

A secondary alignment with **Infrastructure & Research** is present because the proposal also supports protocol continuity, tooling diversity, and client-diversity-related infrastructure. However, the funded work is not primarily an L1 protocol improvement, L2 integration, bridge standard, ZK capability, or direct protocol-security initiative. Its main object is developer tooling maintenance, so Infrastructure & Research is secondary rather than primary.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Pallas is “a collection of Rust crates that re-implement core Ouroboros and Cardano primitives: CBOR encoding, cryptographic operations, mini-protocol networking, multi-era ledger traversal, transaction building, and address handling.” 

### Evidence 2

The proposal says these crates “serve as shared infrastructure for projects like Aiken, Dolos, Lucid, Oura, Mithril, Amaru and many others across the ecosystem.” 

### Evidence 3

The stated work package objective is to keep Pallas “stable, reliable, and current with every Cardano protocol change so downstream projects can depend on it without friction.” 

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s delivery scope includes “dependency updates, protocol compatibility, performance improvements, bug fixing and documentation,” plus issue triage, pull request review, and ecosystem-driven enhancements. 

### Evidence 5

The Vision 2030 framework places “Open-source incentives” under **A.3 Developer Experience**, describing it as maintenance of “core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure in line with open-source best practices.”

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the official Vision 2030 framework explicitly places developer experience, open-source incentives, SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure maintenance under Pillar 2. The proposal’s main deliverables are maintenance, documentation, protocol compatibility, community support, and AI-friendly integration resources for a developer library. These are most directly explained by the Developer Experience focus area.

**Infrastructure & Research** is a plausible classification because Pallas implements low-level Cardano primitives and supports tooling diversity, protocol continuity, and downstream infrastructure. However, the proposal does not primarily fund core protocol development, scalability work, interoperability standards, post-quantum readiness, threat detection, or direct resilience engineering. Its funded activity is maintenance of a developer-facing open-source library, which the Vision 2030 framework formally treats as Adoption & Utility.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple Primary Pillars. The official Vision 2030 taxonomy supports **Adoption & Utility** through Developer Experience, while the technical substance of the work also has a strong infrastructure character.

### Additional Notes

The classification follows the Cardano Vision 2030 framework as the authoritative taxonomy. If a separate treasury allocation methodology distinguishes formal strategic alignment from economic substance, this proposal may be a candidate for manual relabeling as infrastructure-oriented spending. That would be an analytical overlay, not a change to the official Vision 2030 pillar classification.
