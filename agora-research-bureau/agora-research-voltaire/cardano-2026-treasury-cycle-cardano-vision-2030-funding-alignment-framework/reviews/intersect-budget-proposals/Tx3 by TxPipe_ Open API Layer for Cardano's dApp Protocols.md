## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary purpose is to improve Cardano developer experience and application-layer usability by creating an open, standardized API/interface layer across Cardano dApp protocols. Its core deliverables are protocol onboarding, verified interface definitions, auto-generated SDKs, JSON-RPC endpoints, MCP tooling, agent-ready documentation, editor/agent plugins, and public transaction resolution infrastructure.

This maps most directly to **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the framework’s Developer Experience focus area, which includes open-source incentives for core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure, plus compatibility with common tooling to lower switching costs. The proposal is not primarily about L1 protocol improvements, governance mechanisms, treasury economics, SPO incentives, or market/community campaigns. The claimed ecosystem-growth narrative is plausible but mainly downstream from the technical developer tooling, not a direct and substantial second strategic workstream.

---

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal requests funding “to deliver an open, standardized interface across Cardano's leading on-chain protocols, so any developer or AI agent can discover, integrate, and compose them through a single consistent surface.” This directly supports developer experience and application usability. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that Tx3 produces “documentation, auto-generated SDKs in multiple languages, and live JSON-RPC endpoints” from verified protocol interface definitions. This aligns with Pillar 2’s focus on SDKs, frameworks, infrastructure, and compatibility for builders.  

### Evidence 3

The work package intends to onboard “12 additional Cardano protocols” with verified Tx3 interface definitions, SDKs in Rust, Python, Go, TypeScript, Java, and Swift, and live endpoints exposed through open standards. The target users are developers, integrators, and AI agents building or interacting with Cardano applications. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal’s success metrics include concrete delivery targets: 12 protocols onboarded, 6 SDK languages per protocol, MCP tools for 100% of onboarded protocols, Skills bundles for 100%, 4 editor/agent plugins, public TRP endpoints for mainnet plus 2 testnets, and a 99% uptime target. These are project-output KPIs with explicit targets, though they do not directly set targets for Vision 2030 top-level adoption KPIs such as TVL, MAU, or monthly transactions. 

### Evidence 5

The proposal argues that “a single SDK call replaces weeks of reverse-engineering” and that standard interfaces make composability practical. This reinforces that the main strategic purpose is lowering integration friction and improving developer/application utility rather than direct governance, treasury, or base-layer infrastructure reform.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is the strongest classification because the proposal’s core purpose is to make Cardano dApp protocols easier to discover, integrate, compose, and use through standardized interfaces, SDKs, documentation, RPC endpoints, and agent tooling.

**Infrastructure & Research** was not selected as the primary pillar because the proposal does not focus on L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, core ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, or threat recovery as defined under Pillar 1.

**Community & Ecosystem Growth** was not selected as a secondary pillar because the proposal’s references to ecosystem narrative, market differentiation, and confidence-building are mostly downstream effects of the technical API layer. The proposal includes some community-facing elements, such as contributor onboarding, but these are not substantial enough to justify a direct secondary alignment with Pillar 4 under the classification rules.

### Additional Notes

The proposal does include measurable project targets. However, its direct KPI commitments are mostly delivery and usage-tracking metrics for the Tx3 layer, not explicit outcome targets for the Vision 2030 top-level KPIs such as TVL, monthly transactions, or monthly active users.
