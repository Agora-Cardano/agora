## AI Rationale

The Cardano Tooling DAO proposal’s core objective is to fund and coordinate the development of developer tools and infrastructure to increase Cardano’s usage and utility. The proposal description explicitly states its purpose is to “support teams building the tools and infrastructure that power Cardano’s long-term growth” and to allocate funding to projects that drive “developer usage, product adoption, and broader utility across the network.” These goals directly align with Pillar 2: **Adoption & Utility**, which focuses on “driving widespread utility” of Cardano. In particular, Pillar 2’s developer-experience sub-goals, such as improving SDKs, tooling, and adoption metrics, match this proposal’s focus on expanding tooling, including explorers, APIs, SDKs, and measuring success by Monthly Active Users and Monthly Active Developers.

While this proposal also involves the developer community, Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem, that aspect is secondary. The primary emphasis is on improving ecosystem utility, higher adoption, and use of the platform via better tools, not on marketing or talent pipelines per se. The proposal’s main deliverables, tooling projects and usage metrics, support the network’s utility and adoption, matching Pillar 2’s objectives. Therefore, **Adoption & Utility** is the best primary pillar for this proposal.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

The proposal’s intent is to enhance Cardano’s **utility** by funding developer tools and infrastructure, which maps to Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility. The evidence shows a clear emphasis on increasing usage and adoption via developer tools, which matches Pillar 2’s goal of “driving widespread utility” of the network.

**Other pillars:** Pillar 1: Infrastructure & Research pertains to base-layer protocol improvements, scalability, and security, which this proposal does not address. Pillar 3: Governance deals with governance processes, not relevant here as the main strategic purpose. Pillar 5: Sustainability & Resilience focuses on financial health, which is not the proposal’s focus. Pillar 4: Community & Ecosystem is about developer engagement and talent pipelines; the Tooling DAO does involve developer coordination, but that is a means to an adoption end, not the core purpose. Thus Adoption & Utility best explains the proposal’s main strategic purpose.
