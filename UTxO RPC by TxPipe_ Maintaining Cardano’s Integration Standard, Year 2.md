## AI Rationale

The proposal most directly aligns with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the **Developer Experience** focus area. The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure under Adoption & Utility through A.3 Developer Experience, including open-source incentives, education and migration, and compatibility.

The proposal’s primary purpose is to maintain UTxO RPC as an open-source integration standard, including the protobuf specification, multi-language SDKs, documentation, compatibility with Cardano protocol changes, issue triage, pull request review, and AI-friendly developer resources. These deliverables are aimed primarily at developers and builders integrating with Cardano, not at direct protocol scalability, governance operations, treasury management, SPO incentives, or community engagement.

Although the work has technical infrastructure characteristics, the Vision 2030 framework explicitly classifies developer tooling, SDKs, frameworks, and open-source infrastructure maintenance under **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, not Pillar 1. Therefore, **Infrastructure & Research** is not selected as the Primary Pillar.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The Cardano Vision 2030 framework defines Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility as including **A.3 Developer Experience**, with open-source incentives to “incentivize the maintenance of core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure in line with open-source best practices.”

### Evidence 2

The proposal states that UTxO RPC “defines common methods, data structures, and communication patterns using Protocol Buffers” and provides SDKs in Rust, Go, Node.js, .NET, Haskell, and Python to simplify blockchain integration for developers.

### Evidence 3

The proposal’s stated maintenance scope includes the protobuf specification, official SDKs, dependency updates, performance improvements, bug fixing, documentation, issue triage, review of external contributions, and ecosystem-driven enhancements.

### Evidence 4

The proposal explicitly selects Pillar 2 and explains that UTxO RPC aligns with A.3 Developer Experience because it maintains core SDKs, frameworks, and open-source infrastructure used by Cardano developers.

### Evidence 5

The proposal’s work package objective is to keep UTxO RPC “stable, reliable, and current with every Cardano protocol change” so adopters can depend on it without friction, while also supporting issue response, contribution review, and AI-driven development workflows.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** is selected because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is developer enablement through open-source tooling maintenance, SDK support, integration compatibility, and documentation. This directly corresponds to the Vision 2030 Developer Experience focus area under Pillar 2.

**Infrastructure & Research** is plausible at a surface level because the proposal concerns technical infrastructure and interoperability between node implementations. However, the proposal does not primarily deliver L1 protocol improvements, L2 integration, cross-chain interoperability, ZK capabilities, post-quantum readiness, client diversity, threat detection, or disaster recovery. Its core deliverables are SDK/specification maintenance and developer-facing integration support, which the Vision 2030 framework places under Pillar 2.

No Secondary Pillar is assigned because the proposal does not contain a distinct, substantial second workstream that directly supports another Vision 2030 pillar. Any infrastructure benefit is instrumental to developer experience and adoption, not a separate strategic objective.

### Additional Notes

The proposal includes references to broader ecosystem benefits, such as enabling dApps, services, integrations, and future transaction growth. These are treated as expected downstream effects of improved developer experience, not as separate primary or secondary pillar alignment.
