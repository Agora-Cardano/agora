## AI Rationale

The proposal’s strongest alignment is with **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, specifically the **Developer Experience** focus area. The proposal is primarily about maintaining Dolos as open-source developer and infrastructure tooling that gives builders and operators easier, lower-cost access to Cardano chain data. The Cardano Vision 2030 framework places open-source incentives for core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure under Pillar 2, which directly matches the proposal’s stated purpose.  

A secondary alignment with **Infrastructure & Research** is also justified because the proposal repeatedly frames Dolos as contributing to client diversity, protocol compatibility, and resilient access to chain data. However, this is secondary because the funded work is not primarily a protocol change, core L1 scaling initiative, research program, or base-layer implementation effort. Its main purpose is ongoing maintenance of developer-facing open-source infrastructure.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Dolos is a “lightweight Cardano data node built in Rust” that provides efficient access to chain data without the overhead of traditional full nodes and exposes multiple query interfaces, including Mini-Blockfrost, UTxO-RPC, Mini-Kupo, and an Ouroboros N2C Unix socket. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal explicitly says the maintainer’s work is organized to ensure Dolos remains “usable, reliable, and up to date” as a lightweight, resource-efficient gateway to Cardano chain data. 

### Evidence 3

The proposal identifies its core work as essential maintenance, dependency updates, Cardano protocol compatibility, performance improvements, bug fixing, documentation, issue triage, pull request review, and enhancements driven by ecosystem feedback. 

### Evidence 4

The proposal itself aligns Dolos with Pillar 2 through Developer Experience, specifically the open-source incentives area for maintaining core Cardano SDKs, frameworks, and infrastructure. This matches the Vision 2030 framework’s placement of open-source maintenance under Adoption & Utility.  

### Evidence 5

The proposal also states that Dolos contributes to client diversity as one of a small number of non-Haskell nodes and as a production-grade alternative for teams needing chain data access without the full node stack, supporting a secondary Infrastructure & Research alignment.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

**Adoption & Utility** was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s main strategic purpose is to maintain open-source developer infrastructure that reduces friction for builders and operators accessing Cardano chain data. The proposal’s target users are developers, infrastructure operators, and downstream projects that depend on reliable query and indexing infrastructure.

**Infrastructure & Research** is a plausible alternative because Dolos is technical infrastructure and the proposal references client diversity, protocol compatibility, and resilience. However, under the Cardano Vision 2030 framework, the specific category of open-source SDKs, frameworks, tooling, and infrastructure maintenance is placed under **Pillar 2: Adoption & Utility**, not Pillar 1. Therefore, Pillar 2 best justifies the proposal’s existence if only one pillar can be selected.

### Manual Review Reason

Manual review is required because the proposal could reasonably fit multiple primary pillars. The proposal is formally and directly aligned with Pillar 2 through Developer Experience, but its economic substance and technical content also substantially overlap with Infrastructure & Research due to client diversity, protocol compatibility, and lightweight node infrastructure.

### Additional Notes

The proposal text does not provide a dedicated proposal portal URL. The only explicit project URL provided is the Dolos GitHub repository.
