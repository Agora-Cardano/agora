## AI Rationale

The proposal’s primary strategic purpose is to improve Cardano governance participation, literacy, accessibility, and resistance to capture through structured education for Ada holders, DReps, SPOs, and Constitutional Committee participants. That maps most directly to Pillar 3: Governance, which focuses on governance being “hard to capture, easy to use, and paced,” including DRep/SPO/CC participation, governance accessibility, turnout-aware voting, and treasury seasons. 

The proposal is not primarily about infrastructure, protocol development, DeFi/RWA/payments adoption, treasury tokenomics, or SPO economic incentives. Its deliverables are a Governance Workshop Portal, governance curriculum, monthly sessions, community workshop grants, flagship governance workshops, tracking of governance outcomes, and public impact reporting. These are governance capacity-building mechanisms, not direct technical infrastructure or market adoption activities. 

A Secondary Pillar of Community & Ecosystem Growth is justified because the proposal includes substantial, direct workstreams for community-led workshops, global delivery across five continents, translations, open educational materials, and a pipeline of community governance educators. However, this remains secondary because those activities serve the governance education objective rather than a broader market-centric adoption or developer growth agenda.

## Supporting Evidence

### Evidence 1

The proposal states that Cardano’s on-chain governance depends on informed participation from Ada holders, DReps, SPOs, and the Constitutional Committee, and warns that without structured education the ecosystem risks low participation, concentrated voting power, uninformed voting, and governance capture. 

### Evidence 2

The proposal describes itself as a “focused governance education program” built around governance education infrastructure, community workshop grants, and expert-led flagship workshops. 

### Evidence 3

WP1 aims to launch a public Governance Workshop Portal, curate seven governance workshop packs, translate core materials, deliver monthly online sessions, build impact tracking, and create a playbook so the community can continue the program. 

### Evidence 4

WP2 funds up to ten community-hosted governance workshops through a criteria-based grant program, with required outputs including attendance records, knowledge assessments, outcome reports, and 30- and 60-day on-chain activity tracking. 

### Evidence 5

WP3 proposes expert-led governance workshops across five continents, covering Ada holder participation, DRep education, SPO governance, the Constitutional Committee, the Constitution, and governance tooling.

## Classification Notes

### Primary Classification Justification

Governance was selected as the Primary Pillar because the proposal’s core purpose is to improve informed participation in Cardano governance. Its target users are governance participants: Ada holders, DReps, SPOs, Constitutional Committee participants, workshop hosts, and community educators focused on governance. Its expected outcomes are governance-specific: DRep registrations, delegation activity, voting participation, governance literacy, workshop-based governance onboarding, and measurable on-chain governance engagement.

Community & Ecosystem Growth is a valid Secondary Pillar because the proposal contains substantial global and community-enablement components, including community grants, multilingual materials, regional workshops, and educator pipeline development. However, those components are delivery mechanisms for governance education. They do not override the proposal’s dominant governance purpose.

Infrastructure & Research was not selected because the proposal does not primarily build protocol infrastructure, L1/L2 scalability, cryptographic readiness, client diversity, interoperability, or technical research capabilities.

Adoption & Utility was not selected because the proposal does not primarily target DeFi, RWA, supply chain, payments, enterprise adoption, UX abstraction, developer migration, or Cardano application usage.

Ecosystem Sustainability & Resilience was not selected as a secondary pillar because the proposal’s “sustainability” language refers mainly to making educational resources reusable after the funding period. That is not the same as the Vision 2030 Pillar 5 focus on treasury management, tokenomics, L2-to-L1 value retention, SPO incentives, or base-layer economic resilience.

### Manual Review Reason

Not required.

### Additional Notes

The proposal itself claims alignment with Pillars 3, 4, and 5. Based strictly on the supplied Vision 2030 framework and proposal text, Pillar 3 is clearly dominant, Pillar 4 is substantial but secondary, and Pillar 5 is not sufficiently direct for classification.
